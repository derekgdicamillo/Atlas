## Part 1: General Facebook/Meta Ad Image Trends

### 1.1 The Algorithm Shift: Andromeda Changes Everything

Meta's Andromeda retrieval system, powered by NVIDIA GH200 Grace Hopper Superchip, fundamentally changed how ads are delivered. The system processes 10,000x more data per impression than the previous system and is 100x faster at matching people to ads.

**How Andromeda works:**
- Scans millions of ads and picks ~1,000 candidates per user impression
- Uses computer vision to assign each creative an "Entity ID" based on visual pattern
- Passes top performers to GEM for ranking and Lattice for placement optimization
- Creative quality accounts for roughly 56% of the sales lift from digital advertising (Meta research)
- 70-80% of Meta ad performance is driven by creative strength, not budget (AppsFlyer 2025)

**Entity ID and Creative Similarity:**
- If you upload 30 visually similar ads, Andromeda assigns them the SAME Entity ID
- You get only ONE ticket to advance to the ranking stage, not 30
- Creative Similarity Score above 60% triggers retrieval suppression
- Aim for fewer than 40% shared visual/audio features across active assets
- Similar creatives grouped under same Entity ID raise costs 20-30%

**What creates separate Entity IDs:**
- Different visual angles (UGC vs studio vs text-only)
- Distinct hooks and formats (Reels, carousel, static, story)
- Varied messaging themes (awareness vs education vs conversion)
- Different content creators (each introduces unique visual DNA)
- Different environments, backgrounds, lighting conditions

**Bottom line:** Meta is no longer a media-buying platform. It's a creative discovery engine. Creative diversity is the primary performance lever.

### 1.2 UGC vs Polished Studio vs Lifestyle Photography

**UGC dominates in 2026.** The data is overwhelming:
- UGC delivers 4x higher CTR and ~50% lower CPC than branded ads
- 25% lower CPA and 37% greater engagement vs studio content (skincare brand study, Jan 2025)
- UGC campaigns with Meta's "maximize conversion value" tools: 12% ROAS lift (2026)
- 29% higher conversion rates vs polished studio content
- 79% of consumers say UGC strongly influences purchase decisions
- 84% find word-of-mouth more credible than traditional advertising
- 28% say UGC ads feel more unique; 31% more memorable than branded content

**Why UGC wins:**
- Algorithm advantage: sparks interactions (comments, shares, reactions) that Meta rewards
- Scroll resistance: users are trained to recognize and skip polished ads
- Trust gap: polished creative appears staged and inauthentic
- Feed native: looks like something a friend would post, not a marketing department

**The "ugly ad" phenomenon:**
Raw, native-looking creative often outperforms studio-level production because:
- Raw lighting, handheld framing, lo-fi captions, direct-to-camera delivery feel native
- Meta's algorithm in 2026 is heavily weighted toward engagement velocity
- Ads that don't look like ads break through consumer ad fatigue and skepticism
- The system measures thumb-stop rate, 3-second hold, scroll velocity, and comment intent

**What this means for PV MediSpa:**
- Derek-to-camera content filmed on phone will likely outperform studio shoots
- Clinic walkthrough videos shot casually will feel more authentic
- Patient testimonial videos (with consent) filmed in-clinic are ideal UGC
- Mix in some polished brand content for authority, but lean UGC heavy (70/30 split)

### 1.3 Static Image vs Carousel vs Video

**No single format dominates universally. Funnel stage matters:**

| Format | Best For | Key Stats |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **Carousel** | Multi-benefit messaging, storytelling | 30-50% lower cost per conversion vs single image; up to 10x higher CTR |
| **Static image** | BOF conversion, clear offers | Making a comeback; easier to process quickly; outperforms video at BOF |
| **Video (UGC)** | Awareness, prospecting, engagement | Default format for top performers; longest active lifespans in spy tools |
| **Text-only static** | Pattern interruption, hot take messaging | Trending format; stands out by being visually different |

**Carousel specifics:**
- Currently one of the hottest formats on Meta
- Test whether video cards outperform static within carousels
- Great for multi-specialty clinics to showcase different services/benefits
- Effective for "everyday wins" storytelling (better sleep, more energy, clothes fit better)

**Static image comeback:**
- Static images with clear offer and CTA often outperform video at bottom of funnel
- Simple product photos or text-on-image designs outperforming video in many accounts
- Hypothesis: video can be overwhelming; static images are easier to process quickly
- Best for direct-response with specific offers

**Key principle:** Don't pick one format. Feed Andromeda diversity. Run one testimonial, one high-production brand story, one technical explainer, and one text-only static. Five identical UGC videos with slight hook changes = one Entity ID.

### 1.4 Aspect Ratios and Placement Strategy

**90% of Meta inventory in 2026 is vertical.** Design accordingly:

| Placement | Optimal Ratio | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| Reels | 9:16 | 41% higher engagement vs cropped formats |
| Stories | 9:16 | Strong retargeting performance |
| Feed | 4:5 or 1:1 | Still drives majority of conversions |
| Right column | 1:1 | Lower volume, supplement only |

**CPM comparison by placement:**
- Reels: $10-12 CPM (15-25% lower than Feed)
- Feed: ~$16 CPM (highest conversion volume)
- Stories: Lower CPMs, good for retargeting

**Placement strategy:**
- Feed (55-65% of impressions) is still the workhorse for conversions
- Stories (30-40%) perform well for retargeting at lower CPMs
- Reels (10-20%) growing fast, best for prospecting
- Use Flexible Ad Format to let Meta auto-optimize placement mix
- Always optimize for cost per result, not cost per impression (a $4 CPM with 50% higher CPA costs more than $10 CPM at baseline CPA)

### 1.5 Color Psychology for Healthcare Ads

**Colors that work for healthcare/weight loss:**

| Color | Effect | Use Case |
|-------|--------|----------|
| **Blue** | Trust, calm, reliability | Brand foundation, backgrounds, authority signals |
| **Green** | Health, growth, vitality | Wellness messaging, healthy lifestyle content |
| **Teal** | Modern health, clean energy | Standout accent on dark or white backgrounds |
| **Orange** | Warmth, action, urgency | CTA buttons, special offers, energy-related messaging |
| **White** | Clean, clinical, professional | Backgrounds for clinical authority |
| **Navy** | Premium, sophisticated | Dark backgrounds for premium positioning |

**Contrast principles:**
- Colors that contrast with Facebook's blue/white interface work best (orange, warm tones)
- High contrast (bright colors against dark backgrounds) stops the scroll
- Adding a colored border around an ad image can double CTR
- Text: black on light OR white on dark. Never soft mid-grey blends
- Primary CTA needs 60%+ contrast against background

**Warm vs Cool temperature:**
- Warm colors (red, orange, yellow) stimulate emotional responses, drive urgency and action
- Cool colors (blue, green, purple) create trust, professionalism, pleasantness
- For weight loss ads: warm accents (orange CTA buttons, energy imagery) on cool foundations (blue/teal brand colors)
- Warm colors generate higher arousal scores; cool colors generate higher pleasantness scores
- Match temperature to goal: warm for action-driving CTAs, cool for trust-building brand content

**PV MediSpa application:** Use teal/blue foundation for clinical trust, warm orange accents for CTAs and energy messaging. Contrast against white or navy backgrounds.

### 1.6 Text on Images

**Current rules:**
- Meta's strict 20% text rule has been eliminated (no longer enforced)
- However, images with less than 20% text still perform better (lower costs, better delivery)
- Less overlay text = better user experience = better algorithmic treatment

**Best practices:**
- One line of text maximum ("30% Off," "Free Consultation," "Lose Weight Without Dieting")
- Heading font: under 42px; body text: around 24px
- Sans-serif fonts for readability at mobile sizes
- Minimum 40pt font for text overlays (for readability on mobile)
- Bold, legible, high-contrast against background
- Keep headlines under 100 characters for full visibility across all placements

**What works:**
- Short, punchy value props as overlays
- Question hooks as text overlays
- Numbers and statistics as text (stop the scroll)
- Minimal text + strong visual = best combination

### 1.7 Image Composition & Visual Hierarchy

**Fundamentals that drive scroll-stopping:**

**Faces:**
- Human faces are highly effective for stopping scrolls (brain locks onto eyes and expressions in <1 second)
- Close-up photos outperform wide group shots
- One face performs better than a crowd
- Direct eye contact triggers social attention (we're hardwired to notice eyes looking at us)
- If person looks toward headline or CTA, viewer follows that gaze (directional cue)

**Composition rules:**
- Rule of thirds: place key elements at intersection points for more tension and interest
- One primary message, one visual focal point, one clear CTA per ad
- Product-in-use outperforms product-on-white-background
- Larger elements naturally draw more attention
- Use selective focus (sharp foreground, blurred background) to draw eye to subject
- Leave breathing room around focal points
- Lead with product/benefit in upper 50% of frame

**Clean vs busy:**
- Clean compositions outperform busy ones for healthcare
- Minimal elements = faster cognitive processing = better scroll-stopping
- Too much visual information causes scroll-through

**Bright vs dark backgrounds:**
- High contrast either way works. The key is CONTRAST, not specific brightness
- Bright teal, rich orange, deep navy all perform when used with clear contrast
- Dark backgrounds create urgency and premium feel
- Light backgrounds feel clean and clinical

### 1.8 Scroll-Stopping Techniques

The question isn't "Static or video?" but "Does this hook stop the scroll in less than 2 seconds?"

**Visual hooks that stop the scroll:**
1. **Pattern interruption** - Something visually unexpected in the feed
2. **Problem agitation** - Visual that immediately connects to a pain point
3. **Identity-based callout** - Visual that makes the viewer say "that's me"
4. **Contrarian visual** - Image that contradicts expectations
5. **Result-first** - Show the end result immediately

**What Meta measures:**
- Thumb-stop rate (did they pause?)
- 3-second hold (did they stay?)
- Scroll velocity change (did they slow down?)
- Comment intent (did they want to engage?)
- Hook rate = 3s views / impressions

**Overproduced ads often lose to raw, clear ones** because Meta rewards speed of feedback, not perfection.

### 1.9 Advantage+ Creative Optimization

**Key features:**
- Automatically adjusts visuals and text to match audience preferences
- Standard enhancements result in ~4% lower cost-per-result on average
- Works best with quality creative input (it enhances, doesn't create from scratch)

**Best practices:**
- Start with sharp, eye-catching visuals and well-crafted primary text
- Use Flexible Ad Format to let the system mix variations across placements
- Deploy Meta Pixel + Conversions API together for accurate attribution
- Design mobile-first: 4:5 for feed, 9:16 for Stories/Reels
- Start broader targeting and let Advantage+ optimize

**What Advantage+ favors:**
- Authentic, native content (vertical, raw, social-native)
- Creative that feels like something a friend would post on their story
- Multiple distinct creative concepts, not variations of one concept

### 1.10 Creative Testing & Refresh Cadence

**Top performers test 20-50 new creatives per week** and ship 8-12 distinct angles (not variations of the same idea).

**Testing framework:**
- Launch 5-10 new ads per week
- Run untouched for 7 days
- Eliminate underperformers; scale winners
- Feed learnings into next creative cycle

**Refresh frequency:**
- Refresh every 2-4 weeks on Meta (faster-moving than most platforms)
- Add at least one fresh asset every 1-2 weeks
- Plan standing rotation of 3-5 concepts
- Replace any creative when frequency exceeds 4.0 or CTR declines 20%+ over two weeks
- Cold audience frequency above 2.5 = fatigue warning
- Retargeting frequency above 5.0 = fatigue warning

**Ad fatigue in 2026:**
- Sets in faster than ever (audiences scroll quicker, Meta recycles impressions aggressively)
- What once lasted weeks now burns out in days
- If you run the same static image for 2+ weeks, CPM will spike
- Creative volume planning is foundational, not optional

**Budget scaling:**
- Increase budgets 20-30% weekly only if CAC remains stable
- Scale concepts, not individual ads
- Refresh winners every 30-60 days before fatigue sets in
- Monitor CPMr (cost per 1,000 reach) as primary fatigue indicator. Rising CPMr = refresh needed

**For PV MediSpa (smaller budget):**
- Minimum: 3-5 genuinely different ad concepts per month
- Ideal: refresh creative monthly with new angles
- Mix formats: 1 UGC video, 1 static with offer, 1 carousel, 1 text-only, 1 lifestyle

## Part 2: Weight Loss & GLP-1 Specific Ad Creative

### 2.1 The Regulatory Landscape

**Meta compliance rules for weight loss ads:**
- NO before-and-after images (idealized results)
- NO body-shaming imagery or language
- NO tape measure around a person's body
- NO misleading claims about weight loss results
- NO content generating negative self-perception
- NO referring to personal attributes (weight, health conditions, appearance)
- Target 18+ only
- Cannot directly reference personal health status ("Are you overweight?")
- 35 state attorneys general pressured Meta to enforce stricter policies on GLP-1 ads (Dec 2025)

**LegitScript certification:**
- Required for Google Ads (prescription drug names in ad text)
- Recommended for Meta (unlocks advertising permissions, builds trust)
- Prescription drug names (semaglutide, tirzepatide) can only appear if certified
- Meta is increasingly scrutinizing GLP-1 ad content post-AG pressure

**Compliant alternatives to drug names in ad copy:**
- "Doctor-Supervised Medical Weight Loss Program"
- "Clinically Proven Weight Loss Treatments"
- "Science-Backed Weight Loss for Long-Term Results"
- ~~"FDA-Approved Medical Weight Loss"~~ DO NOT USE. Say "Provider-Supervised Medical Weight Loss" instead.
- "Prescription Weight Loss Medication" (generic reference)

### 2.2 Compliant Visual Alternatives to Before/After

Since before/after images are prohibited, these alternatives work:

**1. Everyday Wins Carousel**
- Carousel of lifestyle improvements: better sleep, more energy, clothes fitting better, playing with kids
- Focus shifts from weight loss to overall well-being
- Compliant because it doesn't show body transformation

**2. Patient Journey Timeline**
- Carousel showing milestones: "Week 1: First consultation," "Month 2: Energy through the roof," "Month 6: Running my first 5K"
- Text-based or lifestyle imagery at each stage
- Studies show 83% increase in engagement with journey-style content

**3. Social Proof / Testimonial Cards**
- Quote cards with patient testimonials (with consent)
- Text overlay on lifestyle imagery
- "I finally have energy to keep up with my kids" (positive framing, not weight-focused)

**4. Provider Authority Content**
- Derek in clinic setting, explaining the science
- Builds E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness)
- Clinical setting as trust signal without showing patient transformation

**5. Educational/Infographic Style**
- "5 Signs Your Metabolism Needs Medical Support"
- Data visualization of how the program works
- Educational content that positions as authority

**6. Lifestyle Transformation (No Body Focus)**
- Person hiking, cooking healthy meals, sleeping well, being active
- Focus on LIFE transformation, not BODY transformation
- Shows the outcome without showing the physical change

### 2.3 What Telehealth Competitors Are Doing

**Hims & Hers (most effective weight loss advertiser 2025):**
- Ads 1,810% more effective than category average
- Uses the Everyman archetype (speaking to common experience)
- Combined with Rebel archetype (challenging the system)
- Super Bowl ad: confrontational tone, "The system wasn't built to help us"
- Emphasizes personalized prescriptions, affordable, doctor-trusted
- Visual strategy: movement-based, societal commentary, anti-establishment
- Cacti imagery for sensitive topics (making uncomfortable discussions accessible)

**Weight Watchers ("Let's Get Clinical"):**
- 573% higher impact than category average
- Evolved messaging from diet culture to clinical GLP-1 access
- Positioning: established brand credibility + modern medical approach

**Ro (Body Program):**
- Targeted TV campaigns resonating with women 35+
- 50% weekly increase in new patients within 3 months
- Shifting toward TV/streaming from Meta due to compliance pressure

**Common patterns across telehealth competitors:**
- Professional consultations and online support messaging
- "Virtual physician consultation and lab work included"
- "Our team of clinicians will create a personalized plan just for you"
- Celebrity endorsements and influencer partnerships
- Convenience-first messaging
- Emphasis on licensed providers and provider-supervised programs (NEVER say "FDA-approved" in ad copy)

**Problematic patterns to avoid (getting scrutinized):**

## Part 3: Creative Playbook for PV MediSpa

### 3.1 Core Visual Strategy

**Brand visual identity for ads:**
- Primary: Teal/blue foundation (clinical trust)
- Accent: Warm orange (action, energy, CTAs)
- Backgrounds: Clean white (clinical) or deep navy (premium)
- Typography: Sans-serif, bold, high contrast
- Photo style: 70% UGC/authentic, 30% polished brand

**Key creative principles:**
1. Derek's face is the #1 creative asset (provider-as-brand)
2. Authenticity over polish (phone-shot > studio-shot)
3. Clinic environment as trust signal (real place, real care)
4. Diversity of concepts, not variations (feed Andromeda)
5. Mobile-first, vertical-first (9:16 for Reels/Stories, 4:5 for Feed)

### 3.2 Creative Playbook by Ad Concept

#### Concept 1: Transformation Story (without before/after)

**Visual direction:**
- Carousel of lifestyle improvements (NOT body comparison)
- Card 1: Person looking tired/frustrated (relatable problem)
- Card 2: Derek in clinic, reviewing data on screen (clinical authority)
- Card 3: Patient enjoying outdoor activity/hiking (aspirational lifestyle)
- Card 4: Body comp scale readout (data, not body image)
- Card 5: "Free consultation" CTA with clinic photo

**Image specifications:**
- Format: Carousel (5 cards), 4:5 ratio for feed
- Style: Mix of UGC (cards 1, 3) and clinical (cards 2, 4)
- Color: Teal accents on clean backgrounds
- Text overlay: Minimal, one line per card
- Entity diversity: Each card should be visually distinct (different settings, lighting, subjects)

**Alternative static versions:**
- Single image: Active lifestyle moment with text "This is what medical weight loss actually looks like"
- Single image: Derek in clinic with body comp data visible, text "Your body tells a story. Let's read it together."

#### Concept 2: Hidden Blockers (Education-First)

**Visual direction:**
- Infographic-style static or carousel
- "5 metabolic blockers keeping you stuck" or "Why your diet isn't working (it's not your fault)"
- Clean, educational aesthetic with numbered list
- Each blocker visualized simply (metabolism icon, hormone chart, stress symbol)

**Image specifications:**
- Format: Static (1:1 or 4:5) or carousel (one blocker per card)
- Style: Clean infographic with medical aesthetic
- Color: Navy background, white/teal text, orange accent numbers
- Text: Short labels, NOT wall of text (stay under 20% coverage)
- Derek headshot in corner for authority signal

**Alternative UGC version:**
- Derek direct-to-camera: "Here's what nobody tells you about weight loss..." (video thumbnail with text hook)
- Phone-shot, natural lighting, in clinic
- This generates a unique Entity ID vs the infographic version

#### Concept 3: Body Comp Truth (Data-Driven)

**Visual direction:**
- Body comp scale prominently featured
- Data visualization showing what the scale measures (lean mass, body fat, water)
- "The scale in your bathroom is lying to you" hook
- Educational content about body composition vs weight

**Image specifications:**
- Format: Static (4:5) with body comp scale photo or data mockup
- Style: Clean, clinical, data-forward
- Color: White/light background, teal data visualizations, bold black text
- Photo: Real body comp scale in PV MediSpa clinic (not stock photo)
- Text overlay: One punchy line + small PV MediSpa logo

**Alternative versions:**
- Carousel: Each card explains one metric (lean mass, body fat %, visceral fat, etc.)
- Video thumbnail: Derek holding up body comp printout, looking at camera
- Text-only static: Just the hook line on a clean background with logo

#### Concept 4: Five Pillars Framework (Authority/Education)

**Visual direction:**
- Branded infographic showing the 5 pillars
- Visual hierarchy: pillar names large, brief descriptions smaller
- Framework visualization (pentagon, pillars, building blocks)
- Position PV MediSpa as the systematic, comprehensive approach

**Image specifications:**
- Format: Carousel (one pillar per card + intro + CTA) or static infographic
- Style: Polished brand content (this is the 30% polished allocation)
- Color: Each pillar gets a distinct accent color within the teal/orange palette
- Icons: Simple, modern icons for each pillar
- Text: Pillar names + one-line descriptions

**Carousel structure:**
1. "Weight loss isn't one thing. It's five." (hook)
2. Precision Weight Science (body comp, tracking)
3. Nourishing Health (Fuel Code, real food)
4. Dynamic Movement (strength-first approach)
5. Mindful Wellness (stress, sleep, cortisol)
6. Functional Wellness (inflammation, gut health)
7. "Get your personalized 5-pillar plan. Free consultation." (CTA)

#### Concept 5: No Contract / No Gimmicks (Trust/Differentiation)

**Visual direction:**
- Anti-industry positioning (like Hims but local and personal)
- "No contracts. No shakes. No BS." messaging
- Derek in casual clinic setting, approachable and direct
- Clean, bold text-forward creative

**Image specifications:**
- Format: Static (4:5) text-heavy design or UGC video thumbnail
- Style: Bold, contrarian, pattern-interrupting
- Color: Dark background (navy or black) with white bold text, orange accent
- Photo: Derek's face, genuine expression, NOT corporate headshot
- Text: The hook IS the creative ("No Contract. No Shakes. No 6 AM Bootcamp.")

**Alternative versions:**
- Text-only static: Bold white text on dark background, no photos (trending format, creates unique Entity ID)
- Comparison table: "Our Program vs. The Rest" (not naming competitors)
- UGC-style video: Derek walking through clinic, saying "Here's what we DON'T do..."

### 3.4 What's Working vs What's Not

**WORKING in 2026:**
- UGC-style content (phone-shot, authentic, native feel)
- Provider-as-brand (Derek's face builds trust telehealth can't match)
- Carousel ads (30-50% lower CPA, great for storytelling)
- Text-only static ads (pattern interruption, unique Entity IDs)
- Lifestyle transformation imagery (everyday wins, not body shots)
- Educational/authority content (positions as expert, not salesperson)
- Contrarian hooks ("Everything you know about weight loss is wrong")
- Data-forward content (body comp, metabolic markers, science-backed)
- Warm + authentic aesthetic over cold + corporate
- Direct-to-camera talking head format
- Short, punchy text overlays (one line max)
- High contrast colors against feed backgrounds

**NOT WORKING in 2026:**
- Polished studio photography that looks like an ad
- Before/after body images (prohibited AND feels dated)
- Stock photos of models in medical settings
- Generic "lose weight fast" messaging
- Multiple variations of the same visual concept (Andromeda penalizes)
- Running the same creative for 2+ weeks without refresh
- Busy, cluttered compositions with too much text
- Corporate headshot-style provider imagery
- Low contrast colors that blend into the feed
- Lifestyle photos that feel staged or aspirational rather than real
- Generic stock-photo testimonials
- Body-shaming or negative self-perception language
- Drug name mentions without LegitScript certification

### 3.8 Andromeda Optimization Checklist

Before launching any new creative batch, verify:

- [ ] **Visual DNA diversity**: Different backgrounds, subjects, lighting, environments?
- [ ] **Hook variety**: Different emotional angles (problem, aspiration, education, contrarian)?
- [ ] **Funnel coverage**: Something for awareness AND consideration AND conversion?
- [ ] **Mobile-first**: All creative designed for mobile viewing (4:5 feed, 9:16 Stories/Reels)?
- [ ] **Compliance**: No before/after, no drug names, no body-shaming, 18+ targeting?
