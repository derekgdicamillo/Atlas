# CEU/CME/CNE Accreditation Research: GLP-1 Weight Loss Course

**Prepared for:** Derek DiCamillo, FNP / The Medical Aesthetics Association (TMAA)
**Date:** 2026-04-08

---

## Executive Summary

TMAA has **four realistic paths** to offering accredited CE for a GLP-1 weight loss course, ranked by practicality:

1. **Joint providership with an accredited partner** (recommended) — $3,000–$10,000 per course, live in weeks
2. **AANP individual activity accreditation** — submit the course directly for NP CE credit approval
3. **ANCC provider accreditation** — ~$8,000 first year, covers all nursing (RN + NP), 6–12 month setup
4. **Platform partnership** — submit content to Nursing CE Central or pitch to Medbridge for distribution + accreditation

Full organizational accreditation (ACCME, Joint Accreditation) is **not recommended** at this stage due to cost ($27,000–$40,000+/year) and complexity.

---

## 1. Physician CME — ACCME

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Accrediting body** | ACCME (Accreditation Council for Continuing Medical Education) |
| **Covers** | MD, DO |
| **What's accredited** | The *organization*, not individual courses |
| **Initial accreditation fee** | ~$4,500 |
| **Annual fees** | $2,500–$10,000/year depending on tier and whether going through state/regional accreditor |
| **Timeline** | 6–10 months from application to decision |
| **Requirements** | Must have planned, implemented, and evaluated ≥2 CME activities in prior 24 months using ACCME standards; Self-Study Report; Accreditation Interview |
| **Tiers** | Provisional (2-year), Accreditation (4-year), Accreditation with Commendation (6-year, requires meeting 8 additional criteria) |

### Feasibility for TMAA
**Not recommended as direct path.** ACCME accreditation requires existing CME programming history and significant compliance infrastructure. The practical path for physician CME is **joint providership** — partnering with an already-accredited provider (see Section 5).

## 2. Nurse Practitioner CE — AANP

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Accrediting body** | AANP (American Association of Nurse Practitioners) |
| **Covers** | NPs specifically |
| **NP CE requirements** | 100 contact hours per 5-year cycle (≥25 in pharmacology) via AANPCB |
| **Accepted accreditors for NPs** | ANCC, AANP, ACCME, state nursing boards |

### Two Pathways

**Individual Activity Accreditation (Recommended for TMAA):**
- Submit the GLP-1 course as a single activity for AANP review
- Must submit 14–90 business days before the activity
- NPO (NP Organization) member discounts available
- Fastest path to NP-specific CE credit

**Approved Provider Status:**
- As of January 2026: **new applications by invitation only**
- Requires ≥3 completed AANP accreditation applications with clean track record
- Not viable for first-time applicants

### Feasibility for TMAA
**Individual activity accreditation is achievable.** This is one of the most accessible paths. Submit the GLP-1 course directly to AANP for review. Cost is per-activity (fee schedule available from AANP, NPO members get discounts).

## 3. Registered Nurse CE — ANCC

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Accrediting body** | ANCC (American Nurses Credentialing Center, part of ANA) |
| **Covers** | All nursing roles — RN, NP, CNS, CNM, and nursing specialties |
| **Is RN CE separate from NP CE?** | No. ANCC accreditation covers all nursing. Credits count for both RN license renewal and NP certification renewal. |
| **Can same course be dual-accredited?** | Yes — ANCC CE is accepted for both RN and NP. No separate accreditation needed. |
| **New applicant fee** | $4,950 |
| **Annual fee** | $2,000–$3,100/year (depends on org type; TMAA likely ~$3,100 as "Other") |
| **Timeline** | 6+ months (must implement ANCC criteria for ≥6 months before applying, then 2–6 month review) |
| **Requirements** | ≥3 professional development activities per ANCC standards; nurse planner on staff; self-assessment + self-study; virtual appraisal visit |
| **Accreditation terms** | First-time: up to 2 years; Renewal: up to 4 years |
| **Total first-year cost** | ~$8,050 ($4,950 application + $3,100 annual) |

### Feasibility for TMAA
**Realistic medium-term goal.** More accessible than ACCME or Joint Accreditation. If TMAA plans to offer ongoing CE programming (not just one course), ANCC provider accreditation is a solid investment. Covers all nursing roles under one umbrella.

## 4. Joint Accreditation (Interprofessional)

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Accrediting body** | Joint Accreditation for Interprofessional Continuing Education |
| **Co-founded by** | ACCME + ACPE (pharmacy) + ANCC (nursing); 11 total accrediting bodies |
| **Covers** | MD, DO, NP, RN, PA, PharmD, Dentists, Psychologists, Social Workers, Dietitians, Athletic Trainers, Optometrists |
| **Eligibility review** | $2,500 |
| **Initial application** | $27,000 |
| **Annual fee (smallest tier)** | $26,500/year |
| **Application cycles** | June 1 and October 1 |
| **Requirements** | Meet 12 core interprofessional CE criteria + Standards for Integrity and Independence; demonstrate interprofessional education design |

### Feasibility for TMAA
**Not recommended.** Initial cost of $29,500 (eligibility + application) plus $26,500+/year is prohibitive for a small association. Designed for large health systems, academic medical centers, and major medical education companies.

## 5. Joint Providership Partnerships (Recommended Path)

**What it is:** Partner with an already-accredited provider. They handle compliance, accreditation, and reporting. TMAA develops the content and delivers the education. The accredited partner's name appears alongside TMAA's.

### Accredited Partners That Offer Joint Providership

| Partner | Accreditations | Notable Features |
|---------|---------------|------------------|
| **PIM (Postgraduate Institute for Medicine)** | Jointly accredited (multi-profession) | 2,500+ activities/year since 1979; eLearning platform (CME University); ACCME Innovation Partner |
| **Amedco, LLC** | Jointly accredited (multi-profession) | Supports enduring materials, live courses, journal-based CE, blended learning |
| **Global Education Group** | CME + multi-profession | 15+ years; also helps secure pharma/device educational grants |
| **CME Outfitters** | CME + multi-profession | No per-participant fee; custom proposals; JointProvidership@CMEOutfitters.com |
| **CME Consultants** | CME | Another option for joint providership services |

### Typical Costs

| Fee Type | Range |
|----------|-------|
| Administrative fee per activity | $150–$750 |
| Per-credit-hour fee | $50–$300 |
| Tiered by total credits (example) | $3,000 (0–5 credits) to $9,000 (21+ credits) |
| **Realistic budget for one GLP-1 course** | **$3,000–$10,000** |

### How It Works
1. TMAA contacts accredited partner (e.g., PIM or Amedco)
2. Partner reviews proposed content for compliance with accreditation standards
3. TMAA develops content; partner ensures it meets Standards for Integrity and Independence (no commercial bias)
4. Partner handles credit designation, certificate issuance, reporting to accrediting bodies
5. Course is offered as "Jointly provided by [Partner] and TMAA"
6. TMAA pays a fee; keeps course revenue

### Why This Is the Best Path
- **Speed:** Live in weeks, not months/years
- **Cost:** $3,000–$10,000 vs. $27,000+ for own accreditation
- **Multi-profession coverage:** Joint providership through a jointly accredited partner can cover MD, DO, NP, RN, PA all at once
- **No accreditation infrastructure needed:** Partner handles compliance
- **Scalable:** Add more courses later at similar per-course cost

## 6. Online CE Platform Partnerships

| Platform | Accepts Third-Party Content? | Model | Notes |
|----------|------------------------------|-------|-------|
| **Nursing CE Central** | **Yes — open submission** | Flat fee per course to authors | Any nurse with active license can apply; submit topic → approval → write within 21 days → reviewed in 14 days → paid within 5 business days |
| **Medbridge** | **Yes — by pitch** | Undisclosed compensation | Selective; emphasizes "very best instructors"; pitch via help center form; 2,900+ courses, 50+ new monthly |
| **NetCE** | No public program | Internal development | Major accredited provider; may have author programs through parent TRC Healthcare |
| **Relias** | No public program | Enterprise B2B | Sells to healthcare organizations; ANCC-accredited; no individual content creator path |
| **Elite Learning** | No public program | — | Nursing CEUs online but no author portal |
| **Nurse.com** | No public program | — | No third-party content creator program found |
| **CE4Nurses** | No public program | — | Smaller platform |

### Feasibility for TMAA
The CE platform space is **not built like Udemy** — most platforms develop content in-house or commission from known experts. **Nursing CE Central** is the notable exception with open submissions. **Medbridge** is worth a pitch given Derek's clinical expertise.

However, the platform route means TMAA loses branding control and the course lives on someone else's platform. For building TMAA's brand as an education provider, **joint providership** (Section 5) is superior because TMAA retains the course and the student relationship.

## Recommended Action Plan for TMAA

### Phase 1 — Immediate (Next 2–4 Weeks)
1. **Contact PIM and Amedco** for joint providership quotes. Ask specifically about:
   - Cost for a single online enduring GLP-1 weight loss course (likely 3–10 CE credits)
   - Which professions they can accredit under (MD, DO, NP, RN, PA)
   - Their platform capabilities (or if TMAA needs to host)
   - Timeline from contract to live course
2. **Submit to AANP** for individual NP CE activity accreditation as a parallel track (in case joint providership takes longer)

### Phase 2 — Medium-Term (3–6 Months)
3. If TMAA plans **ongoing CE programming** (not just one course), pursue **ANCC provider accreditation** for nursing CE. This gives TMAA the ability to self-accredit all future nursing CE without paying per-course joint providership fees.

### Phase 3 — Long-Term (12+ Months)
4. Once TMAA has a catalog of 3+ CE courses and established programming, consider **ACCME accreditation** (directly or through a state/regional accreditor) to add physician CME independently.
5. Joint Accreditation becomes viable only if TMAA scales to dozens of activities and wants to cover all professions under one umbrella.
