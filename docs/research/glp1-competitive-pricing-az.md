## PART 4 — WHERE PV MEDISPA SHOULD SIT + VALUE GAPS

### The pricing reality
- **The compounded-med floor has collapsed to ~$150–$300/mo** (Ro $149, Hims sema $199, Mochi $178), and **brand direct is now $199–$449/mo** (Wegovy/Zepbound self-pay). A price-shopping patient sees "$199 Wegovy" and "$149 Ro" before they ever call a local clinic.
- **PV's $465 all-inclusive is NOT competing on med price — and shouldn't.** On medication alone it looks expensive vs. telehealth. It only wins when the **bundle** is the comparison.

### The value gaps competitors leave wide open (PV's moat)
1. **No in-person provider relationship** — every telehealth player is async or video-only. A patient who wants a real FNP who knows them, in their town, has zero telehealth substitute. This is PV's single biggest differentiator.
2. **No real labs** — telehealth does minimal/self-reported labs. PV's **advanced lab panel** (baseline + monitoring) is a genuine clinical and safety advantage. Most local clinics don't bundle labs either.
3. **No body-composition tracking** — none of these bundle a body-comp **scale**. PV measures actual fat/lean change, not just scale weight. This reframes the whole "are you losing the right weight?" conversation. (Per PV positioning: scale-based body comp, not InBody/DEXA — keep messaging to what's on hand.)
4. **No coaching depth** — telehealth "coaching" is app nudges. Noom/Mochi are closest but it's still app-based. PV's **1:1 weight-loss coach + 5 Pillars functional framework** is a structured human program, not a chatbot.
5. **No side-effect/adjunct management in person** — PV's **weekly Lipo-B injections** and in-clinic side-effect management (nausea, hydration, electrolytes) are things you cannot get shipped.
6. **Transparent flat fee** — most locals hide price behind a consult. PV publishing **$465 all-in, no surprise bills, cancel anytime** is itself a trust/conversion advantage over consult-gated competitors.

**Conclusion:** PV should sit at a **premium-but-justified $400–$500/mo all-inclusive**, explicitly positioned against the *total* cost of a telehealth stack (membership + med + separate labs + no coaching + no in-person care), NOT against a bare $149 med price. The flat fee is correct; the *framing* is the lever.

## PART 5 — THREE CONCRETE RECOMMENDATIONS

### 1. Reframe the price as a "total cost of care" comparison, not a med price
Patients anchor on $149–$299. Beat that anchor by showing the **real stacked cost** of the alternative. Put a side-by-side on the landing page:
> **Telehealth "$199/mo" actually costs:** $199 med + $39–$149 membership + ~$100+/mo if you add labs + $0 coaching + $0 in-person care + $0 body-comp tracking = **$340–$450/mo for less.**
> **PV MediSpa: $465 flat. Labs, your own provider in Prescott Valley, weekly Lipo-B, a real coach, and body-comp tracking included. No surprise bills.**
This makes $465 read as the *value buy*, not the expensive option.

### 2. Add a lower-priced entry tier to stop losing price-shoppers at the door
PV currently has one price point ($465). The market has a hard $199–$299 expectation. Introduce a **stripped "Essentials" tier (~$299/mo)** = medication + monthly provider visit + labs, *without* the weekly Lipo-B, dedicated coach, or full 5-Pillars program. Use it as the **competitive-match offer** to win the cost-conscious lead, then upsell to the $465 "Full Program" on results and relationship. Keeps PV in the consideration set against Hims/Ro/brand-direct while protecting the premium tier's margin. (Validate margin against Hallandale COGS before launch.)

### 3. Kill the $199 kickstart friction with a first-month offer that matches telehealth intro pricing
Telehealth wins the first click with "$39 first month / $49 first month." PV's **$199 kickstart on top of $465** is the highest entry barrier in the comparison set. Replace with a **"First month $199 all-in" intro** (waive or roll the kickstart into month one), so the patient's first commitment is $199 — matching the psychological entry point of Wegovy direct ($199) and Hims sema ($199) — then $465/mo thereafter. Lowers the activation barrier without discounting the ongoing program. Pair with the existing free-consult funnel.
