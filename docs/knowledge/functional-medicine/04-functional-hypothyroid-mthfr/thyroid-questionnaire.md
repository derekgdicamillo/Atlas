# Functional Hypothyroid & MTHFR — Thyroid Symptom Questionnaire (JHHC Intake Form)

Reproduction of the Joyful Heart Health Care "Thyroid Symptom Questionnaire" patient intake form, usable as-is for patient intake or symptom tracking over time.

## Source

"thyroid questionnaire JHHC.pdf," Joyful Heart Health Care (Jenni Gallagher, APRN), distributed as part of the Spring 2026 mentorship MTHFR/hypothyroidism video session materials. Contact on the original form: jgallagherfnp@gmail.com.

## Purpose (as stated on the form)

"This questionnaire helps determine the function of thyroid hormone in your body. It does not matter if you have had lab tests that say your thyroid is OK or that you may be taking prescription thyroid medication, you still may have problems with metabolism that directly relates to how thyroid hormone is functioning in your body."

## Scoring Instructions (verbatim)

Enter the following score for each item:
- **10** — If this is a noticeable issue or significant problem
- **5** — If this is a problem, but not a major issue
- **2** — If this happens every now and then, but you don't notice it too much
- **0** — If you seldom or never have this issue

**Total all points at the bottom right of the chart.**

## Supplementary Symptom List (from the mentorship PPT — "Collect Adequate S/S from Patient")

The mentorship PPT gives a slightly expanded/overlapping symptom-collection checklist for clinical intake conversation (not identical in wording to the JHHC form above, reproduced separately per source):

- Mood swings
- Menses mood changes
- Depression symptoms
- Irritability, "on-edge" all the time
- Fatigue, waking tired, poor sleep, daytime nap
- Use of caffeine to stay alert
- Thinning hair, eyebrows, lashes
- Dry, itchy skin, rough/scaly elbows
- Frequent early AM headache, brain fog, loss of focus
- Neck feels swollen/full/knotty
- GI problems, especially IBS, constipation
- Muscle aches, pain, weakness, cramping
- Numbness in hands/feet or plantar fasciitis, carpal tunnel syndrome — frozen shoulder

## Evidence Quality Note

This is a **practitioner-authored screening instrument** (Joyful Heart Health Care), not a validated/psychometrically-tested clinical scale (unlike, e.g., the Billewicz index or Zulewski clinical score for hypothyroidism). Use it as a structured symptom-tracking and patient-education tool alongside — not instead of — the lab panel and targets in [thyroid-management.md](thyroid-management.md). No published cutoff score for "likely hypothyroid" is given on the source form; total points are for tracking symptom burden and trend over time (e.g., pre-/post-treatment comparison) rather than a validated diagnostic threshold. **[source unclear — no scoring interpretation guide was included in the source PDF]**
