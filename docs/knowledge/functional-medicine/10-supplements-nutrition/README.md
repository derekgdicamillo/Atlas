# Supplements & Nutrition — Overview

Navigation and scope for the Supplements & Nutrition Reference topic, built from Derek's Spring 2026 functional-medicine mentorship (Week 5, Jenni Gallagher APRN / Joyful Heart Institute) plus every named supplement referenced across the rest of the course.

## Scope

This topic is a **cross-cutting supplement/nutrition lookup layer** for the functional-medicine knowledge base — not a new clinical protocol in its own right. Individual condition-specific topics (iron/ferritin, thyroid, metabolic syndrome, sleep, bone health, gut health, inflammation) each teach their own supplements in depth, with case studies and dosing rationale. This topic consolidates every one of those named supplements — plus the ones taught only in the Week 5 "Supplements" deck and never repeated elsewhere (DLPA, black seed oil, oil of oregano, the gout supplement stack, B12-form nuance) — into a single table for fast lookup, and covers the FullScript dispensary workflow and the course's patient-facing nutrition-education graphics.

Primary source: Jenni Gallagher APRN, *Supplements* (Joyful Heart Institute), Spring 2026 Mentorship Week 5, plus supplement references embedded in Weeks 1–4 and 6–10 of the same course (lab optimization, iron/ferritin, hypothyroidism, inflammation/Hashimoto's, metabolic syndrome, CKD/NAFLD/sleep, methylene blue/ivermectin/LDN, bone health).

## Key Takeaways

- **Supplement sourcing:** the course uses **FullScript exclusively**, not Amazon/retail — an Amazon audit found **lead and arsenic contamination** and widespread counterfeit products in supplements sold there.
- **The "4 base ingredients"** for thyroid function, repeated throughout the course: **Iodine, Vitamin D, Iron/Ferritin, Protein.**
- **Foundational thyroid-optimization stack** (Week 5 "Core Protocols"): **Iodine 225–300 mcg/day + Osteo Px (D3/K2/Mg/Boron) + Ferrosolve 45 mg BID + Vitamin C + Ashwagandha** (the only supplement in the course shown to raise Free T3).
- **Biotin interferes with ~150 different lab tests.** Washout rule taught: for every 100 mcg of biotin in a product, hold it 1 day before labs (general practical rule = hold 1 week). Full washout table for iron, hormones, vitamin D, and other supplements: [supplement-reference.md](supplement-reference.md) §"Lab Draw Timing."
- **Insulin-resistance stack:** berberine (1,000 mg BID) or dihydroberberine (200–400 mg 1–2x/day) plus inositol (2,000–4,000 mg/day powder) — **never combine berberine with metformin** (same AMPK/insulin-lowering pathway → lactic acidosis risk).
- **Hormone precursor support:** DHEA 5–10 mg/day titrated to 20–30 mg/day; micronized progesterone 100–200 mg PO at bedtime (oral only — vaginal/rectal routes are not effective for systemic sleep benefit).
- **Calcium supplementation is capped, not maximized:** doses above 600 mg/day are linked to atherosclerosis and don't reduce fracture risk in the cited evidence — prioritize dietary calcium; supplement only at low dose (100–200 mg BID) if needed.
- **Vitamin D never works alone** — it must be dosed with its cofactors (magnesium, K2, boron) and with fatty food, since it is fat-soluble.

## When to Use

Reference **[supplement-reference.md](supplement-reference.md)** whenever generating a supplement plan, a FullScript template, or patient-facing dosing guidance for: iron/ferritin repletion, thyroid optimization, insulin resistance/metabolic syndrome, gout/uric acid, sleep and hormone support, general anti-inflammatory protocols, gut healing, or B-vitamin/methylation support. Always cross-check the exact dose against the table (and the deeper condition-specific topic file it links to) before including it in patient-facing output — several supplements have two or three slightly different dosing figures taught across different weeks of the course, and both/all figures are preserved rather than silently reconciled.

Reference **[fullscript-and-resources.md](fullscript-and-resources.md)** when setting up or explaining the FullScript dispensary workflow (templates, discounting, cash-pay labs) or when assembling patient handouts/homework covering grains to avoid, plant-vs-animal protein, or low-carb beginner guidance.

## Evidence Quality Note

All doses and product names in this topic are reproduced **exactly as taught** in a single functional-medicine practitioner's mentorship program (Jenni Gallagher, APRN, Joyful Heart Institute) — practitioner clinical convention, not FDA-labeled dosing or RCT-graded guideline dosing, unless a specific citation is given in the source slide. Where the source gave no numeric dose, the reference table marks the cell **[source unclear]** rather than inferring a value. Cross-check current product labeling, patient-specific labs, drug interactions (notably berberine + metformin), and standard clinical judgment before prescribing or recommending any item in this reference.
