# Supplements & Nutrition — Supplement Reference

Consolidated, provider-level lookup table of every named supplement (and the handful of adjacent peptides/pharmaceuticals) taught across Derek's Spring 2026 mentorship, built primarily from *Supplements for mentorship.pdf* (Week 5, Jenni Gallagher APRN, Joyful Heart Institute) and cross-checked against every other week's course notes and the deeper topic files in this knowledge base.

**How to use this file:** it is a quick-reference table, not a narrative. Several supplements (Ferrosolve, Osteo Px, berberine, progesterone, magnesium, D3/K2) have much deeper dosing rationale, case studies, and evidence-quality discussion in their dedicated topic folders — this file gives the exact number and points you to the deeper file. **Doses are reproduced exactly as taught** (practitioner clinical convention, not FDA-labeled dosing or RCT-graded guideline dosing, unless noted). Where the source gave no number, the cell reads **[source unclear]** — do not infer a dose.

## 2. Insulin Resistance & Metabolic

| Supplement | Form | Typical dose | Primary indication | Notes/cautions |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Berberine** (standard HCl) | Oral, e.g. **CM Core** (Ortho Molecular) — Berberine HCl Hydrate 1 g + Alpha Lipoic Acid 200 mg per 3-capsule serving, 90 caps/container | **1,000 mg BID** (case-study "Insulin Support" dosing) | Insulin resistance, elevated fasting glucose/A1c, PCOS/metabolic ovarian syndrome, post-meal glucose spikes; also an herbal alternative for SIBO with diarrhea | Works via **AMPK activation** ("the body's metabolic master switch"). **Do NOT combine with metformin** — same insulin-lowering pathway, risk of **lactic acidosis**. | **Inositol** (Myo-Inositol) | Powder, e.g. Metagenics Myo-Inositol Plus — Magnesium 200 mg + Citric Acid 800 mg + Inositol 4,000 mg per serving | **2,000–4,000 mg/day powder** | Insulin resistance, PCOS/metabolic ovarian syndrome symptom management, egg quality, ovulatory regulation, miscarriage-risk reduction, mood/anxiety, PMDD/OCD symptoms, neuropathy, sleep quality | **Lowers testosterone in women, not men** — explicitly noted as a sex-specific effect; consider before use in men seeking testosterone-neutral options. ## Lab Draw Timing — Supplement & Hormone Washout

Applies broadly to nearly every supplement in this file. Reproduced from the PDF (slide 2, "Biotin"), the Week 5 core-protocol summary, and the standard patient homework email.

| Substance | Washout before labs | Rationale/source |
|---|---|---|
| **Biotin** | General rule: **stop 5–7 days (1 week)** before all labs. Dose-based formula from the PDF: **for every 100 mcg of biotin in a product, stop it 1 day before labs** (e.g., a product containing 500 mcg biotin → stop for at least **5 days**). | Biotin interferes with **~150 different lab tests** (immunoassay interference). Found in B-complex vitamins, protein drinks/powders, energy drinks, workout powders, hydration drinks, electrolyte mixes — check labels on all of these, not just a dedicated biotin supplement. |
| **B12** | **5–7 days** before draw (grouped with biotin in patient instructions) | Same lab-interference logic as biotin; high B12 (>2,000) can also independently signal an MTHFR variant or, rarely, a solid tumor. |
| **Iron / ferrochel / Ferrosolve** | **3–4 days** before draw | Avoids falsely elevated iron/ferritin readings from recent dosing. |
| **Hormones** (estrogen, progesterone, testosterone) | **2–3 days** before draw (homework email states 3–4 days); stop **2–3 days before** if tolerable per other course notes | Reproduced with both figures as given in different source documents — use the longer window when feasible. |
| **Vitamin D** | **10–14 days** before draw — **only if confirming a true high level** | Long ~2-week half-life; a recent dose skews results upward. Do not routinely hold Vitamin D before a standard optimization recheck. |
| **All other vitamins/supplements** | **2–3 days** before draw | General instruction from the standard patient homework email. |
| **Thyroid medication** | **18–20 hours** before draw | Avoids peak-dose skew on TSH/FT4/FT3. |
| **General lab-draw hygiene** | Fast **8–12 hours** (10–12 preferred); no coffee or juice morning-of; light water intake (**<16 oz**) is encouraged, not restricted, to avoid falsely concentrated (falsely elevated sodium/potassium) results | From the standard patient homework email lab-prep instructions. |

**Evidence quality note:** the biotin-interference washout is grounded in published immunoassay-interference literature (cited on the source slide: testing.com biotin-interference guideline summary; University of Iowa Path Handbook biotin immunoassay tables). The other washout windows (iron 3–4 days, hormones 2–4 days, general supplements 2–3 days) are practitioner clinical convention from this mentorship, not independently cited in the source material — apply standard clinical judgment and confirm against the specific lab's own biotin/analyte interference guidance before finalizing patient instructions.
