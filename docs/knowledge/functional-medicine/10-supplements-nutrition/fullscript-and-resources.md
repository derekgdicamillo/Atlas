# Supplements & Nutrition — FullScript Dispensary Workflow & Patient Education Resources

FullScript practice workflow for supplement dispensing plus verbatim transcriptions of the course's patient-facing nutrition/low-carb-education graphics (RESOURCES, GRAIN LIST, PLANT PROTEIN, TIPS FOR BEGINNERS), as taught in Spring 2026 Mentorship Week 5 (Jenni Gallagher APRN, Joyful Heart Institute).

## 1. FullScript Dispensary Workflow

**Why FullScript, not Amazon/retail:** the course specifically cites an **Amazon audit that found lead and arsenic in supplements sold there**, plus widespread counterfeit products, as the rationale for sourcing exclusively through a controlled dispensary. FullScript is taught as the quality-control layer between the practitioner's recommendation and what the patient actually receives.

**Template workflow:**
1. **Build templates for common conditions** — named examples: "Basic Supplement," "Gout." Templates encode the standard first-line stack for a given presentation so it doesn't need to be rebuilt per patient.
2. **Apply a template to a patient**, then **edit the cart** (add/remove products) to personalize the plan to that patient's specific labs and presentation.
3. **Discounting:** set a **maximum 35% discount for personal use** — this caps the discount at approximately wholesale price (i.e., practitioners should not price supplements below their own cost when using personal/family accounts on the platform).

**FullScript Labs:** the platform also offers **affordable cash-pay labs via Quest or LabCorp, with direct patient billing** — useful as an adjunct ordering channel alongside the supplement dispensary, particularly for cash-pay patients who aren't going through RUPA or Walk-in Labs for their primary lab work.

**Patient-facing link convention:** the deck and homework materials reference the instructor's own dispensary storefront links (e.g., `us.fullscript.com/welcome/jgallagher` and `us.fullscript.com/welcome/jgallagher/store-start`) as the pattern for how a patient accesses their personalized cart/template. Each practitioner sets up their own FullScript storefront under their own account — these specific links are Jenni Gallagher's, shown as a workflow example, not a link to reuse.

**Scope note:** the Week 5 deck also covers non-clinical business/practice-management content (fee-for-service vs. membership vs. DPC pricing models, website/Facebook marketing, LegitScript ad-certification requirements, pharmacy collaborative-agreement pushback). That content is intentionally out of scope for this clinical knowledge base and is not reproduced here.

## 5. TIPS FOR BEGINNERS.JPG (transcribed verbatim)

**Title:** "Tips for Low Carb beginners"

- Weigh & measure your body **MONTHLY**.
- Don't worry about weighing/tracking intake the first 4–6 weeks.
- Consume a **LOT** of salt daily.
- Drink more water — drink about **2–3 L per day**.
- Eat when hungry — get more fat.
- Keep electrolytes up — avocado, salt, greens.
- **STOP** at 1st sign of fullness.
- Avoid eating as a habit or schedule.
- Listen to body's signals.
- Add butter, sour cream, cheese to meals.
- Get **6–8 hours** of sleep regularly.
- Ask prescriber about medication doses.
- **LCHF can be extremely dangerous with certain meds.**

**Main goals stated on graphic:** keep carbs to **<20 g/day**. Avoid frequent hunger.

**Cross-reference:** the "<20 g/day" figure aligns with the "therapeutic ketogenic eating" tier (<20 g carbs/day) defined in [../06-metabolic-syndrome/diet-carb-framework.md](../06-metabolic-syndrome/diet-carb-framework.md) §1. The salt/water/electrolyte instructions echo the gut-health protocol's "2 tsp salt/day minimum, escalate if symptomatic" and "2–3 L/day with electrolytes" guidance in [../05-gut-health-healing/healing-protocol.md](../05-gut-health-healing/healing-protocol.md).
