## Scope

This topic covers:
- **Methylene blue (MB)**: history/regulatory status, mitochondrial mechanism, taught indications (cognitive/energy, infection, sleep), oral dosing, and its two hard-stop safety flags — SSRI/SNRI serotonin syndrome risk and G6PD-deficiency hemolysis risk
- **Ivermectin (IVM)**: mechanism (glutamate-gated chloride channel binding), FDA-approved antiparasitic use, and the much broader off-label uses taught in this mentorship — rosacea (Soolantra), COVID-19, investigational oncology — with evidence quality flagged per indication; dosing, interactions, side effects
- **Low-dose naltrexone (LDN)**: opioid-receptor-antagonist mechanism, the grain-free-diet prerequisite for efficacy as taught, **two distinct titration/dosing protocols** given in the source material (Dr. Wentz's method vs. Gallagher's compounded-Rx pattern), autoimmune/inflammatory/mental-health indications, and the compounded inositol/LDN/methylcobalamin weight-loss formula
- Cross-reference: LDN's role as an autoimmune-inflammation adjunct is introduced in [../03-inflammation-hashimotos/README.md](../03-inflammation-hashimotos/README.md); the full dosing/titration protocol lives here

## Key Takeaways

1. **Methylene blue + SSRI/SNRI carries a serotonin syndrome risk** — taught explicitly as a caution/contraindication. MB is a reversible MAO-A inhibitor at these doses; the instructor's practice is to wean patients off SSRIs/SNRIs *before* starting MB, not co-prescribe. See [methylene-blue.md](methylene-blue.md).
2. **Methylene blue is contraindicated in G6PD deficiency / hemolytic anemia**, and avoided in pregnancy/breastfeeding. Screen G6PD status before prescribing. MB also contains a sulfur moiety — screen for sulfa-drug (e.g., Bactrim) allergy.
3. **Ivermectin dose resolved (2026-07-14 literature review): use 0.4 mg/kg/day, not 4.4.** The source carried two figures — 0.4 mg/kg PO QD (Week 9 slides; empty stomach, avoid curcumin/cyclosporine) and 4.4 mg/kg/day (Week 7 notes). Review confirmed **0.4 mg/kg/day** as defensible (documented safe; corroborated by published integrative parasite protocols), and **4.4 mg/kg/day has no basis and exceeds the highest human-studied dose, 2 mg/kg — treat as a transcription error, do not use.** FDA-approved dose is a single ~0.2 mg/kg; 0.4/day is an off-label extended protocol. Full reasoning + host-susceptibility (MDR1/Loa loa) caveats in [ivermectin.md](ivermectin.md).
4. **LDN has two titration schedules taught in this mentorship** — "Dr. Wentz's method" (0.5 mg → 1.0 mg → 1.5 mg biweekly, maintenance 4.5–7 mg/day) and Gallagher's own two-prescription compounded pattern (0.5 mg capsules stepped to 1.5 mg over 6 weeks, then 1.5 mg capsules stepped to 4.5 mg, then long-term 4.5–6 mg capsules, titratable up to ~8–10 mg PRN). Both are reproduced in full in [low-dose-naltrexone.md](low-dose-naltrexone.md); do not blend the step schedules.
5. **LDN is taught as ineffective without a grain-free diet** — grain-driven inflammatory spikes are said to negate LDN's overnight anti-inflammatory action, so patient buy-in to grain-free eating is a prerequisite screening question, not an afterthought.
6. **Evidence quality varies sharply by indication and must be labeled, not assumed.** IVM's antiparasitic/rosacea use has real mechanistic and trial support; its COVID-19 and oncology uses as taught rest on a contested and/or preclinical evidence base (current FDA/CDC guidance does not endorse ivermectin for COVID-19). MB's antimalarial/methemoglobinemia history is well established; its cognitive/energy/Lyme/CHF claims here are practitioner-experience level. LDN's autoimmune/inflammatory use has a growing but still limited RCT base; its oncology/autism/dissociative-disorder listings are taught benefit categories, not established indications.

## When to Use

- **Considering methylene blue** for mitochondrial/energy/cognitive support: first screen for G6PD deficiency, pregnancy/breastfeeding, current SSRI/SNRI use, and sulfa allergy. If an SSRI/SNRI is on the med list, plan a wean before initiating MB rather than co-prescribing. See [methylene-blue.md](methylene-blue.md).
- **Considering ivermectin** for a confirmed or suspected parasitic infection, rosacea with suspected Demodex overload, or as empiric treatment while awaiting stool O&P results in acute unexplained GI symptoms (per the Week 9 case example): confirm which dosing figure applies to the clinical scenario (standard antiparasitic vs. the higher Week 7 parasite figure) and check for curcumin/cyclosporine co-administration. See [ivermectin.md](ivermectin.md).
- **Considering LDN** for autoimmune disease, chronic inflammatory conditions, fibromyalgia, Lyme, Long-Haul COVID, or as a weight-loss/inflammation adjunct: confirm grain-free diet buy-in first (Key Takeaway 5), select and stay consistent with one titration protocol, and use a compounding pharmacy — LDN is not commercially available at these strengths and is not insurance-covered as taught. See [low-dose-naltrexone.md](low-dose-naltrexone.md).
- **Peri-operative planning**: if a patient on LDN needs surgery requiring narcotic analgesia, discontinue LDN 3–5 days pre-op (naltrexone blocks the opioid receptors needed for perioperative opioid pain control); restart post-op at a lower dose and retitrate faster than the initial schedule.

## Clinical Disclaimers

1. This is provider-level reference material for licensed practitioner use (FNP/APRN scope). It is not a substitute for individualized clinical judgment, current FDA labeling, or current society/guideline review — particularly for ivermectin's COVID-19 and oncology uses, which remain outside mainstream guideline recommendations as of this writing.
2. All three agents discussed here are being used off-label or at non-standard doses for most of the indications listed (MB for cognition/energy; IVM for rosacea/COVID/cancer; LDN for autoimmune/mental-health conditions). Confirm off-label status is disclosed to and understood by the patient, and document informed consent per practice policy.
3. Methylene blue's drug-interaction risk (serotonin syndrome with serotonergic agents) is not limited to SSRIs/SNRIs in general pharmacology — this source material's explicit teaching covers SSRIs/SNRIs; practitioners should apply standard serotonin-syndrome-risk screening (MAOIs, tramadol, triptans, linezolid, etc.) using independent clinical knowledge, since those additional agent classes are not explicitly enumerated in the mentorship slides.
4. Where source material was ambiguous, gave two non-reconciled figures, or a value could not be confirmed, this is marked "[source unclear]" or flagged as a discrepancy rather than invented or silently resolved.
5. Pharmacy names, phone numbers, and pricing reflect one practitioner's local/practice-specific arrangements (as of the Spring 2026 mentorship) and are included for provenance, not as a universal recommendation or current pricing guarantee.
