# Functional Medicine — Case Library

Worked cases from the Spring 2026 mentorship, distilled as reusable reasoning examples. Each shows the same tree in action: read by optimal ranges → find the trunk (insulin/inflammation) → sequence the branches → respect the safety gates. Full per-case detail lives in the topic `case-studies.md` files; this is the cross-topic teaching set.

---

## Case 1 — Perimenopausal, post-hysterectomy, high inflammation + insulin resistance (primary teaching case)

**Profile:** 41 y/o, post-hysterectomy (no ovaries), fatigue, weight gain.

**Labs:**
- Inflammation: CRP 6.0 (high), homocysteine 12.1 (high), WBC 10.6 (high)
- Insulin resistance: fasting glucose 110, triglycerides 176 (high), HDL 28 (low)
- Hormones: estradiol 51, testosterone 11 (low), DHEA-S 78 (very low)
- Thyroid: TSH 2.0, Free T4 1.0, Free T3 3.6
- Iron: iron 87, ferritin 67

**Reasoning (down the tree):**
1. **Trunk = inflammation + insulin resistance.** CRP 6.0 / Hcy 12.1 / WBC 10.6 + trigs 176 / HDL 28 / glucose 110 — these are driving the picture. Treat first.
2. **Hormones:** low HDL (28) and very low DHEA-S (78) signal a lack of raw material (cholesterol) for hormone synthesis — a *consequence* of the metabolic state, not the primary lesion.
3. **Thyroid:** TSH 2.0 is suboptimal, FT3 3.6 is low-optimal — suspect iodine deficiency, but don't chase it before the trunk.
4. **Iron:** ferritin 67 is at the low edge of optimal — adequate for now, above the T3 gate.

**Plan:**
- **Diet:** anti-inflammatory, grain-free, high-protein (red meat).
- **Supplements:** Osteopiex (D3/K2/Mg/boron), high-dose vitamin C, **low-dose iodine (<300 mcg/day).**
- **Insulin support:** berberine 1000 mg BID *or* dihydroberberine 200 mg/day.
- **Hormone support:** progesterone for sleep/irritability; DHEA 5–10 mg/day titrating to 20–30.
- **Follow-up:** retest at **4–5 months** to allow physiologic change.

**Teaching point:** don't start with the hormones the patient "feels." Start with the trunk; the hormone numbers move as insulin and inflammation resolve.

## Case 2 — Hashimoto's, severe iron deficiency, non-compliance (see [03](../03-inflammation-hashimotos/hashimotos-management.md), [02](../02-iron-ferritin/case-studies.md))

**Sketch:** TPO Ab 386 → >1100 IU/mL; TSH 57.3; ferritin 6 → 11.

**Teaching points:** rising antibodies + climbing TSH track non-compliance with the inflammation-elimination diet. Ferritin 6 is far below the **>45 T3 gate** — iron must come up before/alongside thyroid dosing. Iron correction is slow; recheck on the 4-month cycle.

## Case 3 — New-onset AFib after iron infusion (see [02](../02-iron-ferritin/case-studies.md), [03](../03-inflammation-hashimotos/hashimotos-management.md))

**Teaching points:** escalation to IV iron is legitimate when oral fails, but monitor — cardiac rhythm events can follow aggressive correction in a previously depleted, inflamed patient. Sequence and monitor; don't rush repletion.

## Case 4 — 46 y/o, NAFLD picture, estrogen dominance, low ferritin (see [07](../07-ckd-nafld-sleep/nafld.md))

**Sketch:** ferritin 18, estradiol ~6000 (dominance), globulin 2.5.

**Teaching points:** NAFLD driver is total carbohydrate/glucose load (not fructose alone) — carb restriction + weight loss (dose-response: ≥5% steatosis, 7–9% histology, >10% fibrosis). Ferritin 18 (near cardiac-risk floor) and low globulin (protein intake/absorption) are treated alongside. Estrogen dominance sits downstream of the metabolic state.

## How to use these

When a new panel comes in, match it to the nearest case, then re-derive the tree for the specifics — don't copy the plan. The value is the *sequencing logic* (trunk before branches, safety gates before therapy), not the exact supplement list.
