# Functional Medicine — FAQ Knowledge

Distilled recurring questions from the Spring 2026 mentorship, phrased as Atlas Medicine would answer them. Answers follow the course's teaching; numeric targets are functional-medicine consensus unless a study is named. Cross-references point to the source topic files.

## Labs & interpretation

**Q: A patient's labs are all "normal" but they feel terrible. Where do I start?**
"Normal" means inside the lab's reference range, not optimal. Re-read the panel against functional targets ([lab-reference.md](lab-reference.md)) — ferritin 12, fasting insulin 18, FT3 3.2, vitamin D 35 all flag "normal" yet are far from optimal. Start with the trunk: insulin and inflammation.

**Q: Which labs matter most for metabolic root cause?**
Fasting insulin (≤5), HbA1c (<5.3%), triglycerides (<90), HDL (>50). Insulin is the single most useful early marker — it rises years before glucose. >75% of patients with "normal" glucose fail insulin testing (Kraft). See [01](../01-lab-optimization/) and [06](../06-metabolic-syndrome/).

**Q: Why order Free T3 and Reverse T3 instead of just TSH?**
Only Free T3 enters the cell to drive ATP production. TSH and FT4 don't. FT3 is the "gas pedal" (target 3.5–4), RT3 the "brake pedal" (target 10–12). TSH alone misses functional hypothyroidism. See [04](../04-functional-hypothyroid-mthfr/thyroid-management.md).

**Q: How do I prep a patient before a draw?**
Hold biotin/B12 5–7 days, fast 10–12 h, hold thyroid meds 18–20 h, iron 4 days, other hormones 2–3 days. Vitamin D only held (10–14 days) when confirming a suspected true-high. See [01](../01-lab-optimization/lab-interpretation-workflow.md).

## Iron / ferritin

**Q: What iron do you use and why?**
Ferosolv (ferrous bisglycinate) 45 mg BID — best-absorbed, gentlest form. Never exceed 45 mg/dose (nephrotoxicity). Pair with vitamin C 2–4 g TID/QID (to bowel tolerance) and vitamin D (lowers hepcidin, which blocks absorption). See [02](../02-iron-ferritin/treatment-protocol.md).

**Q: Why recheck iron at 4 months, not 3?**
The RBC lifecycle is ~100 days; a full 4-month interval lets the population turn over for an accurate reading. Use a 6–8 week check for tolerance/safety, but judge efficacy at 4 months.

**Q: Ferritin is high — is that iron overload?**
Not necessarily. Ferritin is an acute-phase reactant; when it runs 1.5–2× the iron number it usually signals inflammation, not overload. Read it alongside iron and CRP.

## Thyroid

**Q: Can I start liothyronine right away?**
Not until **ferritin > 45**. T3 is a cardiac stimulant; starting it on depleted iron is a cardiac risk. Fix iron first. Also: once on liothyronine, TSH suppresses and stops being useful — follow FT3/FT4/RT3. See [04](../04-functional-hypothyroid-mthfr/thyroid-management.md).

**Q: How much iodine in Hashimoto's?**
Low-dose only (~150–300 mcg/day; one case explicitly <300). Never high-dose iodine in autoimmune thyroid. See [03](../03-inflammation-hashimotos/) and [04](../04-functional-hypothyroid-mthfr/).

**Q: One thyroid antibody is positive, the other isn't — is it Hashimoto's?**
Yes — a single positive TPO or TgAb is diagnostic. Levels in the hundreds–thousands confirm; <100 is more likely general inflammation. Draw both on every suspected hypothyroid patient.

## Metabolic / insulin

**Q: Berberine or metformin?**
Berberine 1000 mg BID (or dihydroberberine 200–400 mg) for insulin resistance — but **never combine berberine with metformin** (shared pathway, lactic-acidosis risk). See [06](../06-metabolic-syndrome/).

**Q: Isn't obesity the cause of metabolic syndrome?**
No — the course frames obesity as a *symptom*, not the cause. Hyperinsulinemia is upstream. Carb restriction is the primary lever. See [06](../06-metabolic-syndrome/metabolic-syndrome-tree.md).

## Gut

**Q: Where do I start with a complex GI patient?**
Constipation is the root of ~80–90% of GI complaints, including reflux. Establish transit first. Most patients need only Steps 1–3 of the 7-step gut protocol; binders/leaky-gut repair are reserved for toxin/permeability cases. The gut protocol is the foundation that precedes other plans. See [05](../05-gut-health-healing/healing-protocol.md).

## Adjunct pharmaceuticals

**Q: Methylene blue with a patient on an antidepressant?**
No. MB is an MAO-A inhibitor at these doses — combined with an SSRI/SNRI it risks serotonin syndrome. Wean the antidepressant before starting MB; don't co-prescribe. Screen G6PD first (hemolysis risk). See [08](../08-methylene-blue-ivm-ldn/methylene-blue.md).

**Q: How do you dose LDN?**
Titrated low-dose, typically toward 4.5 mg nightly (two protocols in the course — Wentz-style 0.5→1.5 biweekly to 4.5–7 mg, and a compounded step-up to 4.5, titratable higher PRN). A grain-free diet is a stated prerequisite for efficacy. Stop 3–5 days pre-op if narcotics are needed. See [08](../08-methylene-blue-ivm-ldn/low-dose-naltrexone.md).

**Q: What ivermectin dose does the course teach?**
**0.4 mg/kg/day** (empty stomach), given over a multi-day course (≈5–10 days, or 1–3 doses by indication). The source also contained a 4.4 mg/kg/day figure — **that was a transcription error and should not be used** (resolved by literature review 2026-07-14; it exceeds the highest human-studied dose, 2 mg/kg). Note 0.4 mg/kg/day is an *off-label extended* protocol — the FDA-approved dose is a single ~0.2 mg/kg; it's corroborated by published integrative parasite protocols but isn't FDA-labeled. Any above-standard dosing: watch host susceptibility (ABCB1/MDR1 mutations, Loa loa co-infection can cause neurotoxicity even at standard doses). See [08](../08-methylene-blue-ivm-ldn/ivermectin.md).

## Bone

**Q: Should patients take calcium for bones?**
Not high-dose — calcium > 600 mg/day is linked to atherosclerosis without reducing fractures. Prefer dietary calcium plus the D3/K2/Mg/boron stack (Osteopiex). Bone health is systemic (protein, estrogen, thyroid, inflammation), not just calcium. See [09](../09-bone-health/).

## Meta

**Q: How confident should I be in these "optimal" numbers?**
Most are functional-medicine practitioner consensus taught in this course, not guideline-level evidence. A handful rest on cited studies (hs-CRP/JUPITER, HbA1c/Butalia, vitamin D/Garland, uric acid/Leiba). Present them as reasoned convention, name the evidence tier, and defer to Derek's clinical judgment.
