# Functional Medicine — Root-Cause Decision Tree

The recurring triage logic across every case in the Spring 2026 mentorship. The organizing principle: **work upstream to downstream.** Insulin resistance and inflammation are the trunk; hormones, thyroid, iron, and gut are branches that resolve more easily once the trunk is addressed.

## Step 0 — Read the whole panel by optimal ranges

Before treating any single number, lay the full panel against functional targets ([lab-reference.md](lab-reference.md)). Reading order: thyroid → inflammation → iron → electrolytes/protein/liver → glucose-insulin → lipids → hormones. Note every "normal but not optimal" value.

## Step 1 — Insulin resistance / metabolic (the trunk)

**Screen:** fasting insulin > 5, HbA1c > 5.3%, triglycerides > 90, HDL < 50, waist/BP criteria, low AST/ALT paradox, fatty liver signs.

- **If present → treat first.** Carbohydrate restriction is the primary lever (low-carb/keto framework, topic 06). Consider **berberine 1000 mg BID** or **dihydroberberine 200–400 mg** — **never with metformin (lactic acidosis).**
- Insulin resistance is upstream of sex-hormone imbalance, inflammation, HTN, NAFLD, PCOS. Correcting it often partially self-corrects the branches.
- → then proceed down the tree.

## Step 2 — Inflammation

**Screen:** hs-CRP > 1, WBC > 6.5–7, homocysteine > 8–9, uric acid > 5, ferritin running 1.5–2× iron (acute-phase, not overload).

- **If present → identify & remove drivers** (gut, diet/grains/seed oils, insulin, infection, toxins), then support resolution. Anti-inflammatory stack per topic 03.
- Homocysteine branch: high Hcy + **normal** MMA ⇒ folate deficiency; high Hcy + **high** MMA ⇒ B12 deficiency → methylated forms (methylfolate, methylcobalamin), topic 04.
- Inflammation resolves slowly — recheck markers at 9–18 months.

## Step 3 — Hormones

**Screen:** low DHEA-S/testosterone, estradiol far from ~100, E1 > E2, low HDL (raw material for hormones).

- Because insulin drives hormone imbalance, **confirm Steps 1–2 are underway before aggressive hormone replacement.**
- Support raw materials (cholesterol/HDL, DHEA 5–10 mg titrated to 20–30, progesterone for sleep/irritability). BHRT specifics cross-reference `docs/knowledge/bhrt/`.

## Step 4 — Thyroid

**Screen:** full panel — FT3 < 3.5, RT3 > 15, TPO/TgAb > 100, symptoms despite "normal" TSH.

- **Hard gate: ferritin must be > 45 before starting any T3-containing med** (liothyronine/NDT) — cardiac risk otherwise. If ferritin low → fix iron first (Step 5), then dose T3.
- RT3 high ⇒ look back up the tree (inflammation, stress, low ferritin, dieting) — RT3 is the "brake."
- Hashimoto's ⇒ eliminate inflammatory drivers (grains, seed oils, dairy) on top of dosing. **Low-dose iodine only** (≤300 mcg/day); never high-dose in autoimmune thyroid.

## Step 5 — Iron / ferritin

**Screen:** ferritin < 65 (symptoms), < 45 (blocks T3), < 20 (cardiac risk); fatigue/brain fog/palpitations/dyspnea.

- **Protocol:** Ferosolv (ferrous bisglycinate) **45 mg BID, never > 45 mg/dose** (nephrotoxicity) + **vitamin C 2–4 g TID/QID** to bowel tolerance + **vitamin D** (lowers hepcidin) + red meat, remove grains/phytates.
- Establish bowel regularity *before* loading iron (constipation management first).
- **Recheck at 4 months**, not 3 (100-day RBC lifecycle). Escalate to IV iron (Feraheme/Venofer) if oral fails.

## Step 6 — Gut (foundation beneath it all)

**Screen:** constipation (root of ~80–90% of GI complaints), reflux, bloating, dysbiosis (SIBO/Candida/H. pylori), food reactivity, autoimmune signals.

- The gut protocol is the **foundation that precedes other plans**; most patients need only Steps 1–3 of the 7-step protocol (topic 05). Fix transit/drainage before aggressive kills or heavy supplementation.

## Cross-cutting safety gates (check on every plan)

- Ferritin > 45 before T3.
- No berberine + metformin.
- Methylene blue: **not with SSRI/SNRI** (serotonin syndrome); screen **G6PD**.
- Low-dose iodine only in autoimmune thyroid.
- Calcium supplement ≤ 600 mg/day.
- **Ivermectin: use 0.4 mg/kg/day** (off-label extended protocol; FDA single-dose is 0.2 mg/kg). The old 4.4 mg/kg/day figure was a transcription error — resolved 2026-07-14, do not use. Screen for MDR1/Loa loa susceptibility with any above-standard dosing.
