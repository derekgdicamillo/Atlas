# Metabolic Syndrome — Overview

Navigation and clinical scope for the metabolic syndrome / insulin resistance topic, built from Derek's Spring 2026 mentorship (Jenni Gallagher, APRN, Joyful Heart Institute) and Derek's own first/second-appointment patient homework packet.

## Scope

Metabolic syndrome is treated here as a **spectrum disease rooted in hyperinsulinemia and insulin resistance (IR)**, not a checklist of five independent risk factors. The mentorship's core teaching point — echoed directly in the source graphic — is:

> "FACT: Obesity doesn't cause metabolic syndrome. It's only one symptom of metabolic syndrome."

The root is insulin resistance in liver and muscle, fed by excess starch/grain/sugar intake, producing a trunk of hyperinsulinemia that branches into weight gain, hypertension, dyslipidemia, T2DM, PCOS, fatty liver, and — left unchecked — coronary artery disease, dementia, and cancer risk. See [metabolic-syndrome-tree.md](metabolic-syndrome-tree.md) for the full transcribed branching logic and diagnostic criteria.

This topic also carries the **dietary/nutrition framework** taught alongside metabolic syndrome, since carbohydrate restriction is the mentorship's primary lever for reversing IR. See [diet-carb-framework.md](diet-carb-framework.md) for carb-tier definitions, fat/protein/butter transcriptions, and berberine dosing.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Obesity is a symptom, not the cause.** The root of the "tree" is insulin resistance in liver and muscle; hyperinsulinemia is the trunk. Treating weight without addressing insulin signaling misses the driver.
2. **Standard fasting glucose and A1c are late indicators.** Per Kraft's oral insulin assay research (14,384 patients), more than 75% of people with "normal" glucose tolerance results failed to show a normal insulin response — i.e., they were diabetic by insulin pattern despite normal glucose. Post-prandial/fasting insulin is the earlier signal.
3. **Diagnostic criteria (ATP III/NCEP)** require 3 of 5: waist circumference, triglycerides, HDL, blood pressure, fasting glucose (exact thresholds in [metabolic-syndrome-tree.md](metabolic-syndrome-tree.md)). The mentorship teaches tighter "early identification" thresholds than ATP III for functional-medicine-level intervention (e.g., insulin >5 mcU/mL, A1c >5.2%, triglycerides >90–100 mg/dL).
4. **Insulin is framed as a "big bully"** driving downstream hormone imbalance: low testosterone in men, estrogen dominance in men and women, and blunted estradiol in some menopausal women — plus inflammatory marker elevation (CRP-hs, uric acid, WBC, homocysteine).
5. **The primary intervention is carbohydrate restriction**, supported by optimizing vitamin D, iron/ferritin, protein intake and absorption, thyroid, and reproductive hormones. The mentorship cites an 11:1 evidence ratio favoring carbohydrate restriction over fat restriction across most cardiometabolic risk markers (LDL concentration being the one exception favoring low-fat).

## When to Use

- New patient intake showing any combination of: central adiposity, elevated triglycerides, low HDL, hypertension, elevated fasting glucose/A1c/insulin, acanthosis nigricans, skin tags, PCOS, or fatty liver.
- Any patient on the standard first/second-appointment homework packet (this is literally the packet content).
- Differentiating true insulin resistance from "normal labs" — use the Kraft-pattern reasoning and early-identification thresholds in [metabolic-syndrome-tree.md](metabolic-syndrome-tree.md) rather than relying on fasting glucose alone.
- Building a low-carb/keto dietary prescription — pull exact gram thresholds and food-category tables from [diet-carb-framework.md](diet-carb-framework.md); do not freehand carb targets.
- Cross-reference [02-iron-ferritin](../02-iron-ferritin/) and [03-inflammation-hashimotos](../03-inflammation-hashimotos/) — iron/ferritin optimization and inflammation-marker control are named co-interventions for reversing IR in this curriculum.

## Evidence Quality Note

Source material mixes tiers:
- **Established/consensus**: ATP III/NCEP diagnostic criteria, HOMA-IR, insulin-signaling/inflammation pathway biology (de Luca & Olefsky 2008).
- **Emerging/practitioner-level**: Kraft insulin-pattern research (large N=14,384 but retrospective clinic dataset, not RCT), Society of Metabolic Health Practitioners carb-restriction guidelines, ketogenic-diet remote-care outcome studies (Virta/IU Health cohorts — industry-affiliated but peer-reviewed).
- **Practitioner opinion / clinical convention, not peer-reviewed**: the "early identification" lab thresholds on slides 12–14 (e.g., insulin >5 mcU/mL, HOMA-IR >1.9, uric acid >5.0), the carb-gram tier definitions (two internally inconsistent versions exist — see [diet-carb-framework.md](diet-carb-framework.md)), and all "FM connections"/hormone-bully framing.
