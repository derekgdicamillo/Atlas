# Gut Health — The Gut Healing Protocol

The step-by-step Gut Healing Protocol as authored by Jenni Gallagher, APRN (Joyful Heart Health Care / KetoNurses), reproduced verbatim for products, doses, and durations from the Rev. Jan 2025 patient handout and the Week 7 Spring 2026 mentorship teaching.

**Source documents (identical protocol text in both):** `gut healing protocol2025.pdf` and `GUT HEALING PROTOCOL.pdf`, both "Gut Healing Protocol," Revised Jan. 2025, COPYRIGHT Joyful Heart Health Care, Jenni Gallagher, APRN. Cross-checked against the Week 7 slide deck (`Gut Health for Mentorship.pdf`) and the Week 7 section of `course_lesson_notes.md`.

**Evidence quality:** This is a **practitioner-authored clinical protocol based on clinical experience**, not a peer-reviewed or RCT-validated treatment algorithm. Dosing and duration are the author's own clinical recommendations ("I typically encourage...", "I now encourage..."). Apply standard clinical judgment (renal/hepatic function, drug interactions, pregnancy/pediatric status, medication list) before prescribing. Where the mentorship course notes give a figure that differs from the written protocol/slide deck, both figures are given and flagged.

## Who This Protocol Is For

Per the author, this protocol is recommended as **the foundation of a patient's entire treatment plan** — "Until you empty the intestines completely and start HEALING the lining & muscles of your gut, you will not start to heal from the inside out. All nutrient absorption is based on having a HEALTHY digestive system."

- **Most average patients** (no major GI disease such as Crohn's or severe IBS): the author explicitly states Steps 1–3 (cleanse/broth, GI Synergy, probiotic) are typically sufficient — "Most average people can do the cleansing, the GI Synergy, and the Probiotic and be done with the protocol."
- **The Universal Toxin Binder (Step 3)** is intended specifically for patients with suspected toxin burden — mold, heavy metals, or chemical exposures — and can be skipped by patients without recent antibiotic use, anesthesia in the past year, or suspected toxicity.
- **BioFilm and L-glutamine (Steps 5–6)** target leaky gut/intestinal permeability specifically and are for patients with known or suspected gut lining damage.
- **Full protocol (all steps):** average completion is 5–6 months; patients with a long history of severe digestive problems may need 6–12 months (course notes: framed as a "9–12 month plan for severe dysfunction (e.g., IBS, Crohn's)").

## Cost-Management Structure

The author explicitly designed the protocol to be completed **one step per month, one bottle/package at a time**, to reduce upfront cost: "use one supplement (unless specified differently) for 4-6 weeks or for ONE package/bottle until it is used completely up... This method saves you from a lot of upfront expense AND allows you to order 1-2 items at a time as needed."

## Step-by-Step Protocol

### Step 1 — Laxative/Cleansing & Broth

**Goal:** Completely empty the colon before starting any repopulation/repair supplement.

- **Laxative:** Any laxative is acceptable **except Miralax or fiber/psyllium** (these can take up to 2 weeks to act, causing unnecessary discomfort while waiting).
  - Acceptable options: milk of magnesia, magnesium citrate, or other laxatives, **provided the patient has no kidney injury/damage** — "Laxatives can worsen kidney damage."
  - **If kidney damage is present** and a colon cleanse is still needed: use senna, coconut oil, and/or salt as a natural laxative instead.
  - **Senna dosing note:** to work as an effective overnight purgative, the label dose typically must be **doubled or tripled**; check the label for a second, higher purgative-dose directions section.
  - **Duration:** typically 1–2 days, sometimes 3–5 days, depending on constipation severity. Up to 3–5 nights of laxative use (following safe label directions) may be needed to fully empty the colon. Some patients clear after just 1–2 doses.
  - *(Course lesson notes give an additional example option not named in the written protocol: high-dose Vitamin C, titrated to bowel tolerance, approximately 10–12 g/day, used in a case study for a patient with severe constipation and ferritin 18 — [source: course lesson notes case study, not the written protocol document].)*
- **Broth/collagen (started same week, continued long-term):**
  - Begin a collagen product containing **at least Type 1, Type 2, and Type 3 collagen** from an animal source. Bone broth is the preferred source; other broths are acceptable as long as they supply Types 1–3.
  - **Frequency:** salted, buttered broth/collagen **several times a day** during the initial weeks; continue **once or twice daily for many weeks to months** — broth-based membrane repair is described as "a pretty slow process."
- **Diet during Step 1 (and ongoing):** Patient is **not required to fast or skip meals**. Meats and vegetables may be eaten freely, cooked in bacon grease and butter. **No grains** — specifically excluded: corn, rice, oats, wheat, rye, barley, quinoa.

### Step 2 / Month 1 — GI Synergy

**Goal:** Kill off unhealthy Candida (yeast) and some pathogenic bacteria.

- **When to start:** once the colon is believed to be completely empty (can take up to 4–5 days from Step 1 start).
- **Product:** **GI Synergy** — sourced via the author's FullScript shop (patient emails `ketonurses@gmail.com` for the FullScript link).
- **Duration:** **4–6 weeks** for most patients; **some patients may need 3–4 months**. Duration is response-guided, not fixed — "not forever."
- **Die-off management:** herbs + colon cleansing together can cause bacterial/yeast die-off — mild to moderate weakness, brain fog, and tummy upset. **During this period it is vital to drink extra water or broth and increase salt intake.**
- **Bowel monitoring:** some patients become constipated on GI Synergy — do **not** allow stool to become hard/difficult to pass; increase water, salt, and butter as needed. **Never skip 3 or more days without a bowel movement.**

### Step 3 / Month 2 — Universal Toxin Binder (Optional Step)

**Goal:** Bind and remove toxins (a "toxin magnet" that moves through the intestines).

- **Product:** Universal Toxin Binder.
- **Dose/duration:** **once daily for 20 days, or one full box of packets.**
- **When to use:** consider **optional/skippable** if the patient has not taken antibiotics or had anesthesia in the past year and reports fairly good overall gut health. Consider this step **important** if the patient has suspected heavy metal, mold, or other toxin exposure.
- **Critical timing rule:** Binders must **not** be taken within **1–2 hours of any food, medication, or vitamin/supplement** — binders will bind and prevent absorption of those items.

### Step 4 / Month 3 — Probiotics

**Goal:** Repopulate the gut with healthy bacterial strains.

- **Product:** **Trust Your Gut Probiotic** (author's FullScript shop) — selected for its **7 strains** of bacteria, including *Lactobacillus reuteri*. The author explicitly instructs to disregard the CFU/"billions" count on the front label and focus instead on the **number of distinct bacterial strains listed on the back**.
  - *(Course lesson notes reference a generic example of "a multi-strain probiotic (e.g., Orthobiotic)" when summarizing this step — this is the course-notes paraphrase, not the named product in the written protocol; the written protocol's named product is Trust Your Gut Probiotic.)*
- **Dosing/administration:** twice daily on a **completely empty stomach** — no food, medicine, or other drinks, **only water**, for **at least 30–45 minutes** before/after.
- **Schedule:** twice daily for **1 week**, then **once daily** thereafter.
- **Duration:** **4–6 weeks** is generally sufficient to replace healthy gut flora for most patients. Long-term daily use is not typically required; many patients continue **1–2×/week** long-term to support ongoing gut bacterial health. Best re-use case: after any future "gut insult."

### Step 5 / Month 4 — BioFilm

**Goal:** Heal leaky gut syndrome (intestinal permeability).

- **Product:** BioFilm.
- **Dose:** **once or twice daily.**
- **Duration:** determined by the extent of the patient's gut damage/history — **"continue as long as you see benefits."** No fixed endpoint given.
- **Indication:** primarily for patients believed to have leaky gut syndrome, where damaged intestinal lining allows nutrients/toxins to leak through a weakened barrier.

### Step 6 / Month 5 — L-Glutamine

**Goal:** Continue leaky gut repair alongside BioFilm.

- **Dose:** **twice daily.**
- **Continue BioFilm concurrently if still needed.**
- **Duration:** determined by extent of gut damage, same response-guided approach as Step 5.
- **Combined maintenance:** continue glutamine, BioFilm, probiotics, and collagen/broth together, once or twice daily, for:
  - **3–6 months** for patients with a long history of severe digestive problems, extending to **6–12 months** if needed.
  - **Most patients only need 5–6 months total** of these supplements; duration is experience- and symptom-based — "continue as long as you see benefits."
  - **L-glutamine is often continued long-term** as general gut-health support, distinct from the other steps which are typically time-limited.

### Step 7 — Berberine (Adjunctive, Any Time)

- **May be added at any point during or after the protocol**, not tied to a specific month/step number.
- **Primary indication:** gut issues **combined with** insulin resistance, PCOS, or diabetes.
- **Rationale:** improves gut health *and* insulin resistance.
- **Cross-reference:** berberine and metformin act via overlapping pathways to lower insulin (not glucose directly) and should **not be combined** due to lactic acidosis risk [source: Week 8 course lesson notes, CKD section — relevant safety note when berberine is added to a patient already on metformin].

## Ongoing / Foundational Diet & Lifestyle Instructions (Run Throughout the Protocol)

- **No grains, from Day 1:** corn, rice, oats, wheat, rye, barley, quinoa excluded — described as the single most damaging dietary input in this framework (fermentation, bloating, lining damage, blocked absorption of iron/T3/zinc/protein, increased inflammation).
- **No fasting/meal-skipping required** — patient may eat meats and vegetables freely (cooked in bacon grease/butter) unless they personally choose to fast.
- **Salt:** minimum **2 teaspoons of salt per day** for the average person; if symptoms persist at that intake, **add another teaspoon**. Rationale given: salt/chloride supports peristaltic muscle function and stomach acid (HCl) production; low salt is described as a major contributor to fatigue and fibromyalgia-type symptoms.
- **Butter:** **about ½ stick of real butter per day** (author's stated minimum for most adults) — roughly **<50 g of fat**. *(Course lesson notes phrase this as "2 tbsp on each meal for butyrate production" — treat as the course-notes paraphrase of the same underlying instruction; the written protocol states the ½-stick/day figure.)* Rationale given: butter supplies hydroxybutyrate as a nutrition source for gut bacteria and is a source of Vitamin A, which the author states is essential for protein absorption. Dairy-protein-allergic patients are advised to try clarified butter/ghee (removes the dairy protein fraction, not the fat).
- **PPI patients:** the author states this protocol should be the **first step** for any patient on omeprazole, Nexium, or Protonix, because these medications impair chloride/acid-dependent nutrient absorption; once the gut is emptied and restored, "it is VERY likely that you will not need to continue your PPI." (Clinical judgment/deprescribing protocol still required — this is the source's stated expectation, not a guarantee.)
- **Oxalate consideration:** patients on vegetarian/vegan/paleo diets with high intake of greens/spinach/kale may be experiencing oxalate-related symptoms (diarrhea, belly pain, cramping, fatigue, brain fog). Suggested test: **trial a carnivore diet for 4–6 weeks** and reassess symptoms. **[Evidence note: this is the author's clinical heuristic, not a validated diagnostic test for oxalate toxicity.]**
- **Post-"gut insult" rule:** any GI illness, antibiotic course, anesthesia, or systemic illness (cold/flu/COVID) is treated as a gut insult requiring either restarting the relevant protocol step or, at minimum, **2–4 weeks of probiotics afterward.**
- **Maintenance after completing the protocol:** if motility slows again, any piece of the protocol can be reused as a "kick start." Long-term constipation prevention = adequate water, 2 tsp salt/day, and adequate fat intake (½ stick butter/day minimum).

## Expected Adverse Effects / Die-Off Warning

A subset of patients will have symptoms during the first 1–2 weeks that feel different or worse than their baseline — this is attributed to bacterial/yeast **die-off**, most notably during Step 2 (GI Synergy). Counsel patients in advance and increase water/salt/broth during this period. Provider should be notified if symptoms are concerning.

## Escalation / When to Order Further Testing

If symptoms do not improve, or are concerning at any point:
- **OAT (Organic Acids Test)** — gut/nutrient status testing.
- **Mycotoxin (mold) panel** — if mold exposure syndrome is suspected.
- Patients with severe leaky gut syndrome or SIBO may need to work the protocol over many months to a couple of years rather than the standard 5–6 months.
