# Atlas FAQ Knowledge — Quick-Recall Facts

A rapid lookup file for Atlas to answer common patient and clinician questions without re-reading the long files. Each entry is one fact, one line, one source map.

---

## Prevalence

- Pinworm in U.S.: ~40 million cases at any time (CDC).
- Toxoplasma seropositive U.S. adults: ~11% (NHANES). Globally up to 60%+.
- Blastocystis in industrialized stool surveys: 5–25% with microscopy, up to 50% with PCR. (file 01)
- Dientamoeba: 0.4–42% depending on assay; higher with PCR. (file 01)
- Giardia U.S.: ~1.2 million cases/year (CDC).
- Chagas U.S.: ~300,000 infected, <1% diagnosed.
- Strongyloides hidden prevalence: Vietnam vets, Appalachia, Gulf states immigrants — under-diagnosed.

---

## Testing Pearls

- Single ova-and-parasite has 30–50% sensitivity. Three samples on alternate days = standard. (file 03)
- GI-MAP qPCR: sensitive for protozoa and select helminths; can miss many helminth eggs.
- Doctor's Data CSAP-x3: best balance of microscopy + PCR for helminths.
- Strongyloides IgG: order in any patient with eosinophilia >500, immigrants from endemic regions, pre-immunosuppression. (file 03)
- Echinococcus serology BEFORE biopsy of any hepatic cyst — biopsy can rupture cyst and trigger anaphylaxis.
- Pre-immunosuppression: always Strongyloides serology + Toxo IgG status documented.

---

## Eosinophilia Triggers

- Tissue-migrating helminths (Ascaris, hookworm, Strongyloides, Toxocara, Trichinella, schistosomes): yes.
- Protozoa (Giardia, Crypto, Entamoeba): typically no.
- Encysted helminths between flares: typically no.
- Other causes: atopy, drug reaction, EGID, EGPA, malignancy, adrenal insufficiency, HIV.

---

## Drug Pearls

- Ivermectin: drug of choice for Strongyloides, scabies, lice. 200 mcg/kg daily x 2, repeat in 2 wks.
- Albendazole: take with fatty meal — absorption increases 5x.
- Praziquantel: not for Fasciola — use triclabendazole.
- Tinidazole 2 g single = giardia/trichomonas; 2 g daily x 3 d = blastocystis.
- Nitazoxanide: best single-agent broad protozoal coverage; first-line cryptosporidiosis in immunocompetent.
- Metronidazole cumulative >30 g → peripheral neuropathy risk.
- Atovaquone + azithromycin: Babesia first-line.
- Pyrantel pamoate 11 mg/kg: OTC pinworm; treat household; repeat in 2 weeks.
- Methylene blue: G6PD test mandatory; serotonin syndrome with SSRIs/MAOIs/triptans — avoid combo.
- Berberine in pregnancy: avoid, especially third trimester (kernicterus).

## Herb Pearls

- Wormwood max ~4-6 weeks continuous; thujone neurotoxicity at high cumulative dose.
- Andrographis: ~1% allergic reactions, can be severe; pre-test small dose first if allergy-prone.
- Cryptolepis: Buhner Babesia/Lyme cornerstone, 5 mL TID.
- Mimosa pudica: 6+ months for Strongyloides, Hymenolepis, Enterobius (autoinfection cycles).
- Black walnut: nut allergy contraindication; tannins reduce iron absorption.
- Berberine: potent CYP3A4 inhibitor — major drug interaction screening before use.
- Oregano oil: encapsulate to avoid mucosal burn; rotate to avoid mucosal damage.
- Pomegranate husk preferred over bark/root (pelletierine alkaloid toxicity in bark).

## Phase Pearls

- Phase 1 must establish daily bowel movements before kills start. (file 05)
- Drainage funnel opens bottom-up (colon first, brain last).
- Lunar pulsing: 3 days before, day of, 3 days after full moon = 7-day window.
- Binders separated from antiparasitics by 1 hr, from supplements/meds by 2 hr.
- S. boulardii safe and helpful Phase 1 through Phase 4.
- Mimosa pudica taken 30 min before food or 2 hr after, with water.

---

## Herx Pearls

- Most common in first 1–3 doses, fades in 48–72 hr.
- Severity 1-2 = good operating zone.
- Severity 3 sustained 48 hr → halve dose.
- Severity 4 (fever, vomiting, neuro) → stop, escalate.
- Differentiation: Herx improves with binders; drug reaction does not.

## Diet Pearls

- Pumpkin seeds 2-4 tbsp/day = cucurbitin, classical anthelmintic.
- Garlic crushed and rested 10 min before eating activates allicin.
- Vinegar soak 1:4 ratio for produce, 5-10 min.
- 12-hour overnight fast standard; 14-16 hour during full-moon pulse.
- No sushi, raw fish, ceviche, raw watercress during active treatment.
- No alcohol during eradication phase.
- Coffee enema okay 1-2x/week if no hemorrhoids/fissures, away from binders.

## Pediatric Pearls

- No wormwood/mugwort under age 6.
- No berberine under age 1 (kernicterus).
- Pyrantel pamoate OTC, 11 mg/kg, household-wide for pinworm.
- Treat household and pets simultaneously.
- Dose herbs as fraction of adult by weight or age band (file 12).

---

## Pregnancy Pearls

- Most antiparasitic herbs and drugs contraindicated.
- Generally safe: ginger, chamomile, garlic food doses, slippery elm, marshmallow, S. boulardii, pumpkin seed (food doses).
- Strict avoid: wormwood, mugwort, tansy, black walnut tincture, berberine, goldenseal, neem, andrographis, artemisinin first trimester.
- Treatment of necessary infections: refer MFM/ID. Spiramycin pre-fetal toxoplasma.

---

## Immunocompromised Pearls

- Always Strongyloides screen before biologics, steroids, transplant.
- CD4 <100 → toxoplasma reactivation risk.
- Cryptosporidium can be protracted; HAART for HIV; nitazoxanide longer course.
- Babesia in asplenia is severe.
- Defer aggressive functional protocols; specialist drives, functional supports.

---

## Lyme/Mold Overlap Pearls

- Address mold environment first.
- Extended Phase 1 (4-8 weeks).
- Buhner stack for Lyme co-infection: cryptolepis, Sida, Houttuynia, knotweed, andrographis.
- Mast cell stabilization before kills.
- Cholestyramine or BAC + Welchol for ochratoxin.
- Methylene blue, lumbrokinase under specialist for biofilm/microcirculation.

---

## Stop Conditions (memorize)

- Severe abdominal pain / vomiting / fever
- Bloody stools
- Jaundice or RUQ pain with vomiting
- New rash with mucosal involvement (SJS warning)
- New focal neuro symptom, severe headache, confusion
- LFTs >3x ULN
- Pregnancy detected
- Hemolysis signs

---

## Re-Test Schedule

- Stool: end Phase 2 (week 12) and 3 months post.
- Strongyloides IgG: 6 and 12 months.
- LFT/CBC: baseline, week 6 if Rx or extended herbal, week 12.
- Eosinophils, IgE, ferritin: baseline, end Phase 2, 3 months.
- Mycotoxin: baseline if mold positive, 6 months post.

## Common Patient Questions — Atlas's Stock Answers

(Mirrored from file 14; faster lookup here.)

- "Do I have parasites?" → Possibly yes — common in U.S.; testing recommended.
- "Why do I feel worse?" → Herx; manage with binders, hydration, sleep.
- "Why full moon?" → Observational pattern of increased parasite activity; we dose stronger that week.
- "Coffee okay?" → Yes, unsweetened.
- "Alcohol?" → No during kills.
- "Pregnant?" → No protocol; refer.
- "Kids?" → Pediatric dose, household-wide for pinworm, no wormwood under 6.
- "Pets?" → Treat concurrently via vet.
- "Will it work?" → Markers normalize; symptoms improve; we re-test.
- "Will it come back?" → Possible; prevention + quarterly maintenance pulses.

## Pitfalls Atlas Should Watch For

- Patient stops Phase 1 prep and jumps to kills → severe Herx
- Skipping binders during Phase 2 → toxin recirculation, neuroinflammation
- Constipation during kills → endotoxin reabsorption → migraine, mood
- Not treating household → reinfection
- Not deworming pets → reinfection
- Skipping Strongyloides screen pre-immunosuppression → hyperinfection
- Berberine + statin or cyclosporine without dose adjustment → toxicity
- Methylene blue + SSRI → serotonin syndrome
- Wormwood >6 weeks continuous → thujone neurotoxicity
- Pregnancy missed at intake → fetal exposure to teratogens
- Aggressive metal chelation during kills → mobilization without binding
- Treating Babesia without atovaquone-azithromycin or ID consult → undertreatment
