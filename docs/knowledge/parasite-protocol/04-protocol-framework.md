# 04 — Protocol Framework: The 4-Phase Functional System

## 1. Why Phases Matter

The most common reason functional medicine parasite protocols fail or harm:
- Killing organisms before drainage and binding capacity is established → severe Herxheimer reactions, mast cell flares, neuroinflammation, mobilized toxin redistribution.
- Killing without restoration → recurrence within months because terrain remains hospitable.
- Treating one organism without addressing co-infections (Candida, H. pylori, mold) → symptom shift, not resolution.

The four-phase model below is synthesized from:
- Klinghardt parasite protocol (drainage, mental toxin clearing, antiparasitics, retoxification prevention)
- Davidson/Watts CellCore "Foundational Protocol" (drainage funnel before binders before kills)
- Microbe Formulas Mimosa Pudica "Para Kit" sequencing
- IFM 4R/5R framework (Remove, Replace, Reinoculate, Repair, Rebalance)
- Buhner herbal protocols (Lyme/co-infection emphasis)

## 2. The Drainage Funnel (foundational concept)

Drainage must open from the bottom up, in this order:

```
   ┌──────────────────────┐
   │  Brain / lymph / skin │   ← top of funnel
   └──────────┬───────────┘
              ▼
   ┌──────────────────────┐
   │  Liver / gallbladder  │
   └──────────┬───────────┘
              ▼
   ┌──────────────────────┐
   │  Kidneys              │
   └──────────┬───────────┘
              ▼
   ┌──────────────────────┐
   │  Colon (open daily)   │   ← bottom of funnel
   └──────────────────────┘
```

If the colon is constipated, opening upper drainage just relocates toxins. **Bowel transit must be regular — minimum once daily, ideally 1–2 well-formed stools — before any kill phase begins.**

## 3. The Four Phases — Overview

| Phase | Duration | Goals | Core tools |
|-------|----------|-------|------------|
| **Phase 1 — Preparation** | 2–4 weeks (longer if Tier 3) | Open drainage, support liver and bile, normalize stool, raise mineral status, calm mast cells | Bowel movers, bile flow, electrolytes, mast cell stabilizers, terrain support |
| **Phase 2 — Eradication** | 4–12 weeks (lunar-cycled) | Kill adult and larval parasites, disrupt biofilms, prevent re-infection | Herbal antiparasitics ± pharmaceuticals, biofilm disruptors, lifestyle controls |
| **Phase 3 — Biofilm + Binding** | Concurrent with Phase 2 and continued 2–4 weeks after | Disrupt protective biofilms, bind mobilized endotoxin, metals, mycotoxins | Enzymes, lumbrokinase/serrapeptase, monolaurin, EDTA-cycled, binders (charcoal, BioActive Carbon, zeolite, chlorella, bentonite) |
| **Phase 4 — Restoration** | 6–12 weeks | Repair gut barrier, restore microbiome, replenish nutrients, retrain immune balance | Glutamine, mucilaginous herbs, probiotics, prebiotics, micronutrients, vagal/HPA support |

Phases overlap. They are not strictly linear. Phase 3 binders run concurrently with Phase 2 kills. Phase 4 restoration begins gently in late Phase 2 and intensifies after kills end.

## 4. The Lunar Cycle Question

Functional practitioners commonly time pulses around the full moon, observing increased parasite shedding and symptom flares in the 3–5 days surrounding it. The mechanism is hypothesized — possibly serotonin-mediated motility shifts or melatonin suppression. Evidence is observational, not RCT-backed.

**Pragmatic position for this protocol:**
- Use full-moon pulses (3 days before, day of, 3 days after = 7-day pulse) when running an intermittent herbal kill protocol.
- For continuous pharmaceutical regimens (e.g., albendazole 3-day course), do not delay treatment for moon timing.
- For severely symptomatic patients, dose continuously and increase intensity at full moon.

## 5. Pulsing vs. Continuous Dosing

| Pattern | When to use |
|---------|-------------|
| **Continuous daily, 4–12 weeks** | Heavy infection, multiple organisms suspected, robust patient with good drainage |
| **Pulse 7 days/month at full moon** | Maintenance, suspected mild infection, sensitive patient who needs lower intensity |
| **Pulse 4 days on / 3 days off** | Combining herbs that benefit from receptor recycling; gentler Herx control |
| **Loading + maintenance** | Mimosa Pudica seed: 4-week build to full dose, then continue 6+ months for autoinfecting helminths |

## 6. Combining Herbs and Pharmaceuticals

In functional practice, three patterns are common:
1. **Herbal-only** (Tier 1–2): mimosa pudica + Para 1/2/3 or Microbe Formulas equivalents + black walnut, wormwood, clove (Hulda Clark triad) for 90 days.
2. **Pharmaceutical sandwich**: Phase 1 herbal prep → 1–2 short pharmaceutical courses (e.g., albendazole, ivermectin, nitazoxanide, tinidazole) at full moons → Phase 4 restoration. Used when specific pathogens identified or symptoms severe.
3. **Sequential by organism**: Treat protozoa first (nitazoxanide or herbal), then helminths (albendazole pulse + mimosa), then complete with biofilm + restoration.

## 7. Co-Infection Sequencing

Do not run aggressive parasite kills in patients with active untreated:
- **Mold colonization / mycotoxin illness** — treat exposure and start binders + drainage first.
- **H. pylori with severe gastritis** — eradicate H. pylori first; PPI use during parasite tx should be minimized.
- **SIBO with severe distension** — partial herbal treatment of SIBO first to reduce gas burden, then proceed.
- **Severe Candida overgrowth** — concurrent antifungal herbs (caprylic acid, undecylenic acid, oregano) help, but reduce antiparasitic dose during overlap to manage Herx.

## 8. Patient Compliance Reality Check

Most protocols fail because patients miss doses or stop early. Build in:
- Daily checklist app (Cronometer, Notion template, paper log)
- Visible pill organizer with phases marked
- Weekly check-ins (Atlas can automate these)
- Family/household coordination — "everyone in the house at once"
- Pet deworming reminder

## 9. Safety Stop-Conditions (mandatory escalation criteria)

Stop the protocol and contact a clinician if any of:
- Severe abdominal pain, vomiting, fever
- Bloody stools
- Jaundice or RUQ pain
- New rash with mucosal involvement (possible drug reaction)
- New confusion, severe headache, focal neurologic symptoms
- LFTs >3x upper limit on monitoring
- Pregnancy discovered mid-protocol
- Hemolysis signs (dark urine, pallor, breathlessness)
