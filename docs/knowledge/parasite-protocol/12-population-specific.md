# 12 — Population-Specific Considerations

## 1. Pediatric

### General principles
- Children carry the highest pinworm prevalence, common Giardia, Blastocystis, Dientamoeba, Hymenolepis. Behavioral, GI, sleep, and skin presentations dominate.
- Dose by weight, not adult tables.
- Liquids, chewables, glycerites preferred over capsules.
- Parents must treat the whole household for pinworm and Hymenolepis; pets dewormed concurrently.
- Avoid alcohol-based tinctures; use glycerites or capsule contents in applesauce.
- No wormwood or thujone-rich herbs under age 6; cautious 6+; favor benzimidazoles by Rx instead.

### Dosing references (pediatric herbal)

| Herb | Pediatric dose |
|------|----------------|
| Mimosa Pudica | Open capsule, give ¼ adult dose for ages 4–8, ½ adult 9–12 |
| Black walnut tincture (low alcohol or glycerite) | 1 drop per year of age, 2x/day, max 10 drops/dose |
| Clove powder | 100–250 mg 2x/day |
| Berberine | NOT under 1 year (kernicterus risk in neonates); cautious >1 yr, 100–250 mg 2x/day |
| Garlic (food) | 1 small clove crushed in food daily |
| Pumpkin seed | 1 tbsp ground daily, mix in smoothie or yogurt |
| Papaya seed | ¼ tsp ground daily |
| Oregano oil | NOT under 5; >5 emulsified 1 drop in carrier 1x/day |
| S. boulardii | 5 billion CFU 1x/day |
| Bone broth | 1 cup daily |
| Triphala | ½ tsp powder at bedtime if constipated |

### Pediatric pharmaceutical defaults
- **Pinworm:** Pyrantel pamoate 11 mg/kg single + repeat in 2 wks (OTC). Or albendazole/mebendazole 100–400 mg per age.
- **Ascaris, hookworm, trichuris:** Albendazole 400 mg single (≥2 yrs); 200 mg if <2 yrs (specialist).
- **Giardia:** Tinidazole 50 mg/kg (max 2 g) single; or nitazoxanide age-banded suspension; or metronidazole 15 mg/kg/d divided x 5 d.
- **Blastocystis (symptomatic):** Nitazoxanide age-banded x 3 d.
- **Strongyloides:** Ivermectin 200 mcg/kg single, repeat 2 wks (>15 kg generally).
- **Tapeworm:** Praziquantel weight-based.

### Pediatric red flags requiring pediatrician/ID
- <1 year of age with any parasite suspicion
- Failure to thrive
- Fever, bloody stools
- Eosinophilia >1500
- Ocular complaint
- Seizures
- Travel/immigration history with tropical exposure

### Atlas guidance
For pediatric protocols Atlas should require parent-confirmed pediatrician/ND oversight. It should not generate Rx-containing protocols for children without practitioner input.

## 2. Pregnancy and Breastfeeding

### Strict avoidance during pregnancy
- Wormwood, mugwort, tansy, rue (abortifacient)
- Black walnut tincture (uterine stimulant, ethanol)
- Berberine (especially third trimester — kernicterus in neonate)
- Goldenseal
- Pomegranate bark/root (pelletierine)
- Neem (abortifacient)
- Andrographis (high dose immune modulation)
- Artemisinin first trimester
- Most pharmaceutical antiparasitics first trimester

### Generally safe / used commonly
- Ginger (nausea, mild antimicrobial)
- Garlic (food doses)
- Pumpkin seeds (food)
- Papaya seeds — controversial, avoid medicinal doses (folkloric abortifacient)
- Chamomile, peppermint, rooibos teas in moderation
- Probiotics including S. boulardii
- Mucilaginous herbs (slippery elm, marshmallow) for GI
- Bone broth, nutrient-dense diet

### When parasites must be treated in pregnancy
- Severe disease, malaria, falciparum, severe babesiosis, invasive amebiasis: ID/MFM specialist
- Uncomplicated giardia: paromomycin (poorly absorbed, safer), metronidazole second-third trimester reasonable
- Toxoplasma in pregnancy: spiramycin pre-fetal infection; pyrimethamine + sulfa + leucovorin if fetal infection confirmed (specialist)
- Always: refer to MFM/ID; do not run a functional protocol solo

### Breastfeeding
- Many drugs and herbs cross into milk; tinidazole/metronidazole pump-and-discard 24–72 h after dose
- Mimosa pudica, garlic food doses, pumpkin seed acceptable
- Avoid wormwood, neem, berberine in nursing infant <6 months

## 3. Immunocompromised (HIV, transplant, chemo, biologics, steroids, primary immunodeficiency)

### Mandatory pre-immunosuppression screen
- Strongyloides serology (IgG ELISA) — anyone from endemic region or with eosinophilia. Empirically treat with ivermectin if positive or borderline.
- Toxoplasma IgG status documented
- TB screen
- Hepatitis B, C
- HIV
- CMV, EBV
- Stool studies if any GI symptoms

### Specific organism risks in immunocompromised
- **Cryptosporidium** — protracted, severe; nitazoxanide longer course, may not clear; HAART for HIV; specialist
- **Toxoplasma** — reactivation with CD4 <100; cerebral toxoplasmosis emergency
- **Strongyloides hyperinfection** — disseminated, gram-neg sepsis, ARDS, mortality 50–80% if missed; do not give steroids without prior ivermectin
- **Microsporidia** — albendazole or fumagillin
- **Babesia** — severe in asplenia, malignancy, HIV; combination IV therapy + transfusion
- **Chagas reactivation** — specialist
- **Disseminated Strongyloides** — ID, ICU

### Functional adjuncts that are still safe and helpful
- Quality nutrition, bone broth
- Probiotics (may avoid certain strains in severe neutropenia)
- Vitamin D, zinc, selenium
- Glutathione/NAC
- Adequate hydration, electrolytes
- Lactoferrin
- Mushroom blends carefully in some transplant patients (immunomodulation)
- Avoid binder timing conflicts with critical immunosuppressants

### Atlas guidance
Tier 4 immunocompromised cases default to a "refer to specialist" output with supportive functional recommendations only.

## 4. Chronic Lyme / Co-infection / Mold / MCAS / CIRS overlap

### Heuristic
Functional medicine parasite cases often coexist with:
- Borrelia burgdorferi and co-infections (Bartonella, Babesia, Anaplasma, Ehrlichia, Mycoplasma)
- Mold colonization and mycotoxin illness (Stachybotrys, Aspergillus, Penicillium, Wallemia)
- Mast cell activation syndrome
- Chronic viral reactivation (EBV, HHV-6, CMV)
- POTS / dysautonomia
- Connective tissue laxity (hypermobility, EDS)

### Sequencing principles
1. Address mold exposure first — cannot detox in a moldy building
2. Open drainage — extended Phase 1 (4–8 weeks)
3. Stabilize mast cells before any heavy kill
4. Treat parasites and Lyme co-infections together with overlapping herbs (Buhner protocols)
5. Use binders aggressively
6. Methylene blue, lumbrokinase under specialist for biofilm + microcirculation
7. Slow, low, and consistent — never aggressive in this population

### Buhner core overlap
- Cryptolepis (Babesia)
- Sida acuta (Babesia)
- Houttuynia (Bartonella)
- Japanese knotweed (Borrelia, broad)
- Andrographis (Borrelia, immune)
- Cat's claw (Borrelia immune)
- These layer with mimosa pudica and binders

## 5. Elderly

- Reduced LFT/renal reserve — choose lower doses, monitor closely
- Polypharmacy interactions — berberine, oregano CYP cautions
- Constipation more common — emphasize magnesium, hydration
- Fall risk during heavy Herx — supervise
- Cognitive monitoring during methylene blue or any neuro-active herb
- Frailty — adequate protein, glycine, mineral support is non-negotiable

---

## 6. Pets and Household

- Pets are reservoirs. Treat all dogs and cats with vet-prescribed dewormers concurrently with the index patient.
- Common veterinary anthelmintics: pyrantel, fenbendazole, praziquantel combo (Drontal, Profender)
- Heartworm prevention covers most filaria and some roundworm
- Wash pet bedding hot weekly
- Litter boxes scooped daily; pregnant women never handle (Toxoplasma)
- Yards and sand boxes: cover, remove feces, don't let kids play barefoot in contaminated areas
- Reptile owners — Salmonella + parasitic risk; hand wash
- Outdoor dogs in tick areas — preventives, daily tick checks

## 7. Travel and Returning Travelers

### Pre-travel
- Update vaccines per CDC
- Discuss malaria prophylaxis if endemic destination
- Pack: mimosa pudica, S. boulardii, berberine, oregano oil capsules, electrolytes, charcoal, bismuth
- Water filtration plan

### During travel
- Bottled or filtered water only; no ice
- Hot, freshly cooked food; "boil it, peel it, cook it, or forget it"
- Avoid raw salads, undercooked meat
- Avoid freshwater swimming in endemic areas (schistosomiasis)
- Avoid barefoot on contaminated soil
- DEET, permethrin clothing for mosquitoes/ticks
- Hand hygiene

### Post-travel screening
- Any new GI, fever, rash, or fatigue → physician evaluation
- Thick/thin smear if febrile and malaria-endemic
- Strongyloides serology if eosinophilia or skin tracks
- Schisto serology if freshwater exposure
- Multi-target stool PCR + O&P x 3
- Consider empiric praziquantel for confirmed schisto exposure even if asymptomatic (CDC)
