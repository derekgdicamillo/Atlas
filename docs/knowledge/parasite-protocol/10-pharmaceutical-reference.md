# 10 — Pharmaceutical Reference

Functional medicine is not anti-pharmaceutical. For confirmed pathogens, severe disease, autoinfecting organisms, immunocompromised patients, and certain protozoa where herbs are inadequate, drugs are the right tool. This file is a clinical reference for the antiparasitics most commonly used in adult outpatient practice.

---

## 1. Albendazole (Albenza)

- **Class:** Benzimidazole; binds parasite β-tubulin → microtubule disruption → glucose uptake failure
- **Spectrum:** Most nematodes (ascaris, hookworm, whipworm, pinworm, Strongyloides as adjunct), cestodes (Taenia, hydatid, neurocysticercosis), microsporidia, trichinellosis
- **Adult dose:**
  - Pinworm: 400 mg single, repeat in 2 wks
  - Hookworm, ascaris, trichuris: 400 mg single (or BID x 3 d for heavy)
  - Strongyloides adjunct: 400 mg BID x 7 d
  - Cysticercosis: 15 mg/kg/d in 2 doses x 8–30 d (with steroids)
  - Hydatid: 10–15 mg/kg/d for 1–6 months in cycles
- **Pediatric:** 200 mg if <2 yrs (limited data — specialist), 400 mg ≥2 yrs same as adult for most indications
- **Take with:** Fatty meal — increases absorption 5x
- **Adverse effects:** LFT elevation (monitor), GI, headache, alopecia, neutropenia (rare)
- **Cautions:** Pregnancy category C → avoid first trimester; bone marrow suppression risk in extended courses; ocular cysticercosis (precipitates blindness without steroid coverage)
- **Drug interactions:** Cimetidine, dexamethasone, praziquantel raise levels; carbamazepine, phenytoin lower
- **Monitoring:** LFTs and CBC at baseline, day 14, monthly if prolonged

## 2. Mebendazole (Vermox, Emverm)

- **Class:** Benzimidazole, similar mechanism to albendazole
- **Spectrum:** Pinworm, ascaris, hookworm, whipworm
- **Adult dose:**
  - Pinworm: 100 mg single, repeat in 2 wks
  - Ascaris, hookworm, trichuris: 100 mg BID x 3 d (or 500 mg single)
- **Pediatric:** Same as adult ≥2 yrs
- **Adverse effects:** Mild GI; LFT rarely
- **Cautions:** Pregnancy category C
- **Note:** Less systemic absorption than albendazole — better for luminal-only indications

## 3. Ivermectin (Stromectol)

- **Class:** Avermectin; potentiates GABA-gated chloride channels in invertebrates → paralysis
- **Spectrum:** Strongyloides (drug of choice), scabies, lice, lymphatic filariasis (Wuchereria), Loa loa, onchocerciasis, gnathostomiasis, cutaneous larva migrans
- **Adult dose:**
  - Strongyloides: 200 mcg/kg PO daily x 2 d, repeat in 2 wks (extended in disseminated)
  - Scabies: 200 mcg/kg single, repeat in 1–2 wks
  - Onchocerciasis: 150 mcg/kg every 6–12 months
- **Take with:** Empty stomach historically; with food increases bioavailability
- **Adverse effects:** Mazzotti reaction in onchocerciasis; mild GI
- **Cautions:** Loa loa heavy infection — encephalopathy risk; pregnancy category C (avoid if possible); CNS infections — penetrates BBB in inflammation
- **Drug interactions:** Warfarin (rare INR shift); CYP3A4 substrate (caution with strong inhibitors)

## 4. Praziquantel (Biltricide)

- **Class:** Increases parasite cell membrane permeability to calcium → tetanic paralysis and tegument damage
- **Spectrum:** All schistosomes, all flukes (Clonorchis, Opisthorchis, Fasciola variable, Paragonimus), tapeworms (Taenia, Hymenolepis, Diphyllobothrium), cysticercosis adjunct
- **Adult dose:**
  - Schisto haematobium/mansoni: 40 mg/kg single
  - Schisto japonicum: 60 mg/kg in 2–3 doses single day
  - Taenia: 5–10 mg/kg single
  - Hymenolepis: 25 mg/kg single
  - Clonorchis/Opisthorchis: 25 mg/kg TID x 1 d
  - Cysticercosis: 50 mg/kg/d divided x 14–30 d (with steroids and albendazole)
  - Fasciola: NOT effective — use triclabendazole
- **Take with:** Food (increases absorption)
- **Adverse effects:** GI, dizziness, headache; severe in heavy schistosome load (Mazzotti-like)
- **Cautions:** Ocular cysticercosis (vision loss without steroid); pregnancy avoid first trimester; not for cysticercosis without specialist

## 5. Nitazoxanide (Alinia)

- **Class:** Thiazolide; inhibits pyruvate-ferredoxin oxidoreductase
- **Spectrum:** Cryptosporidium (drug of choice in immunocompetent), Giardia, Blastocystis, Entamoeba (with paromomycin), broad protozoal, some helminth, viral adjunct
- **Adult dose:** 500 mg PO BID x 3 d (extend to 7–14 d if immunocompromised)
- **Pediatric:** Suspension dose-by-age (CDC table)
- **Take with:** Food
- **Adverse effects:** Mild GI, headache; greenish urine
- **Cautions:** Pregnancy B; well tolerated overall
- **Note:** Best protozoal coverage with single agent; useful first-line for Blastocystis empiric

## 6. Tinidazole (Tindamax)

- **Class:** Nitroimidazole — same family as metronidazole, longer half-life, better tolerated
- **Spectrum:** Giardia, amebiasis (luminal + invasive), trichomoniasis, Blastocystis, anaerobes
- **Adult dose:**
  - Giardia: 2 g single
  - Trichomonas: 2 g single (treat partner)
  - Amebic colitis: 2 g daily x 3 d
  - Amebic liver abscess: 2 g daily x 3–5 d
  - Blastocystis: 2 g daily x 3 d
- **Take with:** Food (reduces GI upset)
- **Adverse effects:** Metallic taste, GI, headache, mild neuro
- **Cautions:** Disulfiram-like with alcohol (avoid 72 h after dose); pregnancy first trimester avoid; lactation pump-and-discard 72 h
- **Notes:** Generally preferred over metronidazole for tolerability and shorter course

## 7. Metronidazole (Flagyl)

- **Class:** Nitroimidazole
- **Spectrum:** Giardia, Trichomonas, amebiasis, Dientamoeba, anaerobes
- **Adult dose:**
  - Giardia: 250 mg TID x 5 d
  - Trichomonas: 2 g single or 500 mg BID x 7 d
  - Amebic colitis: 750 mg TID x 7–10 d (followed by paromomycin for luminal)
  - Dientamoeba: 500–750 mg TID x 10 d
- **Adverse effects:** Metallic taste, neuropathy with prolonged use, GI, disulfiram reaction
- **Cautions:** Avoid alcohol 72 h after; pregnancy first trimester avoid; CNS — encephalopathy with prolonged dosing
- **Notes:** Heavy peripheral neuropathy risk if cumulative dose exceeds ~30 g — limits chronic Lyme/Babesia repurposing

## 8. Paromomycin (Humatin)

- **Class:** Aminoglycoside, oral, non-absorbed
- **Spectrum:** Luminal amebiasis (after invasive treated), cryptosporidiosis (HIV adjunct), tapeworms (Taenia), Dientamoeba, Leishmania
- **Adult dose:**
  - Luminal amebiasis: 25–35 mg/kg/d divided TID x 7 d
  - Cryptosporidium HIV: 1 g BID x 14 d
  - Tapeworm: 1 g q15 min x 4 doses
- **Adverse effects:** GI; if absorbed (renal failure, mucosal ulceration) → ototoxicity, nephrotoxicity
- **Cautions:** Use with caution in renal failure or extensive bowel disease; pregnancy okay (not absorbed)

## 9. Atovaquone (Mepron) ± Azithromycin (Zithromax)

- **Class:** Atovaquone — ubiquinone analog, blocks mitochondrial electron transport in parasite
- **Spectrum:** Babesia (drug of choice combo), Pneumocystis, Toxoplasma adjunct
- **Adult dose (Babesia):** Atovaquone 750 mg PO q12h + azithromycin 500–1000 mg PO daily x 7–10 d (mild) or 6+ wks (immunocompromised, severe)
- **Take with:** Fatty meal (atovaquone absorption increases 3x)
- **Adverse effects:** GI, rash, LFT elevation, headache; azithromycin QT
- **Drug interactions:** Rifampin lowers atovaquone; warfarin caution with azithromycin
- **Notes:** Add clindamycin + quinine if severe babesiosis; specialist

## 10. Pyrimethamine + Sulfadiazine + Leucovorin

- **Spectrum:** Toxoplasmosis encephalitis, congenital toxoplasmosis
- **Use:** Specialist only; 6+ weeks therapy with steroids, leucovorin to prevent bone marrow suppression
- **Cautions:** Pancytopenia; SJS; pregnancy

## 11. Antimalarials (overview — specialty)

- **Chloroquine, hydroxychloroquine** — sensitive Plasmodium vivax/ovale/malariae/falciparum (rare)
- **Mefloquine, atovaquone-proguanil (Malarone), artemether-lumefantrine (Coartem)** — falciparum
- **Primaquine, tafenoquine** — radical cure for vivax/ovale hypnozoites (G6PD test mandatory)
- **Artesunate** — severe malaria (IV)
- **Reference:** CDC Yellow Book and Treatment Guidelines for current dosing

## 12. Triclabendazole (Egaten)

- **Spectrum:** Fasciola hepatica (drug of choice)
- **Dose adult:** 10 mg/kg single, repeat in 12–24 h if heavy
- **Source:** CDC IND in U.S.

## 13. Diethylcarbamazine (DEC)

- **Spectrum:** Lymphatic filariasis (Wuchereria, Brugia), Loa loa
- **Dose:** Specialist
- **Cautions:** Severe Mazzotti reaction in onchocerciasis (use ivermectin first); CDC IND

## 14. Benznidazole / Nifurtimox

- **Spectrum:** Trypanosoma cruzi (Chagas disease)
- **Use:** Specialist; toxic profile; works best in acute and early chronic; cardiomyopathy adjunct
- **Source:** CDC

## 15. Methylene Blue (off-label adjunct in functional/Lyme)

- **Use:** Adjunct in Babesia, Bartonella, biofilm; mitochondrial support
- **Dose:** 0.5–2 mg/kg/d, divided
- **Cautions:** G6PD (hemolysis); serotonergic interaction (avoid SSRIs, MAOIs, triptans — serotonin syndrome); blue urine
- **Specialist required**

## 16. Pyrantel pamoate (Reese's Pinworm Medicine OTC)

- **Spectrum:** Pinworm, ascaris, hookworm
- **Adult dose:** 11 mg/kg (max 1 g) single, repeat in 2 wks
- **Pediatric ≥2 yrs:** Same mg/kg
- **OTC** — household pinworm convenient option

## 17. Pharmaceutical-Specific Workflow

### Pre-prescription checks
- Pregnancy test if reproductive-age female
- Baseline LFTs and CBC for any extended (>3 day) course
- Renal function for paromomycin, metronidazole high dose
- G6PD for primaquine/methylene blue
- Drug interaction check (CYP, QT-prolonging combinations)
- Ocular exam if cysticercosis or onchocerciasis suspected before kill (prevent blinding inflammation)
- Steroid premedication if cysticercosis or heavy schisto

### Common combinations in functional practice
- Tinidazole + nitazoxanide stack for refractory Blastocystis
- Albendazole + ivermectin for Strongyloides hyperinfection
- Atovaquone + azithromycin + cryptolepis + artemisinin for Babesia
- Praziquantel + albendazole + steroid for cysticercosis
- Nitazoxanide + bovine colostrum for cryptosporidiosis in mild immunocompromise

### Functional adjuncts to always consider during Rx
- Saccharomyces boulardii through and after
- TUDCA + glutathione for hepatic protection
- Binder 1 hr after Rx dose
- Magnesium and electrolyte support
- Probiotic restoration after course completes
