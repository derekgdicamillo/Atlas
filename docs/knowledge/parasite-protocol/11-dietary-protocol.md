# 11 — Anti-Parasite Dietary Protocol

Diet is the third leg of the stool with herbs/Rx and drainage. Parasites feed on simple sugars, alcohol, and refined carbohydrate; biofilms thrive on excess sugar and dysbiotic substrates; mucosal repair needs nutrient density. The protocol below is a 12–16 week eating plan synced to the four phases.

## 1. The Big Removals (start day 1, maintain throughout)

| Food | Why |
|------|-----|
| **Refined sugar, HFCS, agave, "natural" sugars in excess** | Direct parasite and Candida fuel; insulin spikes promote inflammation |
| **Alcohol** | Hepatic burden during detox; immune suppression; sugar load |
| **Pork, especially undercooked** | Trichinella, Taenia solium risk; immune-modulating compounds |
| **Raw/undercooked beef, fish, sushi, ceviche** | Helminth and protozoa risk during active treatment |
| **Conventional dairy** | Mucus production, immune-mod; lactose if Giardia-induced lactase deficiency |
| **Gluten (wheat, barley, rye)** | Zonulin upregulation → intestinal permeability; cross-reactivity in autoimmune patients |
| **Refined seed oils (soy, corn, canola, cottonseed, sunflower)** | Pro-inflammatory; lipid peroxidation; mitochondrial stress |
| **Processed/ultra-processed food** | Additives, emulsifiers degrade mucin layer |
| **Artificial sweeteners (especially sucralose, saccharin)** | Microbiome disruption |
| **High-mercury fish (tuna, swordfish, king mackerel, tilefish)** | Adds metal load during chelation-prone phase |

## 2. The Big Additions (daily)

### 2.1 Antiparasitic foods

| Food | Dose | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| **Raw pumpkin seeds** | 2–4 tbsp/day, ground if possible | Cucurbitin paralyzes worms; zinc, magnesium |

| **Pomegranate seeds + arils** | ½–1 cup or 4 oz juice/day | Punicalagins, ellagic acid |

| **Pineapple (fresh, with core)** | ½ cup/day | Bromelain (mild proteolytic, biofilm) |
| **Coconut (oil, milk, flesh)** | 1–2 tbsp oil/day | Caprylic, lauric acids — antifungal/antimicrobial |
| **Apple cider vinegar (with mother)** | 1–2 tbsp in water before meals | Digestive support, mild antimicrobial |
| **Carrots (raw, daily)** | 1–2 medium/day | Pectin, beta-carotene; classic folk anthelmintic |
| **Bitter greens (arugula, dandelion, mustard, watercress)** | 1–2 cups/day | Bile flow; choleretic |
| **Cilantro and parsley** | Liberal | Mild metal binding, fresh phytochemicals |
| **Turmeric, ginger, oregano, thyme, rosemary, sage, cumin, fennel** | Used liberally | Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory |
| **Cruciferous (broccoli, kale, cabbage, Brussels)** | 1–2 cups/day | Sulforaphane → Phase II detox; goitrogen tip — cook if hypothyroid |
| **Sea vegetables (kelp, dulse, nori)** | 1–2 servings/week | Iodine, alginate (binder) |

### 2.2 Mucosal and gut healing foods

- **Bone broth** 1–2 cups/day (gelatin, glycine, proline, minerals)
- **Slow-cooked stews and soups**
- **Avocado** ½–1/day (monounsaturated fat, fiber)
- **Olive oil (cold-pressed)** 1–2 tbsp/day
- **Wild-caught low-mercury fish (sardines, anchovies, salmon, herring)** 2–3x/week
- **Pasture-raised eggs** if tolerated, 1–3/day
- **Grass-fed/finished beef, lamb, bison** 4–6 oz with meals
- **Pasture-raised poultry** 4–6 oz
- **Liver and organ meats** 4 oz weekly (B12, retinol, copper, choline)

### 2.3 Hydration

- **Water** 30–35 mL per kg body weight per day
- **Electrolytes** — sodium, potassium, magnesium daily
- **Herbal teas** — nettle, dandelion, ginger, fennel, peppermint, chamomile, pau d'arco
- **Lemon water in morning** — bile stimulation
- **Coconut water** for potassium (avoid sweetened)

## 3. Phase-by-Phase Diet

### Phase 1 (Preparation, 2–4 weeks)

- Begin removals
- Begin additions
- Light eating, focus on vegetables, broths, gentle proteins
- Pre-meal bitters
- Establish 12-hour overnight fast (e.g., 7pm-7am)
- Keep carbs moderate (50–100 g/day) to prepare for kill phase

### Phase 2 (Eradication, 4–12 weeks)

- Strict removals enforced
- Pumpkin seeds, garlic, papaya seeds daily
- Reduce all sugars including high-glycemic fruit (limit to berries, apple, citrus)
- Emphasize bone broth and easy-to-digest proteins during heavy Herx weeks
- Smoothies with chlorella, papaya seed, ginger, cilantro, lemon
- Continue 12-hour fast minimum; experienced patients can do 14–16 hour during full-moon pulse weeks
- Avoid raw fish, raw meat, sushi entirely
- Wash all produce thoroughly (vinegar soak or veggie wash)

### Phase 3 (Biofilm + binders) — concurrent with Phase 2

- Add fiber gradually if tolerated — psyllium, PHGG, ground flax, chia
- Watch binder timing — separate from food by 1 hr

### Phase 4 (Restoration, 6–12 weeks)

- Slowly reintroduce fermented foods if tolerated (sauerkraut, kimchi, kefir)
- Add prebiotic foods — onion, garlic, leek, asparagus, jerusalem artichoke (start tiny if SIBO history)
- Reintroduce eliminated foods one at a time, 4-day rotations to identify reactivity
- Consider gluten reintroduction trial only if no autoimmune disease, with sourdough fermented at least 24 hr
- Dairy reintroduction: A2/Jersey cow, raw if available, fermented (yogurt/kefir), and gradual
- Optional reintroduction of moderate clean carbs (potato, rice, oats) for athletic patients

## 6. Fasting Considerations

- 12-hour overnight fast: standard, daily
- 14–16 hour intermittent fast: useful at full-moon pulse for parasite stress
- 24-hour fast: occasional, advanced patients only, with electrolytes
- Multi-day fasts: NOT during active kill phase — too much detox load without nutrient input
- Water-only fasting plus parasite kills can produce severe Herx; keep food coming through eradication

## 7. Special Diet Considerations

### MCAS / histamine
- Skip fermented foods early
- Avoid leftovers >24 hr
- Avoid aged meats, cheeses, vinegar (use lemon for acidity)
- Use freshly cooked/frozen, not aged

### SIBO overlap
- Low FODMAP through Phase 2
- Avoid fermentable fibers initially; titrate in Phase 4
- Avoid raw cruciferous; cook to break down

### Oxalate sensitivity
- Reduce spinach, beets, almonds, sweet potato, dark chocolate
- Adequate calcium, citrate

### Carnivore / animal-based variant
- Some Klinghardt-leaning patients do well on near-carnivore + clean salt + water
- Add organ meats and bone broth heavily
- Add only the strongest plant antimicrobials (garlic, oregano, herbs in small amounts)

### Pediatric
- Smoothies, soups, hidden additions
- Pumpkin seed butter on apple slices
- Bone broth in everything
- Clear non-negotiables for sugar, processed snacks, juice

## 8. Cooking and Food-Safety Notes

- Wash all produce with vinegar soak (1 cup vinegar + 4 cups water, 5–10 min, rinse) or commercial veggie wash
- Cook meat to safe temps: poultry 165°F, ground meat 160°F, whole cuts beef/lamb 145°F
- Avoid raw fish, sushi, ceviche, rare game during active treatment
- Avoid raw watercress, raw freshwater plants (Fasciola)
- Filter all drinking water (1-micron sediment + carbon + UV ideal); never untreated stream/well
- Travel: bottled or filtered water, peeled fruit, no salads, no ice
- Hands washed before eating, after bathroom, after pets

## 9. Supplement-Diet Integration Reminders

- Pumpkin seeds, garlic, papaya seeds work better with protocol than alone
- Bone broth and glycine pair with glutathione synthesis during kills
- Cruciferous + glutathione + sulforaphane stack supports Phase II
- Coffee enema candidates: cold-brewed organic, mid-morning, away from binder doses
- Avoid grapefruit juice during pharmaceutical phases — CYP3A4 inhibition
