# 08 — Phase 4: Restoration (6–12 Weeks)

## Goal

Repair the gut barrier, restore microbiome diversity, replenish nutrients lost during illness and treatment, retrain immune balance, support mitochondria and HPA axis, and lock in resilience so reinfection doesn't take hold.

## 1. The IFM 5R Framework Applied

| R | Action |
|---|--------|
| **Remove** | Continue to remove triggers — finish kills, clear remaining mold/H. pylori/SIBO, eliminate trigger foods identified during phase 2 |
| **Replace** | Restore digestive capacity — HCl, bile, pancreatic enzymes |
| **Reinoculate** | Probiotics + prebiotics + fermented foods |
| **Repair** | Mucosal repair — glutamine, zinc carnosine, mucilage, polyphenols |
| **Rebalance** | Vagal tone, sleep, stress, exercise, sunlight |

## 3. Microbiome Reinoculation

### 3.1 Probiotic strategy

Rotate strains across 8–12 weeks. Single-strain mega-doses are not the goal; diverse, evidence-supported strains are.

| Strain / formula | Dose | Notes |
|------------------|------|-------|
| **Saccharomyces boulardii** | 5–10 billion CFU 2x/day | Continue from Phase 2; protects against C. difficile, supports SIgA |
| **Lactobacillus rhamnosus GG** | 10 billion 1x/day | Pediatric and adult; prevents AAD |
| **Bifidobacterium longum, B. infantis, B. breve** | 10–25 billion daily | Mucosal repair, immune balance |
| **Lactobacillus reuteri (Gastrus, BioGaia)** | 1–2 lozenges/day | H. pylori, motility, mood |
| **VSL#3 / Visbiome** | 1 sachet/day | High-dose, IBD/UC support |
| **MegaSporeBiotic / Just Thrive (Bacillus spores)** | 1–2 caps/day with food | Spore-based; immune-mod; useful in dysbiosis |
| **Lactobacillus plantarum** | 5–10 billion daily | Lumen support, histamine-friendly |
| **Lactobacillus paracasei, L. acidophilus** | 5–10 billion daily | Standard maintenance |
| **Akkermansia muciniphila (Pendulum, Akkermansia)** | 1 cap/day | Mucin layer rebuilding, metabolic |

Avoid high-histamine probiotics in MCAS patients early in restoration: limit L. casei, L. bulgaricus, L. helveticus until tolerance returns.

### 3.2 Prebiotic strategy

Begin slowly to avoid SIBO flare. Start at 1 g and titrate up.

- **Partially hydrolyzed guar gum (Sunfiber, PHGG)** 5–10 g/day — gentlest; works for SIBO recovery
- **Inulin (chicory root)** 5–10 g/day — feeds Bifidobacteria; can flare SIBO
- **GOS (Bimuno)** 2.75 g/day — anxiolytic, immune
- **FOS** 5–10 g/day
- **Resistant starch (potato starch, green banana flour)** 1 tbsp building to 4 tbsp/day
- **Larch arabinogalactan** 2–4 g/day — immune + bifidogenic
- **Beta-glucan** 250–500 mg/day — immune training

### 3.3 Fermented foods

Reintroduce gradually if tolerated:
- Sauerkraut 1–2 tbsp/day
- Kimchi 1–2 tbsp/day
- Yogurt or kefir (organic, grass-fed if dairy-tolerated, or coconut)
- Water kefir, kombucha (low sugar varieties)
- Miso, natto (vitamin K2)
- Lacto-fermented vegetables

## 4. Nutrient Replenishment

Parasites and inflammation deplete specific nutrients. Restore them aggressively.

| Nutrient | Why depleted | Repletion |
|---------|--------------|-----------|
| **Iron** | Hookworm, schisto, Trichuris, chronic inflammation reducing absorption | Ferritin-guided; iron bisglycinate 25–50 mg with vitamin C; recheck at 8 weeks |
| **B12** | Diphyllobothrium, atrophic gastritis, Strongyloides | Methyl-B12 1–5 mg/day sublingual; injection if MMA elevated |
| **Folate** | Dysbiosis, malabsorption | Methylfolate 400–800 mcg/day |
| **Vitamin D** | Inflammation, low sun, malabsorption | D3 + K2 to target 50–80 ng/mL |
| **Zinc** | Enterocyte repair, immune | 15–30 mg/day with copper 1–2 mg if long-term |
| **Magnesium** | Continued from Phase 1; relaxation | 400–800 mg/day |
| **Selenium** | Glutathione peroxidase | 100–200 mcg/day |
| **Iodine** | Mucosal antimicrobial, thyroid | 150–300 mcg/day if not contraindicated |
| **Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)** | Anti-inflammatory | 2–3 g combined daily |
| **Choline / phosphatidylcholine** | Liver, membranes | 500–1800 mg/day |
| **Carnitine** | Fatigue, mitochondrial | 1–2 g/day |
| **CoQ10 (ubiquinol)** | Mitochondrial | 100–300 mg/day |
| **NAD+ precursors (NMN/NR)** | Mitochondrial recovery | 250–500 mg/day if energy still poor |
| **Glycine** | Glutathione, sleep, collagen | 3–5 g at bedtime |

## 5. Mitochondrial and Energy Recovery

Chronic infection drains mitochondrial reserve. After kills, support:

- CoQ10/ubiquinol 200 mg/day
- Acetyl-L-carnitine 1–2 g/day
- D-Ribose 5 g 1–3x/day for ATP regeneration
- Pyrroloquinoline quinone (PQQ) 10–20 mg/day for mitochondrial biogenesis
- Magnesium malate 1–2 g/day
- B-complex with active forms
- Methylene blue 0.5–1 mg/kg under specialist for severe fatigue/cognitive recovery
- Red light therapy 10–20 min daily (660/850 nm)
- Sunlight 20–30 min daily
- Sleep prioritization 7–9 h
- Zone-2 cardio 150 min/week + 2 strength sessions

## 6. Immune Rebalancing

Long parasite infections skew Th2. Restoration brings Th1/Th2/Treg balance back.

- Vitamin D + K2 to target
- Zinc + selenium
- Mushroom blends (reishi, cordyceps, lion's mane, turkey tail) 1–2 g/day
- Probiotic diversity (above)
- Sleep
- Hot/cold contrast (sauna + cold plunge) 3x/week
- Sunlight, vitamin D
- Bone broth, glycine
- Lactoferrin 250 mg 2x/day x 60 days
- Beta-glucan 500 mg/day
- Address chronic underlying triggers (mold, EBV reactivation, oral biofilm)

## 7. HPA Axis and Vagal Tone

- Adaptogens — ashwagandha 600 mg/day, rhodiola 200–400 mg AM, holy basil
- Phosphatidylserine 100–300 mg PM if cortisol high at night
- L-theanine 200 mg 1–3x/day
- Magnesium glycinate or threonate at bedtime
- Vagal tone — humming, gargling, cold face plunge, breathwork (4-7-8, box), Wim Hof
- HRV monitoring (Oura, Whoop)
- Neurofeedback or somatic work if trauma overlap
- Sleep hygiene — cool, dark, 7–9 h, consistent timing
- Sunrise exposure, blue light blockers PM
- Reduce alcohol, caffeine after noon

## 8. Hormonal Repletion (women)

Parasite illness commonly disrupts cycles, depletes progesterone, raises prolactin.
- Reassess cycle 3 months post-clearance
- Vitex 400 mg/day x 3 months if anovulatory or short luteal
- Adrenal cortex support if HPA flat
- DUTCH or full cycle mapping if persistent dysfunction
- Continue B6 (P5P 25–50 mg) for PMS/methylation

## 9. Pet and Household Decontamination (final pass)

To prevent reinfection:
- Deworm all dogs and cats per vet (often pyrantel + praziquantel, or fenbendazole)
- Wash all bedding, towels, pet bedding hot
- Vacuum + steam carpets
- Clean shoes left at door, wipe yoga mats
- Treat any recurring household members concurrently
- Sand/play areas — cover sandboxes; check children's hands
- Water source — well water tested and filtered (UV + 1 micron sediment + carbon)

## 10. Maintenance Protocol (long-term)

Once restored, maintain resilience with:

- Mimosa Pudica 2 caps/day at full moon week monthly (lifetime maintenance)
- Quarterly herbal pulse: black walnut + clove + wormwood for 1 week at full moon
- Annual stool retest if symptomatic; biennial if asymptomatic and risk factors persist
- Continue probiotic rotation
- Continue sauna, sunlight, sleep, movement
- Annual Strongyloides serology if first infection was Strongyloides (declining titer confirms cure)
- Empiric annual deworm of household pets
- Travel prep — pack mimosa pudica, berberine, S. boulardii, oregano oil; herbal prophylaxis pre-travel

## 11. End-of-Phase-4 Markers

Indicators of full restoration:
- Daily formed bowel movements without aids
- Energy 8/10 or above
- Sleep 7+ h restorative
- No skin reactivity
- Eosinophils, IgE, ferritin, B12, D normalized
- Stool retest clear
- HRV improved
- Mood stable
- Resumed normal exercise capacity
- Cognitive clarity restored
