# 07 — Phase 3: Biofilm Disruption + Toxin Binding

## Goal

Strip the polysaccharide–metal–microbial biofilm matrix that shelters parasites and pathogens, and bind mobilized endotoxin (LPS), heavy metals, mycotoxins, ammonia, and bile-bound parasitic byproducts so they leave the body rather than recirculate. Phase 3 runs **concurrently with Phase 2** and continues 2–4 weeks after kills end.

## 1. Biofilm Background

Biofilms are extracellular polymeric matrices secreted by communities of bacteria, fungi, and parasites. They:
- Concentrate divalent cations (Fe, Ca, Mg, Mn) and heavy metals (Hg, Pb, Cd)
- Reduce antimicrobial penetration 100–1000-fold
- Coordinate quorum-sensing for collective virulence
- Are seeded by Klebsiella, Pseudomonas, Candida, Borrelia, and certain protozoa
- Persist in the biliary tree, sinuses, mucosal surfaces, and indwelling materials

Parasites exploit biofilms as sanctuaries. Stripping biofilms exposes organisms to antiparasitics and immune surveillance.

### Practical sequencing
1. Start enzymes early in Phase 2 — gives 2 hours of empty-stomach action before herbal kill dose.
2. Cycle bismuth or EDTA in 4-week courses with mineral repletion breaks.
3. Monolaurin is gentle and continuous-friendly.
4. Avoid combining multiple potent fibrinolytic enzymes if patient is on anticoagulants without medical supervision (bleeding risk).

## 3. Binders: The Workhorse of Functional Detox

Binders sit in the GI lumen and trap toxins, parasitic waste, mycotoxins, and metals so they exit in stool rather than being reabsorbed via enterohepatic circulation. **Without binders, kills produce Herx; with binders, kills are tolerated.**

### 3.1 Binder selection by target

| Binder | Best at binding | Notes |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| **Activated charcoal (coconut shell preferred)** | LPS, broad endotoxin, drugs, ochratoxin partial | Cheap, broad. Will bind drugs/supplements — separate by 2 hr. Constipation risk; pair with magnesium. |
| **Bentonite clay (sodium or calcium)** | Aflatoxin, broad mycotoxins, bacterial toxins | Use food-grade liquid (Living Clay, Yerba Prima Great Plains Bentonite). Hydrate well. |
| **Chlorella (broken cell wall)** | Mercury, lead, dioxins, mild mycotoxin | Sun Chlorella, Recovery Bits, BioPure. Start low (3 tabs); titrate to 20–40/day. |
| **Modified citrus pectin (PectaSol-C)** | Lead, gadolinium, galectin-3 | Less binding of nutrients; safer for chronic use. |
| **Zeolite (clinoptilolite, micronized)** | Heavy metals, ammonia, some mycotoxin | Pure Body Extra, CytoDetox. Hydrate well. |
| **Cholestyramine (Rx)** | Ochratoxin, trichothecenes, glyphosate | Powerful for mold; can reduce fat-soluble vitamin absorption; separate from meds and supplements by 2 hr. |
| **Welchol (colesevelam) (Rx)** | Ochratoxin, milder than cholestyramine | Tolerated better in IBS-D. |
| **Saccharomyces boulardii** | C. difficile toxin A/B; supports SIgA | More than binder; use through Phase 2–4. |
| **CellCore BioActive Carbon series (Foundation, Minerals, BC-ATP, Carboxy, Biotoxin Binder)** | Broad multi-binder with fulvic/humic acids | Less constipating than charcoal; commonly used in functional protocols. |

### 3.2 Daily binder protocol

- 1 hour after antiparasitic herbs/drugs OR 30 min before meals/supplements
- Twice daily standard, more frequent (3–4x) during heavy Herx
- Always with 8–16 oz water
- Always with magnesium support to prevent constipation
- Rotate or stack 2–3 binders for broader coverage

### 3.3 Sample binder schedule

**Morning (empty stomach, 1 hr post-kill dose)**
- BioActive Carbon Foundation 2 caps + zeolite spray 4 pumps + 16 oz water

**Mid-afternoon (1 hr post-kill dose)**
- G.I. Detox+ 2 caps + 16 oz water

**Bedtime (2 hr post last food/supplement)**
- Activated charcoal 1–2 g + bentonite 1 tbsp liquid + 16 oz water + magnesium 400 mg

## 4. Drainage Maintenance During Phase 3

Phase 1 drainage tools continue throughout. Specifically intensify:

- **Glutathione (liposomal or S-acetyl)** 250–500 mg 2x/day during heavy kill weeks
- **Phosphatidylcholine** 1800–2400 mg/day for membrane and lipid clearance
- **TUDCA** 500 mg 2x/day with meals
- **Coffee enema** 1–2x/week (or daily for short bursts in mold-heavy patients per Klinghardt)
- **Sauna** 4–5x/week, 20–30 min, replace electrolytes
- **Castor oil pack** 4x/week
- **Dry brushing + rebounding** daily

## 5. Heavy Metal Considerations

Parasites concentrate metals; killing them releases metal load. If patient has known heavy metal burden:

- Quicksilver Ultra Binder or IMD specifically for mercury
- Modified citrus pectin for lead
- Chlorella for mercury, lead, cadmium
- Glutathione + selenium + zinc as antioxidant base
- Avoid IV chelation (DMPS, EDTA IV) until parasite kills are complete and binders are consistent — provoked metal release without parasite clearance worsens both.
- Do not initiate aggressive metal chelation in Phase 2; defer to Phase 4 or post-protocol.

## 6. Mycotoxin Co-Burden

Most chronic-illness patients with parasites also carry mycotoxins. Address concurrently if mold panel is positive:

- Cholestyramine 4 g BID–QID (Rx) — primary for ochratoxin and trichothecenes
- Welchol 625 mg 1–3 tabs BID (Rx) — gentler alternative
- Activated charcoal + bentonite combo for broad coverage
- Glutathione + NAC + phosphatidylcholine for liver phase II
- Saccharomyces boulardii to displace Candida overlap
- Address environmental mold exposure first — protocol fails if patient still in moldy building

## 7. Hypercoagulation and Microcirculation

Chronic infections (Babesia, Bartonella, parasites) can drive fibrin deposition and microclotting. Signs: cold extremities, livedo reticularis, reduced exercise tolerance, post-COVID overlap. Add:

- Lumbrokinase 20–40 mg empty stomach 2x/day
- Nattokinase 2,000 FU empty stomach 2x/day (alternative)
- Vitamin E (mixed tocopherols) 200–400 IU/day
- Omega-3 EPA 2–3 g/day
- Avoid combining with anticoagulants (warfarin, DOACs) without medical supervision

## 8. Safety Notes for Phase 3

- Binders WILL bind medications and supplements — strict 2-hour separation
- Constipation is the most common adverse effect — magnesium, hydration, vitamin C are mandatory
- Bismuth >4 weeks risks neurotoxicity — limit
- Long-term cholestyramine reduces fat-soluble vitamin absorption — supplement A, D, E, K
- IMD and zeolite quality vary widely — buy from reputable, third-party tested brands
- Cheap charcoal can contain contaminants — coconut shell, USP grade only

## 9. Tapering Phase 3

After Phase 2 ends:
- Continue binders 2x/day for 2 weeks
- Then 1x/day for 2 weeks
- Then 2–3x/week for maintenance during Phase 4
- Stop biofilm enzymes after kills end unless chronic infection picture warrants continuation
