## 1. Welcome Handout: "What to Expect From Your Parasite Cleanse"

Parasite cleansing is a structured, four-phase process. It is not a one-week pill bottle. To do it well takes 3–6 months and asks you to change a few daily habits. The reward is restored energy, clear skin, calm digestion, sharper thinking, and a body that no longer hosts unwelcome guests.

**The four phases**

1. **Preparation (2–4 weeks)** — We open your drainage pathways. You'll start daily bowel movements, support your liver, calm any allergies, and stock your minerals. Skipping this is the most common reason cleanses make people sick.
2. **Eradication (4–12 weeks)** — Targeted herbs and, if needed, prescription medications kill adult and larval parasites. Dosing intensifies around the full moon, when parasites are most active.
3. **Biofilm and binding (concurrent with phase 2 + 2–4 weeks after)** — We dissolve the protective slime parasites hide in and bind their toxins so they leave through your stool, not back into your bloodstream.
4. **Restoration (6–12 weeks)** — We rebuild your gut lining, re-seed beneficial microbes, replenish nutrients, and lock in resilience.

**What you might feel**
- Some days you'll feel worse before better. Headache, fatigue, mild rash, brain fog, vivid dreams. This is normal die-off.
- We have a toolkit to manage it. Tell us early if you're struggling.

**What you'll need to commit to**
- Daily bowel movements (we'll help you)
- Drinking 80–100 oz water with electrolytes
- Following the diet (sugar, alcohol, gluten removed during the kill phase)
- Taking herbs and binders on schedule, away from food
- Treating your whole household (especially for pinworm) and pets
- Daily sleep priority and weekly sauna

**What to call us about immediately**
- Severe abdominal pain, vomiting, fever
- Bloody stools
- Yellowing of skin or eyes
- New rash with blisters or mouth sores
- New severe confusion or weakness
- If you become pregnant during the protocol

## 2. Prevention Handout

You can get parasites again. Most prevention is daily habit, not paranoia.

**Water**
- Filter all drinking water (1-micron sediment + carbon, UV ideal). Test well water annually.
- Travel: bottled or filtered only. No ice. Brush teeth with bottled water in high-risk regions.
- Don't swallow lake or pool water. Don't swim in freshwater in tropical/subtropical endemic areas (schistosomiasis).

**Food**
- Wash all produce. Vinegar soak (1 cup vinegar to 4 cups water, 5–10 minutes, rinse).
- Cook meat to safe temperatures. No raw or undercooked pork.
- Avoid raw fish, sushi, ceviche unless from a vendor who flash-freezes (kills parasites).
- Skip raw watercress and other raw aquatic plants.
- Refrigerate leftovers within 2 hours.

**Hygiene**
- Wash hands after the bathroom, before eating, after handling pets, after gardening.
- Trim and scrub fingernails (pinworm eggs lodge under nails).
- Daily clean underwear, change pajamas often if pinworm in household.
- Shower in the morning if pinworm — eggs deposit overnight.

**Pets**
- Deworm dogs and cats per vet schedule (usually every 3–6 months).
- Pick up pet feces immediately.
- Pregnant women: never handle litter boxes (toxoplasmosis).
- Wash hands after petting.

**Travel**
- Pre-travel kit: mimosa pudica, S. boulardii, berberine, oregano oil capsules, electrolytes, charcoal, bismuth, hand sanitizer.
- DEET or picaridin in mosquito areas. Permethrin-treated clothing in tick areas.
- Don't walk barefoot on contaminated soil (hookworm, Strongyloides).
- See a travel medicine doctor 4–6 weeks before any tropical travel.

**Daily resilience**
- Stomach acid is your first defense — no chronic PPIs without need.
- Daily fiber-rich diet feeds healthy microbes that compete with parasites.
- Sleep, sunlight, movement, low chronic stress all support immune defense.
- Annual stool screening if you have ongoing risk factors.

## 3. Frequently Asked Questions

### "Do I really have parasites? I'm not from a third-world country."
Parasites are common in industrialized countries. Pinworm infects 40 million Americans. Blastocystis, Giardia, Dientamoeba show up in 20–50% of stool samples in U.S. labs that use modern PCR testing. If you have pets, drink well or untreated water, eat sushi, traveled, or work with kids, your exposure is high.

### "Can't I just take one anti-parasite pill?"
For some specific organisms, yes. Pinworm responds to one pyrantel dose. But most chronic infections involve multiple organisms, biofilm, gut dysbiosis, and an inflamed system that won't reset from a single drug. A complete protocol heals the terrain that let the parasites take hold in the first place.

### "Why do I feel worse during treatment?"
This is the Herxheimer reaction — die-off of parasites releases toxins. Binders, hydration, magnesium, and good sleep manage it. Tell us if it gets above a 3 out of 4 on the severity scale.

### "Why do I have to do this around the full moon?"
Functional medicine practitioners observe parasites becoming more active during the full moon. The mechanism isn't fully proven, but the pattern is consistent enough to use clinically. We dose more aggressively for the 7-day window around the full moon to catch them when they're most exposed.

### "Do I have to give up coffee?"
No. Coffee is fine — even helpful for bile flow and bowel movements. Avoid sweetened coffee drinks.

### "Can I drink alcohol?"
No, during the eradication and binder phases. Your liver is processing parasite die-off and herbs. Alcohol adds to that load and feeds microbial overgrowth. After Phase 4, light occasional consumption is fine.

### "Can I do this while pregnant or breastfeeding?"
Most herbs and most antiparasitic drugs are not safe in pregnancy. If you're pregnant, we postpone elective treatment until after delivery and breastfeeding, unless infection is dangerous to you or the baby — in which case we work with maternal-fetal medicine and infectious disease.

### "Should my kids do this?"
For pinworm or confirmed pediatric parasites, yes — with weight-based pediatric dosing and modified herbs. We avoid wormwood and high-dose berberine in young children. Treat the whole household for pinworm.

### "What about my pets?"
Treat them concurrently. Talk to your vet about a deworming course. Pets that share your home share your parasites.

### "How will I know it worked?"
Symptoms improve. We re-test stool at 12 weeks and 3 months post-treatment. Eosinophils, IgE, ferritin, and other markers normalize. Energy, sleep, skin, and digestion improve.

### "Will it come back?"
You can be reinfected. We teach prevention, run quarterly maintenance pulses with mimosa pudica, and re-screen annually if you have ongoing risk.

### "Is this dangerous?"
With proper preparation, monitoring, and dose titration, it's low-risk. Without preparation — going straight into kills — it can cause severe Herxheimer reactions and worsen symptoms. That's why we follow phases.

### "Why isn't my doctor suggesting this?"
Conventional medicine treats specific identified pathogens with specific drugs. Functional medicine treats the broader picture — terrain, biofilm, microbiome, drainage, and the multiple low-grade infections that show up together. They're complementary, not opposed. If you have a specific dangerous infection, you need both worlds.

## 4. Pinworm Specific Patient Handout

Pinworm is the most common parasitic worm in U.S. households. It's spread by tiny eggs that survive on hands, bedding, towels, surfaces, and under fingernails. The hallmark is nighttime anal itching (eggs are laid around the anus at night).

**Treatment**
- Pyrantel pamoate (Reese's, OTC) 11 mg per kg of body weight, single dose. Repeat in 2 weeks.
- OR albendazole/mebendazole by prescription.
- Treat **every person in the house** the same day, even if they have no symptoms.

**Same-day decontamination**
- Wash all bedding, sheets, pillowcases, pajamas, towels in hot water.
- Vacuum bedrooms thoroughly, including mattress.
- Wipe down bathroom surfaces, doorknobs, light switches.
- Trim and scrub fingernails for everyone.
- Shower (don't bath) in the morning to wash off any overnight-deposited eggs.

**Prevention going forward**
- Daily clean underwear; change pajamas often.
- Wash hands after bathroom, before meals, after waking.
- Discourage thumb sucking, nail biting in kids.
- Cotton tight-fitting underwear at night.
- Treat household preventively if recurrence pattern.

**Repeat dose in 2 weeks** is essential — eggs hatch on a 2-week cycle. Missing this is why pinworm "won't go away."

## 6. Sleep Hygiene Handout (because sleep is treatment)

- Bed by 10:30 pm. Aim for 7.5–9 hours.
- Cool bedroom (65–68°F).
- Pitch dark — blackout curtains, cover LEDs, eye mask.
- No screens 60 min before bed; blue light blockers if needed.
- No food 3 hr before bed.
- Magnesium glycinate 400 mg at bedtime.
- Glycine 3 g at bedtime.
- Chamomile, passionflower, lemon balm tea.
- Morning sunlight 10–20 min within an hour of waking.
- Consistent wake time, even weekends.
- No alcohol within 4 hr of bed.
- Limit caffeine to before noon.

## 7. Sauna and Movement Handout

**Sauna**
- Infrared 130–150°F or traditional 160–180°F, 20–30 minutes, 3–5 times weekly.
- Hydrate before and after. Replace electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium).
- Shower in cool/cold water after to flush.
- Skip if febrile, dehydrated, pregnant first trimester, severe cardiovascular disease.

**Movement**
- 30 min daily walking minimum.
- Strength 2–3 times weekly (bodyweight or weights).
- Rebounder (mini trampoline) 5–10 min daily for lymph.
- Yoga, stretching, mobility 2–3 times weekly.
- Cold exposure (face plunge, cold shower) for vagal tone.
- Avoid intense cardio during heavy Herx weeks; resume in restoration.
