# 09 — Herbal Materia Medica

Deep reference for the antiparasitic and supportive herbs used across phases. Each entry covers actions, target organisms, dosing, evidence quality, contraindications, drug interactions, and best-form selection.

---

## 1. Black Walnut (Juglans nigra) — green hull

- **Constituents:** Juglone, plumbagin, tannins, naphthoquinones
- **Actions:** Antinematodal, antifungal, antibacterial, mild laxative
- **Targets:** Roundworms (Ascaris, hookworm, Trichuris), tapeworm-active, Candida
- **Dose adult:** Tincture (green hull) 15–30 drops (1–2 mL) 2–3x/day in water; capsule 500 mg 2x/day
- **Pediatric:** 1 drop tincture per year of age 2x/day, max 10 drops/dose
- **Form selection:** Green hull tincture preferred over dried hull; quality varies. Reputable: Wishgarden, Herb Pharm, NOW.
- **Cautions:** Black walnut allergy. Tannins can reduce iron absorption — separate from iron supplements. Avoid pregnancy (uterine stimulant in tradition).
- **Evidence:** Long ethnobotanical use, in vitro activity against helminths and Candida well documented; clinical RCTs scarce.
- **Pairs with:** Wormwood, clove (Hulda Clark triad)

## 2. Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium)

- **Constituents:** Sesquiterpene lactones (absinthin, anabsinthin), thujone (alpha and beta), azulenes
- **Actions:** Antinematodal, anti-protozoal, bitter, choleretic
- **Targets:** Roundworms, pinworm, Giardia, Plasmodium (related to Artemisia annua), Blastocystis
- **Dose adult:** Capsule 200–500 mg 2–3x/day with meals; tincture 1–2 mL 2x/day
- **Duration:** Maximum 4–6 weeks continuous, then 2-week break
- **Cautions:** Seizure history (thujone is GABA-A antagonist), pregnancy, breastfeeding, severe hepatic disease. Do not exceed dose; thujone is neurotoxic at high cumulative doses.
- **Drug interactions:** Possible CYP induction; avoid with antiseizure meds, warfarin caution.
- **Form selection:** Standardized capsule preferred; thujone-free extracts are weaker. Avoid raw absinthe-like preparations for long-term use.
- **Evidence:** In vitro broad antiparasitic; small clinical studies in Crohn (immune-mod). Companion to artemisinin work.

## 3. Sweet Wormwood (Artemisia annua) — artemisinin

- **Constituents:** Artemisinin (sesquiterpene lactone with endoperoxide bridge), flavonoids
- **Actions:** Antimalarial, anti-babesial, anti-toxoplasmal, anti-schistosomal, anti-cancer (heme-Fe-mediated radical generation)
- **Targets:** Plasmodium, Babesia, Toxoplasma, Schistosoma, certain leishmania
- **Dose adult (artemisinin standardized 4%):** 200–500 mg 2x/day
- **Pulsing pattern (Babesia/co-infection):** 3 days on, 11 days off, repeat 4–6 cycles to prevent receptor downregulation and resistance
- **Cautions:** Hepatic monitoring; pregnancy first trimester; G6PD caution
- **Drug interactions:** CYP3A4 substrate; avoid with strong inhibitors
- **Forms:** Sweet Annie whole herb (lower potency), standardized artemisinin (Allergy Research Group, Researched Nutritionals NutraMedix), artesunate (Rx)
- **Evidence:** Robust RCT data in malaria (WHO ACT regimens); growing data in babesiosis and toxoplasmosis adjunct.

## 4. Clove (Syzygium aromaticum)

- **Constituents:** Eugenol (75–90%), eugenyl acetate, beta-caryophyllene
- **Actions:** Anti-protozoal, anti-bacterial, ovicidal, analgesic, antifungal
- **Targets:** Parasite ova, Giardia, Candida, oral pathogens
- **Dose adult:** Capsule whole-clove powder 500 mg 3x/day; clove oil 1–3 drops in carrier 2x/day
- **Duration:** Continuous through Phase 2
- **Cautions:** Bleeding (eugenol antiplatelet); GI irritation undiluted; pregnancy caution at therapeutic doses
- **Pairs with:** Black walnut, wormwood for lifecycle coverage; with binder
- **Evidence:** Eugenol's antimicrobial action is well established in vitro; clinical use ethnobotanical and through Hulda Clark protocols.

## 5. Mimosa Pudica (Mimosa pudica) — seed

- **Constituents:** Mimosine, mucilaginous polysaccharides, jasmonic acid, tannins
- **Actions:** Mechanical (gel-forming), tubulin inhibition (mimosine), anti-helminthic, mild laxative, biofilm trap
- **Targets:** Broad helminth, biofilm, Candida; physical "scrubbing" of GI lining
- **Dose adult:** Capsule (450 mg) — week 1 1 cap 2x, week 2 2 caps 2x, week 3 3 caps 2x, week 4+ 4 caps 2x (max 4 caps 3x daily). Take with full water, away from food and supplements (30 min before, 2 hr after).
- **Duration:** Minimum 90 days; 6+ months for autoinfecting helminths (Strongyloides, Hymenolepis, Enterobius)
- **Cautions:** Constipation if hydration inadequate; never with bowel obstruction
- **Forms:** Microbe Formulas Mimosa Pudica, CellCore Para 1, Bulk Mimosa Pudica seed powder (Lost Empire Herbs, others)
- **Evidence:** In vitro antinematodal data; clinical data largely from functional medicine practitioner reports

## 6. Berberine (from Berberis, Coptis, Hydrastis, Phellodendron)

- **Constituents:** Berberine alkaloid
- **Actions:** Anti-protozoal, antibacterial (broad including Gram-neg, H. pylori), antifungal, glucose lowering, lipid lowering
- **Targets:** Giardia, Entamoeba, Blastocystis, H. pylori, SIBO, Candida
- **Dose adult:** 500 mg 2–3x/day with meals; SIBO protocols up to 1500 mg/day
- **Duration:** 4–8 weeks; longer cycles for SIBO
- **Cautions:** Pregnancy, breastfeeding (potential for neonatal jaundice via bilirubin displacement), neonates. Hypoglycemia risk if on diabetes meds.
- **Drug interactions:** Strong CYP3A4 inhibitor — major caution with statins, immunosuppressants (cyclosporine), midazolam, many others. Check interactions.
- **Forms:** Standardized berberine HCl; dihydroberberine (better absorbed); goldenseal (~5% berberine)
- **Evidence:** Multiple RCTs in IBS, SIBO, H. pylori, NAFLD; meta-analyses support antimicrobial action.

## 7. Garlic (Allium sativum) — allicin

- **Constituents:** Allicin (formed when alliinase acts on alliin when crushed), diallyl sulfides, ajoene
- **Actions:** Antibacterial (broad), antiviral, antifungal, antiparasitic, cardiovascular
- **Targets:** Giardia, Ascaris, hookworm, Candida, broad bacterial
- **Dose adult:** Standardized allicin (Allimed, Allimax) 600–1200 mg/day; raw crushed garlic 1–2 cloves/day let stand 10 min before consuming
- **Cautions:** Bleeding risk pre-surgery (stop 7 days prior); GI upset; reflux
- **Drug interactions:** Antiplatelet/anticoagulant additive
- **Evidence:** Ancient use; modern evidence for cardiovascular and broad antimicrobial

## 8. Neem (Azadirachta indica)

- **Constituents:** Azadirachtin, nimbin, gedunin, salannin
- **Actions:** Antiparasitic, antibacterial, antiviral, immune modulating, gametocyte inhibition
- **Targets:** Plasmodium, broad helminth, Candida, oral biofilm
- **Dose adult:** Capsule 300–500 mg 2x/day; tincture 2–4 mL 2x/day; neem leaf tea
- **Cautions:** Pregnancy (abortifacient in tradition); contraception interaction (long-term high-dose may reduce fertility); pediatric high-dose neem oil toxicity reported
- **Evidence:** Strong ethnobotanical and growing in vitro/in vivo data; antimalarial trials

## 9. Pau d'arco (Tabebuia avellanedae / impetiginosa) — lapacho

- **Constituents:** Lapachol, beta-lapachone, naphthoquinones
- **Actions:** Antifungal, antiparasitic (Trypanosoma, Schistosoma), antibacterial, antitumor in vitro
- **Targets:** Candida, Giardia, Trypanosoma, schistosomes
- **Dose adult:** Tea 1–3 cups/day (1 tsp inner bark per 8 oz, simmer 10 min); capsule 500 mg 2–3x/day; tincture 2–4 mL 2x/day
- **Cautions:** Bleeding risk at high doses (lapachol antivitamin K); pregnancy
- **Drug interactions:** Anticoagulants
- **Evidence:** In vitro broad antimicrobial; limited clinical

## 10. Pomegranate husk (Punica granatum)

- **Constituents:** Pelletierine alkaloids (in bark/root), punicalagins, ellagic acid
- **Actions:** Anti-tapeworm classically (pelletierine paralyzes worms)
- **Dose adult:** Husk powder 500–1000 mg 2x/day; bark/root traditionally with caution due to toxicity at high doses
- **Cautions:** Pelletierine alkaloids toxic in overdose; use husk over bark for safety
- **Evidence:** Traditional and Ayurvedic use; modern in vitro data

## 11. Pumpkin Seed (Cucurbita pepo)

- **Constituents:** Cucurbitin (paralytic to worms), zinc, magnesium, omega-3
- **Actions:** Anthelmintic (paralytic), prostatic, mild zinc source
- **Targets:** Tapeworms, roundworms
- **Dose adult:** 2–4 tablespoons raw seeds daily, ground for higher activity; classical traditional dose 200–500 g (about 2 cups) ground for one-time anthelmintic
- **Duration:** Daily through Phase 2; high-dose pulse periodically
- **Cautions:** Largely safe; allergy possible
- **Evidence:** Clinical and traditional use across cultures

## 12. Andrographis (Andrographis paniculata) — "King of Bitters"

- **Constituents:** Andrographolide, neoandrographolides
- **Actions:** Antimalarial, anti-Lyme/co-infection, hepatoprotective, immune-mod, anti-inflammatory
- **Targets:** Plasmodium, Borrelia, liver flukes, broad
- **Dose adult:** Standardized to 10% andrographolide — 400 mg 2–3x/day
- **Duration:** Buhner Lyme protocols use 30+ days
- **Cautions:** Allergic reactions ~1%, can be severe (anaphylaxis described); GI upset; bitter taste; pregnancy
- **Evidence:** RCTs for upper respiratory; long traditional use; Buhner protocols

## 13. Cryptolepis (Cryptolepis sanguinolenta)

- **Constituents:** Cryptolepine alkaloid
- **Actions:** Antimalarial, anti-babesial, anti-Borrelia, antibacterial, anticancer in vitro
- **Targets:** Babesia, Plasmodium, Borrelia
- **Dose adult:** Tincture 1 tsp (5 mL) 3x/day (Buhner standard)
- **Duration:** 4–6 months in Babesia protocols
- **Cautions:** Pregnancy; liver monitoring
- **Forms:** Woodland Essence, Green Dragon Botanicals
- **Evidence:** Strong in vitro for malaria and Babesia; Ghanaian RCTs in malaria; Buhner clinical experience

## 14. Sida acuta

- **Constituents:** Cryptolepine and related alkaloids
- **Actions:** Anti-babesial, anti-bartonella, broad antimicrobial, RBC support
- **Dose adult:** Tincture 1 tsp 3x/day
- **Pairs with:** Cryptolepis in Babesia protocols
- **Evidence:** Buhner clinical protocol; African ethnobotany

## 15. Houttuynia cordata ("fishy mint")

- **Actions:** Anti-Bartonella, antiviral, immune-mod
- **Dose adult:** Capsule 1 g 3x/day; tincture 1–2 tsp 3x/day
- **Pairs with:** Bartonella protocols; with cryptolepis
- **Cautions:** Pregnancy; GI upset
- **Evidence:** Buhner; in vitro antiviral

## 16. Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis)

- **Constituents:** Berberine, hydrastine
- **Actions:** Antimicrobial, mucosal astringent
- **Targets:** Giardia, broad
- **Dose adult:** 500 mg 2x/day (similar to berberine)
- **Cautions:** Endangered species — choose cultivated; pregnancy; same drug interactions as berberine
- **Evidence:** Largely overlaps berberine

## 17. Olive Leaf (Olea europaea)

- **Constituents:** Oleuropein, hydroxytyrosol
- **Actions:** Antimicrobial, antiviral, antiparasitic adjunct, cardiovascular
- **Dose adult:** Standardized to 18% oleuropein, 500–1000 mg 2x/day
- **Cautions:** Mild BP lowering; GI upset; bleeding
- **Evidence:** Cardiovascular RCTs; antimicrobial in vitro

## 18. Oregano oil (Origanum vulgare)

- **Constituents:** Carvacrol, thymol, p-cymene
- **Actions:** Broad antimicrobial, anti-protozoal, antifungal
- **Targets:** Giardia, Blastocystis, Candida, SIBO
- **Dose adult:** Standardized P73 emulsified, 200 mg capsule 2x/day; or 4–8 drops oil in carrier
- **Duration:** 4–8 weeks; rotate to avoid mucosal irritation
- **Cautions:** Mucosal irritation, must encapsulate; GERD; iron absorption; pregnancy
- **Evidence:** Strong in vitro and in vivo antimicrobial

## 19. Mugwort (Artemisia vulgaris)

- **Constituents:** Volatile oils (lower thujone than absinthe wormwood), sesquiterpene lactones
- **Actions:** Anti-protozoal, anthelmintic (classical for pinworm), uterine
- **Dose adult:** 200–400 mg 2x/day
- **Cautions:** Pregnancy strict avoid; ragweed cross-reactivity
- **Evidence:** Traditional, in vitro

## 20. Cape Aloe (Aloe ferox) — laxative leaf

- **Use:** Phase 1 bowel transit only
- **Dose:** 50–200 mg of standardized aloin extract at bedtime, short courses
- **Cautions:** Stimulant laxative; not for long term; not pregnancy

## 21. Triphala (Ayurvedic — amalaki + bibhitaki + haritaki)

- **Use:** Phase 1 maintenance bowel and detox tonic
- **Dose:** 1–2 g at bedtime in water
- **Cautions:** Mild loose stools when starting; safe long term
- **Evidence:** Ayurvedic RCTs for transit, oxidative stress

## 22. Milk Thistle (Silybum marianum)

- **Constituents:** Silymarin (silibinin, silidianin, silichristin)
- **Actions:** Hepatoprotective, antioxidant, anti-fibrotic, glutathione preservation
- **Dose adult:** 200–400 mg standardized to 80% silymarin, 2x/day
- **Cautions:** Generally very safe; mild GI; Asteraceae allergy
- **Evidence:** Robust hepatic literature

## 23. TUDCA — already covered Phase 1; herbal-adjacent (bile acid)

## 24. Boswellia serrata

- **Constituents:** Boswellic acids
- **Actions:** Anti-inflammatory (5-LOX), gut healing, joint
- **Dose adult:** 300–500 mg 3x/day standardized AKBA
- **Use:** Adjunct in gut repair Phase 4

## 25. Curcumin (Curcuma longa)

- **Constituents:** Curcuminoids
- **Actions:** Anti-inflammatory, anti-fibrotic, hepatoprotective, mild antimicrobial
- **Dose adult:** 500–1000 mg 2x/day enhanced absorption form
- **Use:** Anti-inflammatory base across phases

## 26. Quercetin

- Anti-inflammatory, mast cell stabilizer, biofilm adjunct
- 500–1000 mg 2x/day

## 27. Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis), Thyme (Thymus vulgaris)

- Mild antiparasitic and biofilm-disrupting essential oil herbs
- Use as culinary supports + occasional capsules
