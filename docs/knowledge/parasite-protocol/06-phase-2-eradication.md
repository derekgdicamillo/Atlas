# 06 — Phase 2: Eradication (4–12 Weeks)

## Goal

Active killing of adult and larval parasites across the lifecycle. Run for a minimum of 90 days for helminths (covers reproduction cycles), 4–6 weeks for protozoa, longer with autoinfecting organisms (Strongyloides, Hymenolepis, Enterobius — 6+ months of mimosa pudica).

## 1. Core Herbal Stack — Standard Adult Protocol

This is the default starting point for Tier 1–2 adult patients with no Rx-mandating organism identified.

### 1.1 The "Mimosa Pudica" backbone

**Mimosa Pudica seed** (Microbe Formulas, CellCore, Cellcore Para-1, or bulk seed)
- Loading: Week 1 — 1 capsule (450 mg) 2x/day, away from food
- Week 2 — 2 caps 2x/day
- Week 3 — 3 caps 2x/day
- Week 4 onward — 4 caps 2x/day (some practitioners go to 4 caps 3x/day)
- Take 30 min before food or 2 hr after, with full water
- Continue 6+ months for autoinfecting organisms
- Mechanism: gel-forming fiber that physically traps and removes parasites; phytochemicals (mimosine, tubulin disruptors) with antiparasitic action documented in vitro

### 1.2 Hulda Clark herbal triad (concurrent)

**Black walnut hull tincture (green hull, Juglans nigra)**
- Adult: 2 droppers (2 mL) 2–3x/day in water away from food
- Pediatric: 1 drop per year of age 2x/day
- Active naphthoquinones (juglone) — antinematodal, antifungal, antimicrobial

**Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium)** — capsule preferred over tincture due to thujone content
- 200–500 mg 2–3x/day with meals
- Maximum 4–6 weeks continuous, then break 2 weeks; rotate
- Caution: seizure history, pregnancy
- Active sesquiterpene lactones, artemisinins

**Cloves (Syzygium aromaticum)** — for ova
- 500 mg whole-clove powder 3x/day, or 1–2 drops clove oil in carrier
- Eugenol disrupts parasite eggs; pair with adult-killing herbs
- Continuous use okay

## 2. Pharmaceutical Adjuncts — When and How

Pharmaceuticals are not "anti-natural." For confirmed pathogens, severe symptoms, or autoinfecting organisms, they are often the right tool. Detailed pharmacology in [10-pharmaceutical-reference.md](10-pharmaceutical-reference.md).

| Drug | First-line for | Typical adult dose |
|------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Albendazole** | Most nematodes, hydatid, neurocysticercosis, microsporidia | 400 mg PO BID (or once for pinworm/hookworm, repeat 2 wks) |
| **Mebendazole** | Pinworm, ascaris, hookworm, whipworm | 100 mg BID x 3 d (or 500 mg once) |
| **Ivermectin** | Strongyloides, scabies, lice, lymphatic filariasis | 200 mcg/kg single dose, repeat at 2 wks |
| **Praziquantel** | Tapeworms, schistosomes, flukes | 5–25 mg/kg single or split dose (organism-specific) |
| **Nitazoxanide** | Cryptosporidium, Giardia, Blastocystis, broad | 500 mg BID x 3 d (longer if immunocompromised) |
| **Tinidazole** | Giardia, amebiasis, trichomoniasis, Blastocystis | 2 g once daily x 3–5 d |
| **Metronidazole** | Giardia, amebiasis, trichomoniasis | 250–750 mg TID x 5–10 d |
| **Paromomycin** | Luminal amebiasis, cryptosporidiosis (HIV), tapeworms | 25–35 mg/kg/d divided TID x 7 d |
| **Atovaquone + azithromycin** | Babesiosis | 750 mg q12h + 500–1000 mg/d x 7–10 d (longer if severe) |
| **Pyrimethamine + sulfadiazine + leucovorin** | Toxoplasmosis encephalitis | Specialist Rx |
| **Hydroxychloroquine, mefloquine, artemether-lumefantrine** | Malaria | Per CDC/IDSA, organism-specific |

Default functional pattern: short pharmaceutical pulse (3–7 days) at one full moon, embedded inside a broader 90-day herbal protocol. This catches what herbs miss without long-term Rx exposure.

## 3. Sample Eradication Protocols by Indication

### 3.1 Empiric broad herbal (Tier 1–2, no specific organism identified)

90 days:
- Mimosa Pudica per loading schedule, 4 caps 2x/day by week 4
- Black walnut tincture 2 mL 2x/day
- Wormwood 250 mg 2x/day (4 weeks on / 2 weeks off rotation)
- Cloves 500 mg 3x/day continuous
- Berberine 500 mg 2x/day
- Neem 400 mg 2x/day
- Plus Phase 3 binders and biofilm enzymes (file 07)
- Plus Phase 1 maintenance

### 3.2 Confirmed Blastocystis hominis (symptomatic, IBS-like)

6 weeks:
- Tinidazole 2 g daily x 3 days OR nitazoxanide 500 mg BID x 3 days, repeat at day 21 (full moon)
- Saccharomyces boulardii 10 billion CFU 2x/day x 60 days
- Berberine 500 mg 2x/day x 6 weeks
- Mimosa Pudica 2 caps 2x/day x 60 days
- Oregano oil capsule 200 mg 2x/day x 6 weeks
- Bismuth subcitrate 240 mg BID x 4 weeks (biofilm)
- Phase 4 restoration (probiotics, glutamine) starting week 4
- Re-test at week 10

### 3.3 Confirmed Giardia (symptomatic)

5–7 days primary, then 6-week functional follow:
- Tinidazole 2 g single dose, repeat in 5–7 d if persistent OR
- Nitazoxanide 500 mg BID x 3 d
- Saccharomyces boulardii 10 billion BID x 30 days
- Lactose-free or lactase enzyme x 30 days (transient lactase deficiency)
- Pancreatic enzyme support (often transient pancreatic insufficiency)
- Berberine 500 mg 2x/day x 4 weeks
- Mimosa Pudica + Phase 4 restoration

### 3.4 Confirmed Strongyloides (any positive serology)

- **Ivermectin 200 mcg/kg PO daily x 2 days, repeat in 2 weeks** — first-line, do not skip
- Optional adjunct albendazole 400 mg BID x 7 days for very heavy infection
- Mimosa Pudica 4 caps 2x/day x 6+ months (autoinfection cycle)
- Mark chart "Strongyloides positive — do not use steroids without preceding ivermectin"
- Re-test serology at 6 and 12 months (declining titer indicates clearance)

### 3.5 Confirmed pinworm (Enterobius)

- Albendazole 400 mg single, repeat in 2 weeks OR mebendazole 100 mg single, repeat in 2 weeks
- **Treat entire household simultaneously**, including asymptomatic
- Wash all bedding, towels, pajamas in hot water
- Trim and scrub fingernails daily
- Mimosa Pudica 6+ months
- Optional herbal follow: black walnut, wormwood, clove

### 3.6 Tapeworm (Taenia, Diphyllobothrium, Hymenolepis)

- Praziquantel single-dose (organism-specific dosing)
- Examine stool for scolex passage (cure indicator for Taenia)
- For neurocysticercosis: never empiric Rx; specialist with steroids + albendazole/praziquantel
- Pumpkin seed + pomegranate husk traditional adjuncts
- Re-test at 3 months

### 3.7 Babesia (confirmed or strong clinical suspicion)

- **Atovaquone 750 mg q12h + azithromycin 500–1000 mg/day x 7–10 days (mild) or longer (severe/immunocompromised)** as primary
- Add cryptolepis tincture 5 mL 3x/day for 4–6 months
- Add Sida acuta tincture 5 mL 3x/day
- Artemisinin 200 mg BID x 3 days, off 11 days, repeat for 4–6 cycles (controversial; consult ILADS)
- Methylene blue 0.5–2 mg/kg/day under specialist
- Lumbrokinase or nattokinase for hypercoagulation
- Liposomal glutathione, electrolytes, sleep, low-histamine diet
- Concurrent Lyme/Bartonella treatment per ILADS if co-infection
- Monitor CBC, retic, haptoglobin, LDH for hemolysis

### 3.8 Toxoplasmosis (chronic, neuropsych picture, immunocompetent)

Most chronic toxo cysts are not eradicable. Goal is to suppress reactivation and reduce inflammation.
- Acute or reactivated: pyrimethamine + sulfadiazine + leucovorin (specialist)
- Chronic suppression / functional: atovaquone 750 mg/day + azithromycin pulses, or methylene blue per specialist
- Sweet wormwood (artemisinin) 100–200 mg 2x/day pulse
- Cat litter handled by another household member; never pregnant women
- Monitor CD4, IgG titers

### 3.9 Schistosomiasis or fluke

- Praziquantel per organism-specific dose
- Repeat in 3–6 months (mature worms only, juveniles less responsive)
- Surveillance for hepatic fibrosis, bladder pathology, cholangiocarcinoma

## 4. Lunar Pulsing Schedule

Mark the calendar three days before, day of, three days after each full moon. During this 7-day window:
- Increase mimosa pudica to top dose
- Add wormwood pulse if rotating off-on
- Embed any pharmaceutical pulse here when possible
- Increase binder dose
- Hydrate aggressively, sauna, light exercise

## 5. Combination and Sequencing Notes

- Berberine + oregano oil + caprylic acid → strong against Candida overlap
- Andrographis + cryptolepis → tick-borne and protozoal overlap
- Mimosa pudica + binders → physical clearance synergy
- Black walnut + wormwood + clove → Hulda Clark classic, lifecycle coverage
- Do not combine multiple high-thujone herbs (wormwood, mugwort, sage) at full dose simultaneously
- Reduce dose 50% in first week if patient has high MCAS reactivity, then titrate up
- Do not start two new agents on the same day; stagger by 3–5 days to identify intolerance source

## 6. Monitoring During Phase 2

- Daily symptom log: GI, sleep, mood, skin, energy 1–10
- Weekly weight
- Bowel movement quality (Bristol)
- Herx severity scale (file 13)
- LFT and CBC at week 6 if pharmaceuticals used or wormwood >4 weeks continuous
- Stool re-test at week 10–12

## 7. End-of-Phase-2 Checklist

- Symptoms reduced or resolved
- Stool retesting clear or significantly reduced load
- LFTs normal
- Eosinophilia and IgE trending down
- Patient ready to taper kills and emphasize Phase 4 restoration
