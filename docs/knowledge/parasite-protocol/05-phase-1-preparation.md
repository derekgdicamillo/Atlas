# 05 — Phase 1: Preparation (2–4 Weeks)

## Goal

Open drainage pathways, normalize bowel transit, support hepatobiliary flow, replenish minerals and antioxidants, calm mast cells, and identify any conditions that should be addressed before kills begin. The phase is non-negotiable. Skipping it causes most adverse Herxheimer reactions and protocol failures.

## 1. Bowel Transit (the gate)

Target: minimum **one well-formed bowel movement daily**, ideally 1–2. Bristol scale 3–4.

If constipated:
- **Magnesium citrate** 200–800 mg at bedtime, titrate to soft stool. Magnesium glycinate is gentler but less aperient; magnesium oxide is harshest, reserve for short courses.
- **Triphala** 1–2 g at bedtime, classical ayurvedic blend (amalaki, bibhitaki, haritaki). Tones rather than purges, safe for chronic use.
- **Vitamin C** to bowel tolerance (1–10 g divided through day, back off to one dose under tolerance threshold).
- **Cape aloe / aloe ferox** 50–200 mg short course. Avoid prolonged use of stimulant laxatives.
- **Hydration** 30–35 mL/kg body weight per day, with electrolytes.
- **Castor oil pack** over abdomen, 30–60 min, 3–4x/week.
- **Squatty potty / footstool** to relax puborectalis.
- **Coffee enema** 1–2x/week if patient tolerates and has no hemorrhoids/fissures (controversial, but standard in functional protocols for liver/bowel; never daily).

If diarrhea:
- Investigate cause first (Giardia, Crypto, IBD, bile acid malabsorption).
- Saccharomyces boulardii 5–10 billion CFU 2x/day — strong evidence for parasitic and antibiotic-associated diarrhea.
- Soluble fiber (psyllium 5–10 g, partially hydrolyzed guar gum 5 g) — paradoxically improves stool form in either direction.
- Berberine 500 mg 2–3x/day if H. pylori or SIBO suspected.
- Replace electrolytes aggressively.

## 2. Hepatobiliary Support

The liver and bile system process all parasite die-off and toxin metabolites. Open it before kills.

| Tool | Dose | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **TUDCA (tauroursodeoxycholic acid)** | 250–500 mg with meals | Bile flow, hepatocyte protection, ER stress reduction. First-line. |
| **Ox bile** | 125–500 mg with meals | If post-cholecystectomy or steatorrhea. |
| **Milk thistle (silymarin)** | 200–400 mg standardized to 80% silymarin, 2x/day | Hepatoprotective; not strongly excretory. Long-term safe. |
| **Dandelion root tincture or tea** | 2–4 mL tincture or 1 cup tea 2x/day | Bile flow, mild diuretic. |
| **Beet root powder or whole roasted beets** | 1 tsp powder or 1 cup cooked daily | Betaine, nitric oxide, bile thinning. |
| **Bitter herbs (gentian, artichoke, andrographis)** | 5–10 drops bitter tincture on tongue 15 min before meals | Stimulates digestive cascade; bile, HCl, pancreatic enzymes. |
| **Phosphatidylcholine** | 900–1800 mg/day | Cell membrane repair; bile thinning; helpful in mold + parasite overlap. |
| **Glutathione (liposomal or S-acetyl)** | 250–500 mg/day | Phase II conjugation; supports detox of die-off. |
| **NAC** | 600–1200 mg 1–2x/day | Glutathione precursor; biofilm-disrupting too. |

## 4. Mineral and Nutrient Optimization

Many chronic-illness patients are mineral-depleted. Antiparasitics work better and are tolerated better with replete reserves.

- **Magnesium** total 400–800 mg/day mixed forms (citrate, glycinate, malate, threonate)
- **Zinc picolinate or bisglycinate** 15–30 mg/day with food (30 mg max long-term without copper)
- **Copper** 1–2 mg/day if zinc >25 mg
- **Selenium** 100–200 mcg/day (Brazil nuts 1–2/day acceptable)
- **Iodine** 150–300 mcg if not contraindicated by Hashimoto with high TPO
- **Iron** only if ferritin documented low; do not over-supplement (parasites use iron)
- **B-complex** activated forms: methylfolate 400–800 mcg, methyl-B12 1–5 mg, P5P, riboflavin
- **Vitamin D3 + K2 (MK-7)** 2,000–5,000 IU D3 + 90–180 mcg MK-7
- **Omega-3 EPA/DHA** 2–3 g/day
- **Vitamin C** 1–3 g/day mixed (ascorbic acid + buffered)

## 5. Mast Cell and Histamine Stabilization

Many parasite patients have MCAS overlap. Stabilize before kills.

- **Quercetin** 500–1000 mg 2x/day
- **DAO enzyme** with high-histamine meals
- **Vitamin C** for histamine clearance
- **Luteolin** 100 mg 2x/day
- **Mast cell diet trial** — low histamine 2 weeks; reintroduce as tolerance returns
- **H1/H2 blockers** if needed (cetirizine + famotidine), prefer non-sedating
- **Cromolyn sodium** 100 mg 4x/day if severe (Rx)

## 6. Digestive Capacity

If hypochlorhydria suspected (rosacea, brittle nails, undigested food, low B12, atrophic gastritis):
- **Betaine HCl** with pepsin 1 cap with high-protein meals, titrate up by one cap per meal until warmth/burn, then back off by one
- **Digestive bitters** before meals
- **Pancreatic enzymes** with meals if elastase <200 or steatorrhea

If H. pylori positive:
- Treat first per IFM/conventional protocol; commonly: mastic gum 1 g 2x/day + zinc carnosine 75 mg 2x/day + DGL 380 mg 3x/day + matula tea + berberine 500 mg 3x/day for 6 weeks. Or quad therapy if severe.
- Defer parasite kill until after H. pylori cleared or clearly suppressed.

## 7. Glycemic and Adrenal Stability

Die-off increases stress hormone demand.

- Stable blood sugar — protein and fat at every meal, avoid refined carbs
- Adaptogens — ashwagandha 600 mg/day, rhodiola 200–400 mg AM, holy basil 500 mg 2x/day
- Sleep 7–9 h, cool dark room
- HRV monitoring optional, vagal tone work (humming, gargling, cold face plunge, vagus nerve stim)

## 8. Anti-Parasite Diet Initiation (full detail in file 11)

Begin transitioning during Phase 1. By end of Phase 1 patient should be:
- Off refined sugar, alcohol, gluten, dairy (if reactive), processed foods
- Eating high-fiber vegetables, bitter greens, onion, garlic, pumpkin seeds
- Including raw pumpkin seeds 2–4 tbsp/day, pomegranate seeds, papaya seeds 1 tsp/day, fermented foods (if histamine tolerated)
- Hydrated, electrolyte-replete
- Carb timing — most carbs midday; protein and fat for breakfast and dinner

## 9. Sample Phase 1 Daily Schedule

**Morning (fasted)**
- 16 oz water + LMNT or mineral mix + lemon
- Bitter tincture under tongue 5–10 drops
- Coffee or matcha (optional)
- Castor oil pack 30 min while reading

**With breakfast**
- Betaine HCl + enzymes
- TUDCA 250 mg
- Multivitamin / B-complex
- Vitamin D3+K2
- 1 tbsp raw pumpkin seeds

**Mid-morning**
- 16 oz water with electrolytes
- Magnesium 200 mg

**With lunch**
- Bitters
- TUDCA 250 mg
- Pancreatic enzymes if needed
- Quercetin 500 mg

**Mid-afternoon**
- Lymphatic herbal tea (nettle/cleavers/dandelion)
- 5–10 min rebounder or walk

**With dinner**
- Bitters
- Quercetin 500 mg
- Omega-3
- Berberine 500 mg if SIBO/dysbiosis

**Bedtime**
- Magnesium glycinate 200–400 mg
- Triphala 1 g
- Glycine 3 g (sleep + glutathione synthesis)

**Weekly**
- Sauna 3–5 sessions
- Coffee enema 1–2x optional
- Dry brushing daily

## 10. Readiness Criteria for Phase 2

Move to Phase 2 only when:
- Bowel movements daily, formed
- Bile flow improved (less RUQ tenderness, better fat tolerance)
- Sleep adequate (>6 h)
- Electrolyte status stable
- No active untreated H. pylori or severe SIBO
- Mast cell symptoms manageable
- Patient psychologically prepared (knows die-off symptoms, has stop-conditions written down)
