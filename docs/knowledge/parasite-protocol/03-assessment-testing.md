# 03 — Assessment and Testing

## 1. The Functional Medicine Assessment Pyramid

```
        Clinical history (60% of dx)
              │
              ▼
    Targeted exam + symptom mapping
              │
              ▼
   Multi-target stool DNA PCR ± ova-and-parasite ×3
              │
              ▼
 Functional adjuncts: OAT, GI map, IgG/IgE serologies
              │
              ▼
 Specialty / imaging when indicated (Strongyloides serology,
 Babesia FISH/PCR, Toxoplasma IgG/IgM/avidity, schisto serology,
 echinococcus serology, abdominal US/CT, MRI brain)
```

## 2. Clinical Intake (use this verbatim with patients)

### 2.1 Exposure history
- Lifetime international travel — countries, duration, water source, food, freshwater swimming
- Birthplace and where grew up (lifetime risk for Chagas, Strongyloides, schistosomiasis)
- Well water, untreated water sources, recreational water (lakes, rivers, hot tubs)
- Pet history — dogs, cats, reptiles, livestock; cleaning practices
- Occupational — agricultural, veterinary, daycare, healthcare, sewage, mining
- Diet — sushi, ceviche, raw or rare meat, wild game, raw watercress, raw milk
- Barefoot walking on soil
- Tick exposure, known bites, Lyme history
- Camping, hiking, cave exploration
- Sexual history relevant to enteric protozoa, Trichomonas

### 2.2 Symptom timeline
- Onset relative to a clear trigger (travel, illness, antibiotic, stressor)
- Cyclical pattern — full moon, premenstrual, seasonal
- Worsening with sugar, alcohol, after meals
- Response to prior antibiotics or empiric metronidazole

### 2.3 Past treatments and reactions
- Prior antiparasitic herbs or drugs and outcome
- Severe reactions to herbs, sulfa, nitroimidazoles
- Strong Herxheimer history with prior detoxification

### 2.4 Family screening
- Household members with similar symptoms — high yield, often overlooked
- Pets with worms, fleas, recent deworming
- Daycare, school outbreaks

### 2.5 Functional review
- Bowel pattern (Bristol scale, frequency, urgency, mucus, undigested food)
- Sleep (initiation, maintenance, full-moon worsening)
- Skin (rashes, itching, eczema, urticaria, rosacea)
- Mood, cognition, anxiety
- Energy, post-exertional malaise
- Air hunger, sweating patterns
- Female cycle, libido

## 3. Laboratory Testing Tiered by Tier

### 3.1 Tier-1 baseline (every patient)

| Test | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| CBC with differential | Eosinophils, microcytic anemia, thrombocytopenia |
| CMP | Liver, kidney, electrolytes — baseline before antiparasitics |
| Ferritin, iron, TIBC, transferrin | Iron deficiency from hookworm, schisto, Trichuris |
| Vitamin B12, folate, MMA, homocysteine | Diphyllobothrium, malabsorption |
| Total IgE | Helminth burden indicator |
| 25-OH vitamin D | Immune competence |
| TSH, fT3, fT4, TPO, TgAb | Differential and baseline |
| Hs-CRP, ESR | Inflammation |
| Lipid panel | Hepatic processing baseline |

### 3.2 Tier-2 stool testing (cornerstone)

#### Recommended functional stool panels (rank-ordered for parasite detection)

| Panel | Strengths | Limitations |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **GI-MAP (Diagnostic Solutions)** — qPCR | Strong PCR sensitivity for Giardia, Crypto, Entamoeba histolytica, Blastocystis, Dientamoeba, Cyclospora, helminth eggs (limited). Adds H. pylori, virulence genes, dysbiosis, calprotectin, secretory IgA. | Misses many helminths; eggs not consistently detected. PCR positivity does not always equal active infection. |
| **GI Effects (Genova) — comprehensive 1- or 3-day** | Multi-modal: PCR + microscopy + culture + functional markers (elastase, fat, SCFA). Three-day collection improves helminth detection. | Slower turnaround, cost. |
| **Doctor's Data Comprehensive Stool Analysis with Parasitology x3** | Three-day O&P plus PCR, culture. Strong for protozoa and helminths. | Microscopy operator-dependent. |
| **Parawellness Research direct microscopy (Dr. Amin lab)** | Saline preparations, bright-field and phase contrast. Detects forms missed by routine labs. | Specimen handling critical; not a complete panel. |
| **Mosaic Diagnostics Organic Acids (OAT)** | Indirect — arabinose (Candida), bacterial dysbiosis markers, mitochondrial. Not parasite-specific but supportive picture. | Indirect inference only. |
| **Conventional ova-and-parasite (LabCorp, Quest)** | Inexpensive, Medicare-covered. | Sensitivity ~30–50% on single sample; need three samples on alternate days; trichrome and acid-fast stains often not run by default. |
| **GI pathogen PCR panel (BioFire, Verigene)** | Hospital ED panel. Fast, includes Giardia, Crypto, Entamoeba, Cyclospora. | Acute use; not a comprehensive functional view. |

**Practical default:** GI-MAP plus conventional 3-day O&P with explicit request for trichrome, acid-fast (Crypto, Cyclospora, Cystoisospora), and modified iron-hematoxylin stains. Add Doctor's Data CSAP-x3 if helminth suspicion is high or repeat testing is needed.

#### Stool collection rules to share with patient
- Three samples on alternate days (Mon, Wed, Fri).
- Stop bismuth (Pepto), barium, mineral oil, antacids, antimicrobials for 7 days prior unless contraindicated to pause.
- Avoid full-moon collection if recent antiparasitic dose given (false positive shedding).
- Refrigerate or use the lab's preservative kit (typically formalin + PVA or Total-Fix) immediately.

### 3.3 Tier-3 serology and specialty

| Test | When to order |
|------|---------------|
| **Strongyloides IgG ELISA** | All immigrants from endemic regions, all veterans with Vietnam/SE Asia exposure, all patients with eosinophilia >500, all patients pre-immunosuppression. |
| **Toxoplasma IgG, IgM, IgG avidity** | Cat exposure, neuropsych picture, undercooked meat, pregnancy planning. Avidity differentiates recent vs. chronic. |
| **Babesia: IgG/IgM serology, PCR, FISH (IGeneX), Babesia duncani panel** | Lyme overlap, air hunger, sweats, hemolysis. |
| **Echinococcus IgG (hydatid)** | Hepatic or pulmonary cyst on imaging — order BEFORE biopsy. |
| **Schistosoma IgG (CDC, NIH, IGeneX)** | Freshwater exposure in endemic regions, hematuria, hepatic fibrosis. |
| **Cysticercosis IgG (EITB, CDC)** | Seizures, especially in patients from Latin America, Africa, Asia. |
| **Trypanosoma cruzi (Chagas) IgG ×2 platforms** | Latin American birth/residence; cardiomyopathy, megacolon. CDC offers free confirmatory. |
| **Filaria, Loa, Onchocerca serologies** | Targeted travel/exposure. |
| **Anti-Saccharomyces, ANCA, calprotectin** | Differentiate IBD overlap. |
| **Total tryptase, plasma histamine, n-methylhistamine, PGD2** | If MCAS overlap. |
| **Mycotoxin urine panel (RealTime, Great Plains)** | Mold co-burden often confounds parasite presentations. |

### 3.4 Imaging and procedural (Tier 4 typically)

- Abdominal ultrasound — hydatid cyst, hepatic flukes, gallbladder ascariasis
- CT abdomen — abscess, hydatid, large worm burden
- MRI brain — neurocysticercosis, cerebral toxoplasmosis
- Echocardiogram — Chagas cardiomyopathy
- Colonoscopy with biopsy — Schistosomiasis, amebic colitis, IBD differential
- ERCP — biliary Ascaris, Clonorchis, Fasciola
- Endoscopy — Anisakis, gastric Strongyloides, H. pylori co-eval
- Slit-lamp / fundoscopy — Toxocara, Toxoplasma, loiasis, onchocerciasis

## 4. Interpreting Results in a Functional Frame

1. **Positive PCR with no symptoms** — colonization vs. infection. Treat if symptomatic, immunocompromised, household has symptomatic members, or pre-immunosuppression. Otherwise, support terrain and recheck in 3–6 months.
2. **Negative stool PCR with strong clinical picture** — do not rule out. Consider:
   - Repeat testing across cycles (helminth eggs shed intermittently)
   - Empiric herbal trial in Tier 1–2 patients
   - Serology (Strongyloides, schisto, Toxo)
   - Mucosal biopsy if endoscopy indicated for other reasons
3. **Eosinophilia is a screen, not a diagnosis.** Common causes besides parasites: atopy, drug reaction, eosinophilic GI disease, hematologic malignancy, adrenal insufficiency, vasculitis (EGPA), HIV.
4. **Elevated calprotectin** does not equal IBD; can be elevated in invasive parasitic colitis (amebiasis, Schistosoma, Strongyloides).
5. **Low pancreatic elastase** with parasites — Giardia and Strongyloides cause secondary pancreatic insufficiency that resolves with eradication.
6. **Low secretory IgA** identifies a vulnerable terrain. Address before and during eradication.
7. **Microbiome dysbiosis with overgrowth of Klebsiella, Proteus, methanogens** — common parasite co-finding; addressed in restoration phase.

## 5. Empiric Treatment Decision Logic

Use empiric herbal treatment when:
- Tier 1–2 patient with strong clinical picture and accessible exposure history
- Stool PCR negative twice but symptoms persistent
- Patient cannot afford or access multi-modal testing
- Family/household decontamination after one positive index case

Do NOT treat empirically:
- Pregnant or breastfeeding (without ID consult)
- Pediatric under age 2 (without ID consult)
- Tier 4 acuity
- Pre-immunosuppression without first ruling out Strongyloides serologically

## 6. Re-Testing Schedule

| Timepoint | Tests |
|-----------|-------|
| Baseline | CBC, CMP, ferritin, IgE, eosinophils, stool PCR ± O&P x3, targeted serologies |
| Mid-protocol (week 6–8) | Symptom diary review; CBC if eosinophilia high; LFTs if pharmaceuticals used |
| End of eradication (week 12–16) | Repeat stool PCR/O&P, eosinophils, IgE, ferritin |
| 3 months post | Repeat stool to confirm clearance; reassess symptoms; SIBO breath test if GI persists |
| 6 months post | If asymptomatic, optional follow-up; if relapse-prone, re-screen |
