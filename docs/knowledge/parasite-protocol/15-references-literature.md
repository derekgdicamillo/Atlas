# 15 — References, Literature, and Guidelines

Curated references for the protocol. Mix of conventional medical literature, functional medicine sources, and herbal monographs. Keep this updated as new RCTs land.

---

## 1. Core Conventional References

### Textbooks
- **Manson's Tropical Diseases**, Farrar et al., 24th ed., Elsevier 2024 — gold standard for tropical parasitology.
- **Markell and Voge's Medical Parasitology**, 9th ed., Saunders 2006 — foundational parasitology textbook for clinicians.
- **Mandell, Douglas, and Bennett's Principles and Practice of Infectious Diseases**, 9th ed., 2020 — chapters on each major parasite.
- **Tropical Infectious Diseases: Principles, Pathogens & Practice**, Guerrant, Walker, Weller, 3rd ed.
- **Atlas of Human Parasitology**, Lawrence Ash, ASCP Press.

### Guidelines and government
- **CDC DPDx — Laboratory Identification of Parasites of Public Health Concern** — https://www.cdc.gov/dpdx/
- **CDC Yellow Book** (Travel Medicine) — updated biennially.
- **WHO Guidelines for the Treatment of Malaria, 3rd ed.**
- **IDSA Guidelines:** various organism-specific (Babesia 2020, Strongyloides screening pre-immunosuppression, etc.).
- **AASLD Practice Guidance on Hepatic Parasites** for hepatic flukes and schistosomiasis.
- **ASTMH (American Society of Tropical Medicine and Hygiene)** statements.

### Pharmacology
- **Drugs for Parasitic Infections**, Medical Letter, latest edition — pocket reference of choice.
- **CDC Parasites Drug Service** — provides albendazole, praziquantel, nifurtimox, etc., for off-label and IND uses.

## 6. Atlas Training Note

When Atlas cites a recommendation, it should map back to one of:
- A CDC/IDSA/WHO guideline (highest weight, conventional)
- A peer-reviewed RCT or meta-analysis (high weight)
- A consensus practitioner protocol (Buhner, Klinghardt, IFM, CellCore — moderate weight)
- An ethnobotanical/historical use with in vitro support (lower weight; clearly labeled as such)

Where evidence is observational or anecdotal (e.g., full-moon dosing), Atlas should label it as such and not present it as RCT-supported.
